## B's"d. Sicha of Sunday, Parshas Tetzaveh, 7 Adar, 5713.

– To a group of Yeshiva students, in his holy room –
Unedited

**1.** It is known what is written in Iggeres HaKodesh and explained in the Sichos of the Rebbe Rayatz, that in addition to the obligation of all Bnei Yisrael in the general observance of Torah and Mitzvos, every individual has a specific mitzvah in which they must be "extra careful". This is because that mitzvah is the "gate" through which all matters of avodah ascend from below to above, and through which all influences are drawn down from above to below.

Beyond what has been said—which applies to every Jew, both students who sit in the tent [of Torah] and those involved in business—there is an additional aspect that applies specifically to Yeshiva students:

Students who have been privileged to be brought to study in a Yeshiva—especially a Yeshiva where Nigleh and Chassidus are studied together, and where the Yeshiva administration provides for all their material and spiritual needs—must know that the "gate" through which all their matters pass, both the ascents from below to above and the influences from above to below, in both material and spiritual terms, is the **Yeshiva administration**. Their appointment is—like all matters—by Hashgacha Pratis. As our Sages have said, "Even the supervisor of a water ditch is appointed from Heaven." How much more so regarding the administration of a Yeshiva, and especially an administration appointed by and under the responsibility of the Rebbe Rayatz, the Nasi of Yisrael in general and the Nasi of Chassidim in particular. Certainly, it is through them that all matters pertaining to the Yeshiva students ascend and are drawn down.

**2.** To what does this refer?
It has been reported to me by the Yeshiva administration regarding a weakness in discipline and in the sense of responsibility toward the ways and schedules of the Yeshiva.

Well, what is past is past, and one does not cry over the past, but from here on in, the matter must be corrected—to accept the authority of the administration absolutely, and to observe the ways and schedules of the Yeshiva in the study of Nigleh and Chassidus one hundred percent.

If there are complaints against the administration regarding shortcomings, etc.—aside from the fact that these may be merely imagined shortcomings, even if there are valid complaints, one should present the matters to the administration. But regarding the practical conduct, one must obey the Yeshiva administration. For it is simple that the conduct in an institution of the Rebbe must be in accordance with the will of the Rebbe who appointed the Yeshiva administration.

Do not try to proceed on your own strength, in the way of the individual. Aside from the fact that it is highly doubtful you will succeed in this—and presumably, since the Rebbe Rayatz appointed the Yeshiva administration, it is *impossible* to go in another way—even if one eventually succeeds by going on their own strength in the way of the individual, why waste energy "hacking a door through a thick wall" when there is a ready-made opening through which one can walk on the king’s highway, the path paved for the many?

**3.** All that has been said applies even more to students who have come from Eretz Yisrael. Since, according to Halacha, it is forbidden to leave Eretz Yisrael for outside the land except for the purpose of studying Torah—and it was only on this condition that they received an agreement from the Rebbe to come here—they bear a double responsibility to obey the Yeshiva administration completely. They have no permission to conduct themselves otherwise, even in the slightest, for then they are not fulfilling the Rebbe’s condition and are transgressing a negative commandment, etc.

Likewise, this applies to the other students who have come here from abroad, as well as those who live here—they too must obey the Yeshiva administration completely and be careful not to reach a state of "one who studied and then turned away" (not entirely, Heaven forbid, but even just in the sense of descending from a higher level of holiness to a lower one) "more than all others".

**4.** May Hashem help you to take upon yourselves good resolutions, both regarding the observance of the Yeshiva’s schedules in Nigleh and Chassidus, and regarding your general conduct even outside the Yeshiva’s schedules (in your private room or at home)—in accordance with the Rebbe’s will, that the "yoke" of the Yeshiva students should be present outside the Yeshiva’s schedules in the same way as it is within them.

It is my hope that there will be no need for much contemplation on the above, and certainly no need for great mesiras nefesh, for even with human intellect, the matter is understood simply.

And the main thing—do not postpone the acceptance of these good resolutions for a long time, but do it immediately, this very evening.

Through this, you will succeed in all matters: in the study of Nigleh and the study of Chassidus, and also in your material affairs.

## Purim (Maamar 1), 5713

(Unedited transcript)

"And he was an *uman* (guardian/tutor) to Hadassah, she is Esther." The Gemara states that she was called Hadassah and she was called Esther. The Gemara continues: Why was she called Hadassah? Because of the *tzaddikim* who are called *hadasim* (myrtles), as it is written, "And he stood among the *hadasim*." And why was she called Esther? Because she would conceal (*masteres*) her words, as it is written, "Esther did not tell." There is another opinion in the Gemara there: Why was she called Esther? Because the nations of the world called her *Astahar*. One must understand the significance of these two names, Hadassah and Esther. Furthermore, what relevance is there to what the nations of the world called her?

The matter is as follows: It is known that Esther represents the *Sefirah* of *Malchus*. It possesses two levels: a higher level called Hadassah, and a lower level called Esther. Regarding this, the verse says, "Hadassah, she is Esther"—meaning that even the lower level, which is called Esther, is [in essence] Hadassah. We must understand the explanation of this matter and the practical application for *avodah*. As is known from what the Rebbe Rayatz said, and repeated many times, the entire purpose of *haskalah* (intellectual understanding) is only as a preparation for *avodah*. Every concept that exists in *haskalah* can be brought down into *avodah*, and consequently, one is obligated to bring it down into *avodah*.

2) The matter is: The Gemara states, "Where is Esther hinted at in the Torah? In the verse, 'And I will surely conceal (*haster astir*) My face on that day.'" This means that the "face" (*panim*) from Above is in a state of concealment. Although He has no body nor form of a body, it is known that Above there exists the aspect of *panim* (face), *yamin* (right), *smol* (left), and *achor* (back). "I will conceal My face" means that within the aspect of *panim* Above, there is how it is in revelation and how it is in concealment.

The explanation is that *panim* is the aspect of *Malchus d'Atzilus* (as will be explained), and it has two levels: as it is in *Atzilus*, it is called Hadassah after the *tzaddikim*, because concerning *Atzilus* it is written, "No evil shall dwell with You." Therefore, as it is in *Atzilus*, it is called Hadassah after the *tzaddikim*. Then there is how *Malchus* becomes *Atik* to *Beriah*, at which point it is in concealment, and it is then called Esther, because she conceals her words. Regarding this, it says "Hadassah, she is Esther"—meaning that even the aspect of *Malchus* as it becomes *Atik* to *Beriah* and descends lower and lower, contains all the aspects and matters that exist in *Malchus* as it is in *Atzilus*.

The explanation of why *Malchus d'Atzilus* is called *panim* is that the aspect of *panim* in general is the aspect of the *Atzmus* of *Ohr Ein Sof* which is above the *Seder Hishtalshelus*, while the entirety of the *Hishtalshelus* is the aspect of *chitzoniyus* (externality) and *achor* (back). Specifically, within the levels of the *Hishtalshelus* itself, *Atzilus* is the aspect of *panim*, and *Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah* are *chitzoniyus* and *achor*. As the Ramaz writes, the four levels—*panim, yamin, smol, and achor*—are the four worlds of *ABYA*, and *Atzilus* is the aspect of *panim*. The reason for this is that *Chochma* is called *panim*, and since *Abba Ila'ah* dwells in *Atzilus*, the entirety of *Atzilus* is the aspect of *panim*. Even the lowest level in *Atzilus*, *Malchus d'Atzilus*, is also in the aspect of *panim* while it is in *Atzilus*. However, as it descends and becomes *Atik* to *Beriah*—which is only an illumination of *Malchus* alone, and within that, only its *chitzoniyus*—this is the aspect of concealment. This is the meaning of "And I will surely conceal (*haster astir*) My face (*panim*) on that day"—that *Malchus*, which is the aspect of *panim*, is concealed (*haster astir*) in *Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah* ("on that day").

3) Another reason why *Malchus* is called *panim* is that it is the *pnimiyus* (inner dimension) of the souls of Israel, as it is written, "And this is *lifnim* (inward/before) in Israel"—*zot* is the aspect of *Malchus*, and it is *lifnim* in Israel, meaning the *pnimiyus* of the souls of Israel. The matter is as follows: It is stated in *Etz Chaim* that there is a very small spark which is the aspect of *Elokus* (the lowest level in the Creator), and this spark is clothed within the power of a created spark called *yechidah*. This is also understood from the Midrash, which says that five names are called for it: *nefesh, ru'ach, neshamah, chayah, yechidah*—meaning that even *yechidah* is only a name, and the essence of the *neshamah* is higher even than the level of *yechidah*. For the level of *yechidah* is a created spark, while the essence of the *neshamah* is the Creator-spark, and it is the *pnimiyus* of the *neshamah*. This is the aspect of *Malchus* which is called *panim*, the *pnimiyus* of the souls of Israel.

*Nefesh* is the aspect of *Asiyah*, *ru'ach* is *Yetzirah*, *neshamah* is *Beriah*, *chayah* is *Atzilus*, and *yechidah* is *Keter*, meaning the aspect of *Arich*. The Creator-spark that is clothed in the created spark is the aspect of *Atik*. Similarly, in *Adam Kadmon*, which is the *machshavah kedumah* (primordial thought) regarding the entire *Hishtalshelus*, there are *levushim* (garments) and *Sefiros*. The *levushim* are the quarry of the souls, and the *Sefiros* are the quarry of the *Sefiros*. The level of *yechidah*, which is the created spark, is from the quarry of the souls, but the Creator-spark (which is clothed in the created spark) is from the quarry of the *Sefiros*, in which it is appropriate to say "the lowest level in the Creator." Although the essence and truth of the Creator is higher than the *Sefiros*—which are lights in vessels that were found and emanated from *Ohr Ein Sof*—nevertheless, the *Sefiros* are *Elokus* in truth, for "He, His life, and His essence are one." This means that even the *levushim* of *Atzilus* are not separate and are *Elokus*, and certainly the *levushim* of *Adam Kadmon* are *Elokus*. All this is regarding the *levushim* which are merely an extension; how much more so the *Sefiros* of *Atzilus*, and especially the *Sefiros* of *Adam Kadmon*, are *Elokus* in truth.

In truth, the Creator-spark is higher even than the quarry of the *Sefiros*, for the Creator-spark is the aspect of *Atik* as mentioned above. It is the same in *Adam Kadmon*, where the Creator-spark is the aspect of *Atik d'Adam Kadmon*, as it is known that there is an aspect of *Atik* above the *Gulgolta* of *Adam Kadmon*, which is the aspect of *Malchus d'Ein Sof*. Just as *Malchus d'Atzilus* becomes *Atik* to *Beriah*, so too the *Malchus d'Atzilus* in general, which is the aspect of *Malchus d'Ein Sof*, becomes *Atik* to the *Beriah* in general, which is *Adam Kadmon*. The Creator-spark, which is the *pnimiyus* of the souls of Israel, is the aspect of *Malchus d'Ein Sof*, the *Atik* of *Adam Kadmon*. Because of this, even as *Malchus* comes down in the *Seder Hishtalshelus* until *Malchus d'Atzilus*, *Malchus* is called *panim*.

4) The two aforementioned levels in the aspect of *Malchus* called *panim*—as it is in revelation in *Atzilus*, and as it comes in concealment to be *Atik* to *Beriah*—exist also in the entirety of the souls of Israel ("And this is *lifnim* in Israel") and in every individual soul. Every soul has two aspects: the aspect of *Yisrael* (*li rosh* - "to me is the head") and the aspect of *Yaakov* (*yud-akev* - "heel"), which are the essence of the soul and the illumination of the soul. It is known that not the entire soul is clothed in the body; the inner powers that are clothed in the vessels are not the essence of the soul. The essence of the soul cannot be clothed in the body, for even an angel stands in a third of the world, and if so, the soul, which is higher than an angel, certainly cannot be clothed in the body. What is clothed in the body is only the illumination of the soul alone—the aspect of *Yaakov*—while the essence of the soul is the aspect of *Yisrael*. Due to these two levels in the soul, there are differences in the manner of *avodah*.

The matter is: In the *avodah* of *ahavah* (love), for "there is no service like the service of love," it is written, "And you shall love... with all your heart, and with all your soul, and with all your might." The love of "with all your heart" is as it is written, "To love Hashem your G-d... for He is your life." This means that when one contemplates that the Divine life-force enlivens and creates the world at every moment and every hour, and he (the person contemplating) is within it—meaning that *Elokus* is the life of the world and the life of his soul, and it is life and good, as it is written, "See, I have set before you life and good, and death and evil"—for spirituality is life and good, and physicality is death and evil because it has no existence of its own at all—this contemplation leads one to "choose life," that his desire and will should be in *Elokus*. This is the meaning of "To love Hashem your G-d, for He is your life," which is the love of "with all your heart." This love is limited, for since it comes from the contemplation of the life-force of the worlds in general and the person in particular, and this life-force is in a state of limitation, the love is also limited.

Higher than this is the love of "with all your soul," regarding which it is said, "even if He takes your soul." This love comes through the contemplation that the entire life-force of the worlds is only an illumination. He feels the life-force of the worlds and contemplates that this life-force is only an illumination, for the worlds were created with a single thought, and even for a person, a single thought does not take up space relative to the total power of thought, and certainly relative to his soul and the essence of his soul. And certainly Above, a single thought is only an illumination. From this contemplation comes the love of "with all your soul." However, in truth, this love is also bound by limitation, for since it comes from the contemplation that the life-force of the worlds is only an illumination—meaning he feels the life-force of the worlds and negates it as being only an illumination—it is also bound by limitation. These two levels of love—the limited love of "with all your heart" and the love of "with all your soul" which is at least connected with limitation—are from the aspect of *Yaakov*, which is only the illumination of the soul alone.

But the ultimate goal of *avodah* is to reach the level of love of "with all your might" (*me'odecha*), which is completely without limit. For even though it is "your *me'od*" (your very own), it is nevertheless completely above limitation. This love comes from the contemplation of the wondrousness of *Ohr Ein Sof*, how it is wondrous and removed from the *Seder Hishtalshelus*, and even more so, how it is wondrous from its own perspective. Through this, one comes to the love of "with all your might." Since it comes from the contemplation of *Ohr Ein Sof* as it is wondrous and exalted from its own perspective, the love itself is also exalted from its own perspective—meaning it is exalted above all its matters and limitations, even by way of negation. This love is from the aspect of *Yisrael*, the aspect of the *makifim* (encompassing lights) of the soul, the *pnimiyus* and the essence of the soul.

5) But in the time of *galus* (exile), it is written, "And I will surely conceal My face," meaning the love of "with all your might," which is from the aspect of *Yisrael* (*pnimiyus* of the soul, *panim*), is in concealment. The order of *avodah* is that in order to reach the love of "with all your might," one must have the prior *avodah* based on *ta'am v'da'as* (reason and knowledge), which is the love of "with all your heart" and "with all your soul." Only afterwards is one given, from Above as a gift, the love of "with all your might." Therefore, when the *avodah* based on *ta'am v'da'as* is missing, one also lacks the love of "with all your might." Especially when one is immersed in matters of desire and craving, the love is concealed and submerged in his desires. Although this love comes from Above as a *makif*, the fact that the love is a *makif* is precisely because the love is in a state of concealment from its own perspective. But through being immersed in matters of desire, it is not just that the love is in concealment from its own perspective, but it is further concealed within his desires. This is the general state of the time of *galus*, which is the departure from *Eretz Yisrael*—the aspect of *li rosh*—and this is the concept of *chutz la'aretz* (outside the land), meaning outside the aspect of *li rosh*. Regarding this, it is stated in the *Zohar*, "I am asleep in *galus*," for the time of *galus* is a state of sleep, where contemplation and *hasagah* (grasp) are lacking, and therefore the love of "with all your might," which is from the aspect of *Yisrael*, is also in a state of concealment.

However, even though "I am asleep in *galus*," nevertheless, "my heart is awake," for my heart, which is the *pnimiyus* of the life-force, is awake even in the time of *galus*. Although it was explained that in the time of *galus* there is "I will surely conceal My face," where *panim* (the *pnimiyus*) is in concealment, this does not contradict "my heart is awake." The matter is that there is the light of the essence and the power of the essence (the light of the soul and the power of the soul). The matter of love is light, for even the love of "with all your might," being love, is a matter of light, especially since it comes from the self-recognition of the essence of the soul recognizing the *Atzmus* of *Ein Sof*. Similar to what is stated, "At three years old, Avraham recognized his Creator." Since the love comes from recognition, it is an aspect of light. Therefore, in the time of *galus*, when this light is absent, the love is in concealment, and regarding this it is said, "I am asleep in *galus*." But nevertheless, "my heart is awake," because the power of the essence exists even in the time of *galus*, and this is the matter of *mesiras nefesh* (self-sacrifice). *Mesiras nefesh* is a matter of power. Proof of this is that even in the simplest of the simple, who lacks the aspect of light, there is also the power of *mesiras nefesh*, because *mesiras nefesh* is a matter of power. The power of *mesiras nefesh* is in revelation even in the time of *galus*; on the contrary, in the time of *galus* it is in greater revelation than in the time of the *Beis Hamikdash*. As our Rebbe the Alter Rebbe said in the name of the Baal Shem Tov on the verse "So I have beheld You in the Sanctuary," which King David said in the name of the *Knesses Yisrael*: At the time when he was in a "dry and weary land" where "my soul thirsts for You," then he said "So I have beheld You in the Sanctuary"—would that I could behold You in the Sanctuary. Therefore, the matter of *mesiras nefesh* is in greater revelation in the time of *galus* than in the time of the *Beis Hamikdash*. This is why the trials in the time of *galus* are greater than in the time of the *Beis Hamikdash*, and since "the Holy One, blessed be He, does not deal tyrannically with His creatures," it must be that in the time of *galus* greater powers were granted—this is the power of *mesiras nefesh*, which is in greater revelation in the time of *galus*. This is true even in simple people who do not have the aspect of light; it is precisely in them that the power of *mesiras nefesh* is greatest.

Through the power of *mesiras nefesh*, which is from the power of the soul that exists in revelation in the time of *galus*, the light of the soul is also revealed—which is the aspect of recognition and the love of "with all your might" mentioned above. The matter is that it is stated in the Midrash, "There is no hour as difficult as that hour in which it was written, 'And I will surely conceal My face,' and together with this, from that hour (precisely) I wait for Him," as it is written, "And I will wait for Hashem who conceals His face." The term "I will wait" (*chikeisi*)—and not just a general term of hope—is as the saying, "For those who labor in the matters of wisdom, He will do for those who wait for Him." This revelation is higher than the revelation of *Gan Eden* and higher than the *Hishtalshelus*. This revelation is achieved precisely through *mesiras nefesh* and the *avodah* of trials in the time of *galus*. As is known, the difference between *birurim* (refinements) and trials is that the *avodah* of *birurim* is to refine the Divine spark clothed in a physical object, and the *avodah* is through closeness. This is not the case in trials, where the spark is in concealment. Although even in trials there is a Divine spark—which is why it has existence—nevertheless, the spark is extinguished within it, like a spark in a forbidden object. Therefore, the *avodah* is not in the object, but in the person, that he should stand in the trial. Through the *avodah* of trials and *mesiras nefesh* in the time of *galus*, which is from the power of the essence, the light of the essence is also revealed—which is the love of "with all your might" that comes from the self-recognition of the essence of the soul in the *Atzmus* of *Ein Sof*.

6) This is "And he was an *uman* to Hadassah, she is Esther"—that even the aspect of Esther, which is in the aspect of "I will surely conceal My face," is nevertheless "Hadassah, she is Esther," because precisely from the concealment comes "I will wait for Hashem who conceals His face." This means that even in the aspect of *Yaakov*, which is the soul clothed in the body, there are all the aspects of *Yisrael*, which is the essence of the soul. Just as in the *Hishtalshelus*, where the aspect of *Malchus* as it becomes *Atik* to *Beriah* contains all the aspects as it is in *Atzilus*, so too in the soul clothed in the body, it contains all the aspects that exist in the essence of the soul. This is the concept of "Hadassah, she is Esther." This matter is revealed through *mesiras nefesh* (as mentioned above), which is the concept of Hadassah (she is Esther), as it is stated in *Maseches Sanhedrin* on the verse "And he stood among the *hadasim*," that it refers to Chananyah, Mishael, and Azaryah, in whom the matter of *mesiras nefesh* was in actuality. This is not like the *mesiras nefesh* in potential that existed at the time of the Giving of the Torah, where at every word their souls departed—that *mesiras nefesh* was from Above—but rather *mesiras nefesh* in actuality in the time of *galus*, which is through *avodah* and toil precisely. Therefore, precisely in the days of Purim (in the time of *galus*) they fulfilled what they had begun to do at the time of the Giving of the Torah—meaning that through *mesiras nefesh* is the revelation of the essence of the soul.

However, the drawing down of the power to the soul is through the Torah. This is what is meant by "And he was an *uman* (tutor) to Hadassah," for *uman* refers to the Torah, as it is written, "And I was with Him as an *amon* (tutor/craftsman)," and the Torah says, "I was the craftsman's tool of the Holy One, blessed be He." More specifically, as it is stated in the Midrash on the verse "And I was with Him as an *amon*": *amon* is a pedagogue, *amon* is hidden, *amon* is covered. *Amon* covered refers to the *mitzvos*, which are the aspect of *levushim*, and *amon* pedagogue and *amon* hidden refer to the revealed Torah and the hidden Torah. This is the concept of giving strength and might to the souls of Israel through the Torah, which is "strength and wisdom"—strength for the *nefesh habehamis* and wisdom for the *nefesh elokis*—which weakens the power of the *nefesh habehamis* that conceals the holiness, and gives strength and might in the *avodah* of the *nefesh elokis* to stand with powerful strength, and even to the power of *mesiras nefesh*. Through this, the light of the essence is also revealed, which is the love of "with all your might" that comes from the essence of the soul recognizing the *Atzmus* of *Ein Sof*, as the saying goes, "Embraced and cleaved to You, the *yechidah* to unite You."

## Purim (Maamar II) 5713

**a)** "Therefore they called these days Purim, after the *pur* (lot)." According to the well-known principle that the name of a thing indicates its essence, it is understood that the fact that these days are called Purim after the *pur* is because the essence of the Yom Tov of Purim is the *goral* (lot)—for *pur* is the *goral*.

One must understand (as is noted in *Torah Or*, in the *maamar* beginning with the word *Zeh*), that on the face of it, this was not the essence of the miracle. Furthermore, the *goral* that Haman cast (not only was it not the miracle, but on the contrary) was part of the decree—as the *Megillah* states, "He cast *pur*, that is the *goral*, to discomfit them and to destroy them." The fact that these days are called Purim after the *pur* implies that this (*the goral*) is the essence of the miracle of Purim, to the point that the miracle is named after it.

Seemingly, one could explain this based on what is stated in the *Gemara*: when the *pur* fell in the month of Adar, Haman rejoiced greatly, saying, "The *pur* has fallen for me in the month in which Moshe died," not knowing that on the seventh of Adar he died and on the seventh of Adar he was born. Based on this, one could say that these days are named after the *pur* because the miracle of Purim was that the *pur* fell in the month of Adar, the month in which Moshe, the redeemer of Yisrael, was born.

However, this explanation is itself difficult, because the fact that the Yom Tov is called Purim after the *pur* implies that the connection of the miracle to the *pur* is (not only to the fact that the *pur* fell in the month of Adar, but also) to the matter of the *pur* itself. On the contrary, the essence of the miracle was from the aspect of the *pur*, which is the *goral*. For *goral* is a most lofty matter, from which the miracle is drawn. The fact that the *goral* fell on the month of Adar, in which the redeemer of Yisrael was born, is (like all the matters that occurred in the miracle of Purim) a result of its essential nature: Purim is named after the *pur*.

**b)** This can be understood based on what is explained in *Torah Or* that Purim and Yom Kippur are related to one another. The interpretation of *Yom HaKippurim* is "a day like Purim" (*k'Purim*)—and even more than that, the fact that Yom Kippur is *k'Purim* (with the *kaf* of comparison only) proves that Purim is loftier than Yom Kippur. The connection between Yom Kippur and Purim is that in both of them, the matter of the *goral* is present. On Yom Kippur, they cast lots on the two goats, and Purim is also named after the *pur*, which is the *goral*.

There is another aspect to the connection between Purim and Yom Kippur: both are above the Name *Havayah*. On Yom Kippur it is written, "Before *Havayah* you shall be purified"—above *Havayah*. Regarding Purim, it is also known that the reason the Name *Havayah* is not mentioned in the entire *Megillah* of Esther is because on Purim there is a revelation of the *Ohr Ein Sof* that is above *Havayah*.

The explanation of the connection between these two matters in the comparison of Yom Kippur and Purim (that in both there is the matter of the *goral*, and both are above the Name *Havayah*) can be understood by prefacing that the matter of the *goral* is above intellect. For a *goral* is (generally) used for two equal things (like the two goats of Yom Kippur, which were equal in appearance, height, and value). When one of them is better, there is no place for a *goral*; it is specifically when two things are equal, since from the perspective of intellect it is impossible to decide which to choose, that one relies on the *goral* which is above intellect. It is similar Above: the *goral* is above *hishtalshelus* (for *Chochma* is the beginning of *hishtalshelus*, and the *goral*, which is above *Chochma*, is above *hishtalshelus*). Based on this, the comparison between Purim and Yom Kippur—that in both there is the matter of the *goral*—is a revelation of the *Ohr Ein Sof* that is above *hishtalshelus*. This is the connection between the two matters in the comparison of Yom Kippur and Purim: that in both there is the matter of the *goral*, and both are above *Havayah*, because *Havayah* is *hishtalshelus* (*Yud* is *Chochma*, *Heh* is *Binah*, etc.), and the fact that Yom Kippur and Purim are above *Havayah* means that the revelation of Yom Kippur and Purim is above *hishtalshelus*—the matter of the *goral*.

**c)** It is known regarding the greatness of the *goral* that it is above (not only intellect, but) also the *ratzon* (will) that is above intellect. For when a person casts a *goral*, he decides that as the *goral* falls, so shall be his *ratzon*. This proves that the *goral* reaches a level that is above even the *ratzon*, which is why the *ratzon* is according to the *goral*. It is similar Above: the matter of the *goral* is in the "Master of the Will" (*Baal HaRatzon*) who is above *ratzon*. Based on this, it can be said that the connection between the two matters in the comparison of Yom Kippur and Purim (that in both there is the matter of the *goral* and both are above *Havayah*) is because the beginning of the Name *Havayah* is the *kotz* (thorn) atop the *Yud*, which hints at the *ratzon* that is above *Chochma*. The fact that Yom Kippur and Purim are above *Havayah* (above even the *kotz* atop the *Yud*) is because on Yom Kippur and Purim there is an *hemshech* (drawing down) and revelation of the level of the "Master of the Will" (who is above *ratzon*)—the matter of the *goral*.

**d)** Based on this, one can explain the connection between the two interpretations of *Yom HaKippurim*—that on this day all undesirable matters are atoned for (*kippurim* in its literal sense), and that this day is "like Purim" (the comparison of Yom Kippur to Purim is that on Yom Kippur they cast a *goral*, like Purim which is named after the *pur*, the *goral*)—because the perfection of atonement for matters done contrary to the *ratzon* of *Torah* and *mitzvos* is when the *hemshech* of atonement is from the *Ohr Ein Sof*, the "Master of the Will" (who is above the *ratzon* of *Torah* and *mitzvos*). This is the connection between the two interpretations of *Yom HaKippurim*: the fact that Yom Kippur is "like Purim" after the *pur*, which is the *goral*, means that on Yom Kippur, too, there is the *hemshech* from the "Master of the Will" (similar to the *hemshech* of Purim). This is the reason and explanation for why it is *Yom HaKippurim* in the simple sense, on which undesirable matters are atoned for. This is why the atonement of Purim is loftier than the atonement of Yom Kippur: Yom Kippur only atones with *teshuvah*, whereas the atonement that occurred for all the Jews in the days of Purim is a day of joy, because the essence of the matter of the *goral* (the *hemshech* of the level of the "Master of the Will") is in the days of Purim, for the days themselves are called Purim after the *pur*. Therefore, the atonement that occurred in the days of Purim has no limitations, while *Yom HaKippurim* is *k'Purim*—with the *kaf* of comparison only—and Yom Kippur only atones with *teshuvah*.

**e)** One must understand: according to the above, that through the *pur*, which is the *goral*, there is an *hemshech* of the *Ohr Ein Sof*, the "Master of the Will," through which all undesirable matters were atoned for, what was the reasoning of Haman the wicked (who prosecuted the Jews) in casting the *pur*, the *goral*? Also, one must understand why it says, "Therefore they called these days Purim after *the* *pur*," for *the* *pur* (with the definite article) refers to the *pur* mentioned previously—the *pur* that Haman cast. But the *pur* that Haman cast was an undesirable matter; why are these days called Purim after *this* *pur*?

The point of the explanation is that from the perspective of the level of *hishtalshelus*, since in this level the deeds of the lower beings carry weight, the primary influence is to Yisrael, because Yisrael fulfill *Torah* and *mitzvos*, and even those who are not *tzaddikim* are full of *mitzvos* like a pomegranate. As it is written, "Look down from Your holy habitation, from the heavens, and bless Your people, Yisrael," for from the perspective of the level of *shamayim* (heavens—*shamayim* is *shem mayim*, and *mayim* is *Chochma*, the beginning of *hishtalshelus*), blessings are bestowed upon Yisrael. Since Haman the wicked also knew that after all the prosecutions he brought against Yisrael, there is no comparison between him and Yisrael (which is why, from the perspective of the level of *hishtalshelus*, it is impossible for Haman the *wicked* to defeat, *chas v'shalom*, Yisrael, for even the least among them are full of *mitzvos* like a pomegranate), he therefore cast *pur*, the *goral*. For since in the level that is above *hishtalshelus* (*goral*), the deeds of the lower beings carry no weight, and all are equal there—"as the darkness, so is the light"—he thought that from the perspective of the level of *goral* which is above *hishtalshelus*, there is a place where he could defeat, *chas v'shalom*, Yisrael.

**f)** This can be connected with what is explained elsewhere regarding Achashverosh, that Achashverosh is *achash* and *rosh*. *Chash* is from the language of silence (as it is stated in the *Gemara*, "What is *chashmal*? Times of silence and times of speaking"), and the silence of *chash* is above speech, similar to the saying, "If a word is worth a *sela*, silence is worth two." The root of speech is in *Chochma*, and silence is in *Keter*, which is above *Chochma*, as our Sages said, "A fence for wisdom is silence," for the fence for wisdom (a fence in the sense of a boundary and *makif*) is the *Keter* that is above *Chochma*. This can be connected with what is stated in the *Midrash* that Achashverosh is the Holy One, Blessed be He, who is the "end and beginning" of all, for *Keter* is (generally) the *Ohr HaSovev* (surrounding light) that is above *hishtalshelus*, for the *Ohr HaSovev* includes the end and the beginning equally—"His end and His beginning."

One must understand: the *Gemara* states that Achashverosh is named for the fact that the faces of Yisrael were blackened in his days. How does this fit with the interpretation that Achashverosh is He who is the "end and beginning" of all? The matter is that this is precisely the reason. Since from the perspective of the *Sovev* that is above *hishtalshelus*, light and darkness are equal before Him (as in section e), therefore, from the level of silence (*chash* of Achashverosh) that is above speech, there was drawn down (through many *tzimtzumim*, etc.) the silence that is below speech, where *Elokus* is in concealment (concealment in the simple sense, below revelation), even to the silence of "like a sheep before its shearers is dumb." Not only is *Elokus* not shining in revelation, but the *kelipah* also conceals the *Elokus*—the *kelipas nogah* which is called *chash*, until it connects with *gashmius* (materiality), *rosh*.

**g)** It can be said that the reason Haman cast a *pur* in order to reach the level of the *goral* that is above *hishtalshelus*—even though then (in the days of Achashverosh, even before he cast the *pur*) the conduct was from the perspective of above *hishtalshelus*—is because the level of the *goral* is even higher than the level of Achashverosh, He who is the "end and beginning" of all. The matter is that from the very fact that He is called Achashverosh, He who is the "end and beginning" of all, it is proven that even regarding Him there are the matters of "end and beginning," only that they are equal regarding Him. (Note what is written: "He made a feast for all his princes and servants, and afterwards for all the people who were found in Shushan," and it is explained in *Likutei Torah* that the revelation that was for all the people was lower in level than the revelation that was for his princes and servants. Furthermore, even in the revelation that was for all the people there were many differences in levels: "fitting for the courtyard to the courtyard, fitting for the garden to the garden, fitting for the pavilion to the pavilion." It can be said that from the perspective of the level of *hishtalshelus*, the revelation is only for princes and servants, and the fact that there was revelation also for all the people (even for those fitting only for the courtyard) is because the revelation was from the level of Achashverosh, He who is the "end and beginning" of all. Nevertheless, the revelation to the people was lower than the revelation to the princes and servants (and even in the revelation to the people there were several levels), because the fact that He is the "end and beginning" of all means that *regarding Him* the end and beginning are equal, but regarding His revelation in the worlds, in the upper worlds (beginning) it is more revealed than in the lower worlds (end)).

This is not the case with the level of the *goral*, where there is no "beginning and end" from the outset, as is understood from the fact that the *goral* is (generally) for two *equal* things (as in section b). Just as it is regarding the matters of "beginning and end" (above and below), so it is regarding *Kedushah* and *Sitra Achra*. From the level of Achashverosh, there is a difference between *Kedushah* and *Sitra Achra*, only that from the perspective of His loftiness this does not carry weight (so to speak), and therefore it is possible for there to be nourishment also for the *Sitra Achra* (*chash* of *kelipas nogah* and *rosh* of *gashmius*), until there is a strengthening of the *Sitra Achra* over *Kedushah*—"the faces of Yisrael were blackened" (one could say it is similar to "a lizard can be caught with the hands, yet it is in the palaces of kings"—even though it dirties the palace, because of the king's loftiness he does not pay attention to be strict about expelling it), but not to destroy them, *chas v'shalom*. When Haman sought to destroy all the Jews, *chas v'shalom*, he cast *pur*, the *goral*, for since in the level of the *goral* there is no difference from the outset between *Kedushah* and *Sitra Achra* (especially according to what is explained above in sections c and d, that the *goral* is not only above *hishtalshelus* but also above the *ratzon* of *Torah* and *mitzvos*), he thought that through this he would be able, *chas v'shalom*, to carry out his scheme.

**h)** Just as the *goral* is above intellect and also above *ratzon*, so it is regarding *bechirah* (choice), which is above intellect and also above *ratzon*. When one's intellect dictates that this thing is worthy of being chosen, and also when one desires something (a *ratzon* that is above intellect), one is compelled in this and it is not free choice. The true matter of *bechirah* is when one's choice is not from intellect and not from *ratzon*, but rather one chooses in his free choice. The difference between *goral* and *bechirah* is that when a person casts a *goral* and decides that as the *goral* falls, so shall be his *ratzon*, his *ratzon* is not from the person but because the *goral* fell that way, whereas *bechirah* is that *the person* is the one who chooses. From this it is understood that *bechirah* is above *goral*. The *goral* is the level in the soul that is *defined and compelled* by the fact that the two things are equal to it, whereas the matter of *bechirah* is that *it is not* defined or compelled by any things, including things that are equal to it, and it is in its power to choose what it desires.

It can be said that in *bechirah* there are two matters. The level of the soul that is above intellect, to which the two things are equal—the level of the *goral*—and the fact that it chooses as it desires (even though the two things are equal to it) comes from the essence of the soul. Through *bechirah*, the *hemshech* from the essence to the level of the *goral* (the level to which the two things are equal), the *goral* itself (even though from its own perspective it could be in either way) is according to the *bechirah*. This is similar to what is written, "You support my *goral*," that the Holy One, Blessed be He, supports the *goral* so that it will be according to the choice of the *Atzmus* (the Essence). This is what is written, "He shall choose our inheritance for us, the pride of Yaakov, etc.," for regarding Yaakov it is written, "Yaakov is the cord of His inheritance," and Onkelos translates this as "Yaakov is the portion of His inheritance," where "portion" is *goral*. The matter of "He shall choose... the pride of Yaakov" is that even in the level of the *goral* that is above *hishtalshelus*, where all are equal, the choice is specifically for Yisrael.

**i)** This is "Therefore they called these days Purim after the *pur*," for the falling of the *goral* Above was according to the intent and choice of the *Atzmus*. Even though "I loved Yaakov and I hated Esav," through the *mesiras nefesh* of Yisrael throughout the year (for the matter of *mesiras nefesh* is from the essence of the soul, meaning from the choice of the essence of the soul in the *Atzmus*), through this, the choice of the *Atzmus* in Yisrael is drawn down and revealed. Through this, the *goral* Above was also that for the Jews there would be "light, and joy, and gladness, and honor," and "Haman and his sons they shall hang on the tree." The verse emphasizes *the* *pur* with the definite article, referring to the *pur* that Haman cast (as in section e), because through the miracle of Purim it was revealed that even the *pur* that Haman cast was from the outset for the sake of the revelation of the choice of the *Atzmus* in Yisrael. This is why the *pur* fell in the month of Adar, the month in which the redeemer of Yisrael was born, for the falling of the *pur* was from the outset so that it would be revealed that even the *pur* itself, although it has room for both sides (and furthermore, in the external sense it appeared that the *pur* was for the benefit of Haman, to the point that there was a decree), the inner dimension of the *pur* was from the outset according to the choice of the *Atzmus*: "I loved Yaakov and I hated Esav."

It is similar regarding "the tree that he prepared for himself"—for himself. The reason he made a tree fifty cubits high is because he wanted to reach the fiftieth gate that is above *hishtalshelus*, where "as the darkness, so is the light." The miracle of Purim was that even from the level that is above *hishtalshelus* (*goral*, the fiftieth gate), they hung Haman on the tree, and then it was revealed that even the fact that he prepared the tree from the outset was for him, for himself.

This is the matter of Purim: that in the days of Purim every year, it is drawn down that even in the level of the *pur* that is above *hishtalshelus* there shall be "He shall choose our inheritance for us, the pride of Yaakov, etc." and "I will surely blot out the memory of Amalek, etc.," in the complete Redemption, very soon.

## Sicha of the Festival of Purim, 5713

**A.** The Rebbe washed his hands for the meal. Afterward, he instructed that a *nigun* be played and recited the *maamar* beginning with the words, "And he was the foster-father of Hadassah."

***

**B.** It was discussed earlier (in the *maamar*) that Purim is on a higher level than *Matan Torah*. This is understood from the verse, "The Jews undertook and accepted what they had begun to do"—meaning that at *Matan Torah* there was only a beginning ("had begun"), whereas on Purim there was a "complete acceptance". From this, it is understood that *Matan Torah* and Purim are related to one another, as their essence is one, except that Purim is on a higher level.

Among the aspects of this:
*Matan Torah* and Purim both involve *mesiras nefesh*. At *Matan Torah*—as the Sages say, "With every single utterance, their souls departed." On Purim—even though the decree was "to destroy, to kill, and to annihilate all the Jews, from young to old, children and women", they nevertheless sacrificed themselves for the sanctification of Hashem’s name (for if they had wished to convert, Haman would not have done anything to them, as he decreed only against the Jews; yet they surrendered themselves to death for the entire year), and no extraneous thought entered their minds, *chas v’shalom*.

However, the *mesiras nefesh* of *Matan Torah* does not reach the *mesiras nefesh* of Purim, because:
The *mesiras nefesh* of *Matan Torah* was: (a) *mesiras nefesh* in potential, meaning, from the side of the *neshamah*—"their souls departed"; (b) the arousal of this *mesiras nefesh* came from the side of the revelations from above—"with every single utterance," and likewise the general concept of "He suspended the mountain over them like a cask", which is the revelation of the supreme love.

In contrast, the *mesiras nefesh* of Purim was: (a) *mesiras nefesh* in actuality, from the side of the body (for the decree "to destroy, etc." was against the bodies, and consequently, the fact that no extraneous thought entered their minds was a matter of *mesiras nefesh* in actuality, from the side of the body); (b) the arousal of this *mesiras nefesh* came (not from the side of revelations from above, but) from the side of the *avodah* and exertion of the Jewish people through their own strength.

**C.** From this, it is also understood regarding the *avodah* of Purim in every single year, as it is written, "And these days are remembered and kept in every generation":
The Rebbe Rayatz says in one of his *sichos* that even now, Haman’s thought to destroy, kill, and annihilate exists, *chas v’shalom*. Consequently, the *avodah* of Purim must also exist now—the matter of *mesiras nefesh* in actuality, and in a manner of *avodah* through one’s own strength. That is, one should not wait for an arousal from above, but always stand in a state and condition of being ready to sacrifice one’s soul in actuality.

This means that in addition to the *mesiras nefesh* of every day in the *avodah* of *Krias Shema*, which is emphasized in a higher way when saying "Shema Yisrael... Hashem Echad" on Yom Kippur—as explained in *Chassidus* that even the lowliest of the low stands in a movement of *mesiras nefesh* when saying *Shema Yisrael* on Yom Kippur—all of which is *mesiras nefesh* in potential and from the side of revelations from above—there must also be the *avodah* that is activated during the days of Purim, which is the matter of *mesiras nefesh* in actuality, and from the side of *avodah* through one’s own strength.

**D.** Now, one of the ways in which the mode of *avodah* of *mesiras nefesh* is expressed is when the *avodah* is not in a set order and sequence.
For example—regarding the study of the teachings of *Chassidus*. There are those who claim that one must first fill one’s belly with *Shas* and *Poskim*, and only afterward study *Chassidus* as well. They support this with the ruling of the *Shulchan Aruch* in the laws of *Talmud Torah*.

The answer to this is that the *avodah* must be without order and sequence, for just as the corruption was without order and sequence, so too the rectification must be without order and sequence. This is similar to what the Rambam writes : that although "the straight path is the middle way in every character trait... which is distant from both extremes," nevertheless, "if one was distant toward one extreme, he should distance himself to the other extreme and conduct himself in it for a long time until he returns to the good path, which is the middle way."

Regarding our matter: since the *avodah* must be without order and sequence, one should not wait to study the teachings of *Chassidus* until after filling one’s belly with *Shas* and *Poskim*; rather, one must study the teachings of *Chassidus* side-by-side with the study of *Shas* and *Poskim*.

**E.** One should add another point regarding this—in connection and relevance to the matters of Purim:
It is stated in the *Gemara*, "If Purim falls on Erev Shabbos, Rav says we advance [the reading of] *Parshas Zachor* [to the Shabbos before], and Shmuel says we delay [it, reading it on the Shabbos after Purim]. Rav says we advance, so that the *asiyah* [the performance of the mitzvos of Purim] does not precede the *zechirah* [the remembrance of *Parshas Zachor*]... And Shmuel says we delay, arguing that since there are walled cities that celebrate on the fifteenth, *asiyah* and *zechirah* come together... If it falls on Shabbos itself, Rav Huna says everyone agrees we do not advance... and Rav Nachman says it remains a dispute."

This matter depends on the well-known inquiry regarding things that must be done one after the other—is it effective if they are done simultaneously? That is: is the obligation that this matter must come *after* that matter specifically, and consequently, if they come simultaneously, it is not effective? Or is "one *after* the other" not strictly required, and the main restriction is that the later should not be before the earlier, and consequently, if they come simultaneously, it is perfectly fine?

Similarly regarding *zechirah* and *asiyah* ("remembered and kept")—is there a necessity for the *zechirah* to precede specifically, or is the main restriction that "the *asiyah* should not precede the *zechirah*," but if the *asiyah* does not precede the *zechirah*, and they come together, it is perfectly fine?

Regarding our matter: according to the opinion that "after this" is not strictly required, and the main restriction is that the later should not be before the earlier—one can perfectly well do both together: fill one’s belly with *Shas* and *Poskim*, and at the same time engage in the study and *avodah* of *Chassidus*.

***

**F.** Regarding the reading of the *Megillah*—it is stated in the *Mishnah* : "From where does a person read the *Megillah* and fulfill his obligation? Rabbi Meir says the whole thing, Rabbi Yehudah says from 'Ish Yehudi,' Rabbi Yosi says from 'After these things'." And in the *Gemara* : "They all derived it from one verse, 'And Queen Esther and Mordechai the Jew wrote all the *tokef* [power]'... He who says the whole thing [refers to] the power of Achashverosh... and he who says from 'Ish Yehudi' [refers to] the power of Mordechai, etc."

And since the *halachah* is that one is obligated to read the entire *Megillah*, it is proven that one must mention "the power of Achashverosh."
Seemingly, one must understand:
It is understandable regarding "the power of Mordechai"—this is a matter connected with the power of the eternity of the Jewish people. That is, the *neshamah* is eternal, and consequently, the body—with which the *neshamah* is connected, and through which it performs its *avodah* in fulfilling the *mitzvos* (as explained in *Tanya* that it is impossible for the *neshamah* to fulfill practical *mitzvos* except by being clothed in a body)—is also eternal. This is the concept of the resurrection of the dead, that the physical body (in which and through which the *neshamah* performed its *avodah* throughout the person’s life) will rise to life.
But what is the content of the matter that we must mention "the power of Achashverosh"?

**G.** This will be understood by first noting the two interpretations of "Achashverosh":
In *Meorei Ohr* it is stated (in the name of the *Midrash*) that "Achashverosh" refers to the Holy One, Blessed be He, "whose is the end and the beginning."
And in the *Midrash* it is stated, "Every place in this *Megillah* where it says 'to King Achashverosh,' the verse speaks of King Achashverosh (literally, the king of Media and Persia), and every place where it says 'to the king' simply, it serves both the Holy and the profane," meaning that it refers (sometimes) to the Holy One, Blessed be He, "the King of the world".
These two interpretations—whether Achashverosh refers to the Holy One, Blessed be He, or, *lehavdil*, to the king of Media and Persia—not only do not contradict each other, but are consistent with and depend upon each other, as follows.

**H.** By way of introduction—when the Holy One, Blessed be He, chooses a certain thing to fulfill His mission, then that thing (through which the mission is fulfilled) is secondary and nullified to the mission. Even when the existence of that thing in this world is greater in measure than the mission fulfilled through it, it is nevertheless secondary and nullified to the mission.
—Similar to what we find in the laws of *Shabbos* regarding the labor of carrying, that "one who carries food less than the required measure in a vessel (that has the required measure) is exempt even for the vessel, because the vessel is secondary to it". That is, even though the vessel is larger than the food within it, since his intention in carrying the vessel is for the sake of the food, and the vessel *serves* the food, the vessel is secondary to the food. Similarly, we find in the laws of *berachos* that a *berachah* on the primary item exempts the secondary item, even when the secondary item is greater in quantity than the primary item, since for the person eating it, it is considered secondary. And if this is true in worldly matters, how much more so in matters of permission and the profane that serve matters of holiness, which are secondary and nullified to holiness. —
To the point that its entire existence is the mission.
Therefore, the excellence that exists in the mission is also achieved in the thing through which the mission is fulfilled (whose entire existence is the mission)—similar to what is explained in *Tanya* regarding the refinement and purification of the body. Since the study of Torah and the fulfillment of *mitzvos* are performed by the body (for even the study of Torah must be done through speech, and the movement of the lips is an act, and how much more so regarding actual *mitzvos*), the body itself is also refined and purified.

**I.** Based on this, one can explain the connection and relevance of the two interpretations of "Achashverosh":
"Achashverosh"—refers indeed to Achashverosh literally, the king of Media and Persia;
But, since the entire existence of Achashverosh is that through him the miracle of Purim occurred, and through him a *mitzvah* from the Sages was added, regarding which the Sages said that the words of the Scribes are beloved and strict, etc.,
— [Note the words of the *Gemara*, "The *Megillah* is called a book and is called a letter," and it is explained in *Chassidus* "the difference between the category of a book and the category of a letter... from what we see that a book is eternal... but a letter is written only for the moment and not to endure at all, for after reading the letter, one will take it and burn it or throw it away and has no more need for it ever, etc.," and the *Megillah*—even though it is called a "letter," it is also called a "book," as we learn from what is written, "Write this as a remembrance in a book," "in the book, that which is written in the *Megillah*"],
Therefore, it is perfectly appropriate to say that "Achashverosh"—whose entire existence is that through him the miracle of Purim occurred—is the one whose is the end and the beginning.
And this is the content of the matter of mentioning "the power of Achashverosh"—for even according to the interpretation of Achashverosh literally, the king of Media and Persia, "the power of Achashverosh," whose is the end and the beginning, is revealed and expressed through him.

**J.** And this is what is written, "He is Achashverosh who reigns from Hodu to Kush"—for since Achashverosh is "the one whose is the end and the beginning," therefore he "reigns from Hodu to Kush":
"Hodu" and "Kush" are two opposites, for: "Kush"—from the word for blackness, as it is written, "For he had taken a Kushite woman", "Can a Kushite change his skin?" —indicates a low level, etc. And "Hodu"—from the word for *hoda'ah* (acknowledgment/surrender), above reason and intellect—indicates a high level in holiness.
And Achashverosh is "the one who reigns from Hodu to Kush," for Achashverosh, "whose is the end and the beginning," refers to a level above the chain of descent (*hishtalshelus*), where there are no limitations of end and beginning (below and above). Similar to what is written, "I am the first and I am the last," that first and last are in equality. Therefore, he is the one who reigns from Hodu to Kush, from the depth of the heights to the depth of the depths.

**K.** And these two matters (beginning and end, Hodu and Kush) exist also among the Jewish people, of whom it is said, "You are children to Hashem your God"—that they possess the matter of *hoda'ah* and nullification to Divinity, and together with this, they also have the power of choice [which is taken from the choice that is in His Essence, may He be blessed, and therefore it is given only to the Jewish people, for they are rooted in the Essence that chose Israel His people] to choose the opposite, *chas v’shalom*, the category of Kush.
Now, in the time before Purim, the Jewish people mixed the two matters of Hodu and Kush: on one hand—it was the time before and in proximity to the redemption (from the Babylonian exile), the category of Hodu; and on the other hand—they enjoyed the feast of that wicked one, the category of Kush.
And from this, they caused the giving of power to the *sitra achra*—for the vitality and nourishment that is drawn to the *sitra achra* is through the Jewish people —that Achashverosh (literally) who reigns from Hodu to Kush, decreed a decree against the Jewish people.
However, through the fact that they stood for the entire year in *mesiras nefesh* that is above reason and intellect, *mesiras nefesh* from the side of the essence of the *neshamah* that is above the revealed powers in which the division of Hodu and Kush belongs—they reached the category of Achashverosh whose is the end and the beginning, a category above the chain of descent, where end and beginning are in equality, and through this, the nullification of the decree was brought about.

**L.** And this matter is also expressed in the *avodah* of Purim—which is in a manner of "not knowing the difference between 'cursed is Haman' and 'blessed is Mordechai'" :
"Cursed is Haman"—refers to the *avodah* of "turn from evil," and "blessed is Mordechai"—refers to the *avodah* of "do good."
Now, even though the *avodah* of "do good" ("blessed is Mordechai") is more pleasant and beloved than the *avodah* of "turn from evil" ("cursed is Haman"), for in the *avodah* of "turn from evil" one must engage with evil, and anyone who wrestles with a filthy person becomes filthy himself —nevertheless, the *avodah* of "turn from evil" is performed *with the same pleasantness and affection* as the *avodah* of "do good" ("not knowing the difference between 'cursed is Haman' and 'blessed is Mordechai'"), because he does not take himself (his own "taste") into account, but stands in a movement of *mesiras nefesh* to the Master of Will, who is above the definitions of Hodu and Kush. Consequently, he does everything that the Master of Will demands with the same pleasantness and affection.

**M.** And this is also what the Rebbe Rayatz demanded of each one of us: two extremes:
On one hand—to be immersed and absorbed ("*ligan*") in the study of Torah, the revealed Torah and the hidden Torah, the teachings of *Chassidus*, secrets and secrets of secrets—which is the depth of the heights.
And at the same time—to influence a Jew and arouse him regarding the observance of *Shabbos*, *taharas hamishpachah* in the simplest way, and to speak with a Jewish child who knows nothing about Judaism, to bring him closer to Judaism.
And through engaging in both extremes together, we hasten and draw down the coming of *Moshiach*—that "behold, he stands behind our wall" —starting with the personal *Moshiach*, for each one in his own personal matters, and consequently also the coming of *Moshiach* for all of Israel, and for the entire world.

But since we lack the aforementioned, we are forced to walk on the paths that were paved for us. And then one walks on the responsibility and shoulders of those who showed and paved the paths. Therefore, we strive to re-establish the practices, even if (for various reasons) they were discontinued for a time.
The Rebbe (Rashab) used to collect money (or instruct that they donate)—at the time of a *farbrengen*—three times a year: *Simchas Torah*, *Yud-Tes Kislev*, and Purim. And through this, in addition to the excellence of *tzedakah* in general, which brings success in material matters, as the Sages say, "Give a tenth so that you may become rich," and likewise in spiritual matters, as our Rebbe the Alter Rebbe writes in *Torah Ohr* that through the *mitzvah* of *tzedakah*, one’s mind and heart become a thousand times purer, and it is understood from the note of the *Tzemach Tzedek* that the expression "a thousand times" is not by way of habit or exaggeration, but a thousand times *literally*—beyond this, when one gives for the matters for which the Rebbe Rayatz collected, and at the same time that he collected, which is a time of favor and a *segulah* for this—this brings an addition of blessing and success, and one can come to the Rebbe Rayatz and demand that he "draw down" all the promises in actuality.

By way of introduction—in general, in the months there is a difference between the first half of the month and the second half, but regarding the matters of Purim, it is said, "And the month which was turned for them from sorrow to joy and from mourning to a holiday, etc.," meaning that the matter of "it was turned, etc.", with all the matters of Purim, exists in every month of *Adar*.
Therefore, all who are here should write to their relatives, friends, and acquaintances that during this month they can participate in this, and whatever they give during this month will be included in the Purim fund.
All participants, both on the spot and those who will participate later, can write on a *petek* their name and their mother’s name and the amount they donate, and it will be read at the holy *tziyun* of the Rebbe Rayatz, and this will arouse and draw down additions of blessing and success for each and every one in all that they and their families need in material and spiritual matters.

**O.** It was discussed earlier (section 6 and onward) regarding "the power of Achashverosh," that "whose is the end and the beginning," in the *avodah* of man—that the *avodah* must be with full force in both lines of "end" and "beginning" equally.
For example—that the *avodah* must be both in the line of "turn from evil" and in the line of "do good," without making calculations as to which *avodah* is more pleasant.
Likewise, the *avodah* must be both regarding oneself and regarding others, without making calculations that he still has plenty of *avodah* with himself, especially when he knows his deeds, words, and thoughts throughout the entire day and night, etc. And if so, how can he be an "influencer" of others? In the words of the Sages : "You have not cleared the area near your own palace"—but as the Rebbe (Rashab) said, we do not know anything in the secret of the *birurim* (refinements), and consequently, one should not make calculations, but whatever comes to hand, one must seize and do it immediately.
And when the conduct is in a manner of "the power of Achashverosh"—then the nullification of the decree is brought about, and on the contrary, "it was turned", that the nations of the world themselves say, "Write concerning the Jews as you see fit".

**P.** One should add and explain also the continuation of the words of the *Gemara* regarding "the power of Mordechai"—that this matter is connected especially with the nullification of the decree, through the fact that "the fear of Mordechai fell upon them" :
By way of introduction, the words of the *Gemara* : "Why is he called *Yehudi* (even though 'he came from Binyamin,' 'a man of Yemini')? Because he denied idolatry, for anyone who denies idolatry is called a *Yehudi*." From this it is understood that the nullification of the decree is brought about by the denial of idolatry.

**Q.** Explanation of the matter:
Idolatry and *shituf*—simply put—means that one thinks there is another existence that has some independent power, etc., and the denial of idolatry means that one believes and knows that the Holy One, Blessed be He, is the sole ruler and Master of the entire world.
However, the teachings of *Chassidus* revealed and explained the faith and attainment in the matter of "there is none else", "even in the void of the world" —that not only is there nothing that has independent power, but furthermore, that "there is none else," that there is no existence at all—not even the existence of a secondary thing ("'else' is a term for secondary")—apart from Him, may He be blessed.
The explanation for this—as it is written, "Apart from Me there is no God": "God" (*Elokim*) has the same *gematria* as "nature" (*hateva*), and "Apart from Me there is no God" means that even the existence of nature—the general order of faces, right, left, and back —is Divinity, except that there is Divinity as it is in revelation, and there is Divinity as it comes in concealment and hiding of the garments of nature, to the point that there is a concealment and hiding that is "directly against Hashem, to say 'I and none else'" ; but the truth is that even this concealment and hiding is only for the sake of fulfilling the mission of the Holy One, Blessed be He, for "there is no place (time or matter) empty of Him".
And this faith—the teachings of *Chassidus* demand that it be (not by way of a *makif*, but) in an internal way, meaning, that it should illuminate and penetrate all the powers of his soul—that this matter can only be through understanding and attainment. Therefore, the teachings of *Chassidus* explain several interpretations and explanations in this, and also brings explanations from books of philosophy, such as the matter that "all that exists" "were found only from the truth of His existence" —that through this, faith will come in an internal way.
And when the knowledge of "there is none else, even in the void of the world" is lacking, meaning, that he thinks there is some other existence, *chas v’shalom*, apart from Divinity—even though he knows that the Holy One, Blessed be He, is the Master over this existence, nevertheless, the very fact that he thinks there is some existence and the Holy One, Blessed be He, must rule over this existence—this is, in the subtlest of subtleties, a matter of idolatry.

**R.** And as a result of the aforementioned knowledge and attainment in His unity, may He be blessed—which must bring benefit in *avodah* like any knowledge and attainment—the *avodah* is performed in a manner of "the power of Achashverosh," that one engages in all matters of *avodah*, and in everything that comes to hand, without calculations as to which *avodah* is easier and more pleasant, and the like.
More specifically:
The reason for the fact that there is room to distinguish between the *avodah* of "turn from evil" and the *avodah* of "do good"—that engaging in matters of good and holiness is more pleasant and beloved than engaging with an existence of evil and opposition, because (a) engaging with opposition must be in a manner of war, which is a very difficult *avodah*, (b) even when one succeeds in defeating the opposition, it is in a manner of "anyone who wrestles with a filthy person becomes filthy himself" (as mentioned in section 12)—is because one relates to the existence of evil as an existence of *opposition*; but when there is the denial of idolatry as it should be, meaning, that one knows there is no existence apart from Him, may He be blessed—then one knows there is nothing that contradicts and opposes Divinity, for no essence conceals another essence, and it is only that the Holy One, Blessed be He, wanted there to be a matter of *avodah* and exertion (and not in a manner of a gift, which is "bread of shame"), and therefore it appears to the body and the animal soul that there is concealment and hiding, but in truth there is nothing that contradicts and opposes Divinity.
And since this is so, there is no difference between the *avodah* of "turn from evil" and the *avodah* of "do good," between "cursed is Haman" and "blessed is Mordechai"—for in truth even "Haman" is not an opposition, and his true essence is only so that he may be transformed into holiness.
Consequently, even the *avodah* of "turn from evil," "cursed is Haman," is easy, and from joy—since one knows that the entire existence of concealment is only *that it appears so* in order to arouse the matter of *avodah*, and when one stands with strength and is not affected by the "concealment," then one sees even with physical eyes that the concealment is not an existence at all, and it is transformed into holiness.
Likewise, it is understood also regarding engaging in everything that comes to hand—that from the knowledge that "there is none else, even in the void of the world," it is laid out for him with complete simplicity that everything is by Divine Providence, and since now this *avodah* has come to him, it certainly belongs to his task and mission for which his *neshamah* descended below.

**S.** Based on this, the connection of the denial of idolatry ("*Yehudi*, because he denied idolatry") and the power of Achashverosh to the miracle of Purim is also understood:
Since through the denial of idolatry, that "there is none else," to the point that even a thing that appears to be "opposition" is not an existence apart from Divinity, the *avodah* is performed in all matters in equality, "not knowing the difference between 'cursed is Haman' and 'blessed is Mordechai'" (as mentioned above)—this is drawn down and revealed also *in material terms*, and consequently the decree is nullified, and on the contrary, "it was turned," as a result of the matter of "it was turned" in spiritual terms.
And from this, it is understood also in the personal *avodah* of each and every Jew, that when he knows that all matters are Divinity, and in truth there is no evil—this is drawn down also in material terms, that he sees with physical eyes that not only is "everything the Merciful One does, He does for good", meaning, that the intention in this is "for good," but more than this, that "this too is for good", that this thing itself is for good, in the good that is seen and revealed to him and his family, in all that he and they need.
