**1.** [Rabbi Yisroel Jacobson introduced the students to the Rebbe, noting that they were graduating from elementary school. The Rebbe said:]

Surely a curriculum, teachers, and administration have already been prepared for the upcoming school year, and if so, this is not a "graduation," but merely a break.

[Rabbi Jacobson read the names of the students (and their mothers' names) before the Rebbe, requesting a blessing.

The Rebbe then said:]

In matters of Torah, it is not appropriate to say that one has "finished" anything. For the Torah was given to us by Hashem, who is Ein Sof—limitless—and therefore His Torah (which "He gave us, His Torah"[1]) is also limitless. Consequently, it is impossible to say that one has finished the entire Torah, as it is infinite.

Furthermore: not only is it impossible to say that one has finished the entire Torah, but on the contrary—the more one learns, the more one’s knowledge and awareness grow regarding how much more there is to add and increase in study ("how much more there is still to learn").

And within this very context—beyond the study of Torah itself—there is also what the Torah must *teach the person* throughout the entire course of their life[2].

**2.** These words apply to study in Torah institutions in general, and specifically at "Beis Rivkah":

"Beis Rivkah" is an institution founded by my father-in-law, the Rebbe, and it is conducted in his spirit.

#### 4

Since this is the case, all those who have studied at "Beis Rivkah" and received an education in yiras shamayim,

– and surely you have had great success in your studies thus far, and will continue to have success in your studies in the future –

surely you have learned and know that you have not yet finished your studies, and that you have much more to learn.

**3.** From this, it is understood that the summer period is nothing more than a *break*—in order to rest and recharge for the coming school year, in which you will be able to continue your studies in the "high school" of "Beis Rivkah" with even greater dedication.

Moreover:

Even during the summer period itself, there will surely be no break or disconnection, chas v'shalom, from the study of Torah,

– for the Torah is "Toras *Chaim*" (a Torah of life)[3], and in matters of life, there is no such thing as a break in time, chas v'shalom[4] –

and surely, even during "vacation," you will set aside—outside the walls of the "Beis Rivkah" building—a set amount of time each day for the study of Torah. And it is best that this study be done together with another friend.

**4.** May Hashem bless you with a healthy summer, and may you have success both in your rest and in your avodah, and may your parents derive from you much nachas—Jewish nachas.

Convey my regards to your parents and relatives.

May the merit of my father-in-law, the Rebbe, the founder of this institution, stand by you, so that by continuing your studies at "Beis Rivkah," you will achieve the goal, purpose, and mission that a student of "Beis Rivkah" must reach—that your Torah education will influence and serve throughout your lives as a "guide" in your personal lives, and in your influence upon your circles and environment.

Be well.

## 5

"When you come to the land of your habitations... and you shall make a fire-offering... and the one who brings the offering shall bring..." [1] and it concludes with the matter of the *nesachim* (libations). As Rashi explains [2], the statement "and you shall make a fire-offering" is not a command but a permission; for when it arises in your heart to make a fire-offering to Hashem, then the one who brings the offering must bring *nesachim* along with the sacrifice—meaning, the command applies to the *nesachim*. This obligation applies specifically "when you come to the land of your habitations," for while they were in the desert, they were not yet commanded regarding *nesachim*. There are several opinions regarding this [3]: one says that in the desert they were not commanded regarding *nesachim* at all; another says that in the desert, *nesachim* were not an obligation together with the sacrifice; another says that the obligation of *nesachim* existed only for the *tamid* offering and not for other sacrifices; and others say the obligation existed only for communal sacrifices but not for individual ones. However, all agree that upon their arrival in the land, a special obligation was introduced regarding *nesachim*, which applies to all sacrifices. This obligation was introduced specifically after the inheritance and settlement of the land, as it is written, "when you come to the land of your habitations"—specifically then. We must understand: why does the matter of *nesachim* depend upon the inheritance and settlement of Eretz Yisrael?

To understand this, we must first explain the difference between *nesachim* and sacrifices [4]—that is, between the sacrifices themselves and the *nesachim* that accompany them (as mentioned above, that the obligation of *nesachim* is with the sacrifices). The concept of sacrifices is connected specifically with fire, as it is written, "and you shall make a fire-offering (*ishah*) to Hashem" (*ishah* being related to *esh*—fire), and it is written [5], "My offering, My bread for My fire-offerings (*ishai*)." The nature of fire is to always be in a motion of ascent, from below to above. As is known, this is the distinction between the element of fire and the other elements [6]: water and earth have a nature of descent, and wind, although its nature is not descent, does not possess the nature of ascent

## 6

either [7]. In contrast, the nature of fire is to ascend upward. Thus, the concept of ascent from below to above, as it exists in physical things, is found in the element of fire below. From the fact that sacrifices are called *ishai* (fire-offerings), it is proven that the primary aspect of sacrifices is the ascent from below to above. This concept applies to all sacrifices—not only the *olah* (burnt offering), which is entirely for Hashem, but the *zevach* (peace offering) as well, whose essence is ascent. Just as it is with the sacrifices below, so too is it with the level above that is reached through sacrifices; for this level, too, is called by the name "fire," as it is written, "My offering, My bread for My fire-offerings," where *ishai* refers to the angels in general, and specifically to the *serafim*, who are always in a motion of ascent [8]. Although angels in general are called "standing ones," as is known [9] in the explanation of the verse [10], "And I will give you walkers among these standing ones," nevertheless, there are several levels among the angels. The *chayos* are always in a state of *ratzo v'shov* (running and returning), as it is written [11], "And the *chayos* ran and returned," while the *serafim* are in a state of standing, as it is written [12], "The *serafim* stood above Him." The explanation of this is that the *chayos hakodesh* are in the world of *Yetzirah*, whereas the *serafim* are in the world of *Beriah*. The difference between *Beriah* and *Yetzirah* is that the world of *Beriah* is the illumination of the *Chabad* of the *Ein Sof*, as the saying goes [13], "The *Imma Ila'ah* (Higher Mother) nests in the Throne," meaning that it is the place of the illumination of the higher *mochin* (intellect). Because of this, the *bitul* (nullification) of the *serafim* is a constant *bitul*; they are in a state of constant standing without change—meaning they are "burned" by their *bitul*. However, the world of *Yetzirah* is the place of the illumination of the higher *midos* (emotions), and the illumination of the *mochin* there is only from the aspect of the *mochin* that relate to the *midos* alone, not the *mochin* as they are in their own essence. Since the nature of the *midos* is *hispaalus* (emotional arousal)—meaning change—their service is in a manner of change, *ratzo v'shov*, and they are not in a state of standing. And even though it is written [14] regarding the *chayos*, "when they stood, they let down their wings," nevertheless [15], this standing is due to the revelation of the aspect of "above the firmament," where an illumination of light from the *mochin* is drawn into them, but in their own essence, they possess the changes of *ratzo v'shov*. In contrast, the *serafim* are in a state of constant standing without change—"the *serafim* stood"—because they are in the world of *Beriah*, the dwelling place of the higher *mochin*. This is what is meant by

## 7

"My bread for My fire-offerings," which refers to the angels in general, and specifically to the *serafim*, who are always in a state of ascent. Thus, both the sacrifices themselves are in a state of ascent (and therefore called *ishah*), and the place they reach above is in a state of elevation and exit from the limitations of the *keilim* (vessels). This ascent reaches up to the level of the name *Havayah*, as it is written, "a fire-offering to *Havayah*" specifically; for *Havayah* denotes a level above the limitations of the worlds, which is the meaning of *Havayah* being *Hayah, Hoveh, v'Yihyeh* (was, is, and will be) as one [16]. Through this, they ascend even higher than the name *Havayah*—up to the *raza d'Ein Sof* (the mystery of the *Ein Sof*), as the saying goes [17], "The mystery of the sacrifice ascends to the mystery of the *Ein Sof*." That is to say, the sacrifices' own ascent is in the aspect of the name *Havayah* ("a fire-offering to *Havayah*"), but afterward, through the ascent and the exit from all limitations of the *keilim*, it ascends even higher, to the *raza d'Ein Sof*. This is the meaning of the phrase "the mystery of the sacrifice ascends to the mystery of the *Ein Sof*": that specifically the aspect of the "mystery of the sacrifice"—the secret within the sacrifice—ascends to the mystery of the *Ein Sof*.

**However**, the matter of *nesachim* in the sacrifices is not a matter of ascent from below to above, but specifically descent from above to below, which is the opposite of the concept of sacrifices. Just as in sacrifices the ascent is to the highest level, to the *raza d'Ein Sof*, so too in *nesachim*, the descent and *hemshech* (drawing down) is to the very lowest place. This is why the *nesachim* that were poured onto the altar would descend into the *shitin* (channels) that were hollow and descended to the *tehom* (the abyss) [18], which is the lowest point, the place of the "lower waters" [19]. As it is stated in the *Midrash* [20], that in the beginning the world was water within water, and through the utterance "Let there be a firmament in the midst of the waters" [21], the upper waters ascended upward and the lower waters descended to the *tehom*. This is the concept of *nesachim*: that the descent and *hemshech* through them reaches the *tehom*. Not only is this not an ascent upward, or even a standing in one place, but it is a *hemshech* downward. Furthermore, the *hemshech* is to the lowest possible place—not just to a place that has value relative to the place from which it descends, but to the very lowest, the place of the lower waters, of which it is said, "The lower waters weep, 'We want to be before the King'" [22].

**And with this** we can understand why in all sacrifices there must also be the matter of *nesachim*. For the sacrifices' essence is ascent from below to above, and this is not the ultimate intent; rather, the ultimate

## 8

intent is specifically the *hemshech* downward, as it is written [23], "He did not create it for chaos, but formed it to be inhabited." Therefore, there must be, together with the sacrifices, the matter of *nesachim*, whose essence is *hemshech* from above to below, because only through this is the higher intent fulfilled. With this, we can also understand what our Sages said [24]: "Whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin* is as if he offered an *olah* without a *minchah* (meal offering) and a *zevach* without *nesachim*," and "Whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin* is as if he bears false witness" [25]. It follows, then, that sacrifices without *nesachim* are like false witness. The matter is that the sacrifices were in the Temple and the Tabernacle, which is called the "Tabernacle of the Testimony" [26], for the essence of the Tabernacle is to be a testimony to Israel that the *Shechinah* dwells among them [27]. And since the testimony to Israel is specifically when they are below, souls in bodies, therefore the concept of sacrifices without *nesachim*—that is, ascent without *hemshech*—is false witness. There is a need specifically for *nesachim*, which is the matter of *hemshech* downward, for through this specifically is the higher intent fulfilled.

**The explanation** of this matter in the service of man: We mentioned above what our Sages said, that whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin* is as if he offered an *olah* without a *minchah* and a *zevach* without *nesachim*. *Krias Shema* and *tefillin* are like *zevach* and *nesachim*; *Krias Shema* is like the *zevach*, and *tefillin* is like the *nesachim*. The explanation of this is that the primary aspect of *Krias Shema* is the ascent from below to above, for the essence of *Krias Shema* is the first verse, "Hear O Israel, *Havayah* our G-d, *Havayah* is One" [28], which is the matter of ascent, as follows. In the earlier generations, this verse alone was sufficient to reach this level. Later, in the final generations, when they saw that it was impossible to reach "Hear O Israel" immediately, they instituted the recitation of the *berachos* of *Krias Shema* before it [29]. Afterward, when they saw that even this was not enough, they instituted the recitation of *Pesukei D'Zimra* before that. And now, when even this is not enough, there must be the *hisbonenus* (contemplation) before the *tefillah*. However, the ultimate goal is to reach the matter of "Hear O Israel." Through the *hisbonenus* in *Pesukei D'Zimra* regarding the greatness of Hashem in the creation of the worlds, one creates an impression from the outside in the person. Afterward, in the *berachos* of *Krias Shema*, one contemplates the root of his soul and the root of his animal soul in the higher angels who are nullified to *Elokus* (Divinity). All this acts upon the *Neshama Elokis* and the *Neshama Behamis*, so that they both go together and say "Hear O Israel." *Shema* is from the language of gathering, as it is written [30], "And Saul gathered

## 9

the people" [31]—meaning the gathering of all the powers and senses. The power for this is from the aspect of *Yisrael*, for "Israel ascended in thought" [32], and thought is for itself and removed from the other. Therefore, even at the time of the soul's descent below, it has the power to gather and collect all the powers and senses. Then one recognizes and feels that "*Havayah* is our G-d"—meaning [33] that the aspect of *Havayah* that is above the *Seder Hishtalshelus* (order of evolution) and the worlds (as mentioned above), *Hayah, Hoveh, v'Yihyeh* as one, is our G-d, our power and our life-force, without intermediaries—that is, without the intermediary of the name *Elokim*. The reason for this is that the souls are higher than the angels, for the angels' root is from the exteriority of the *keilim*, and the exteriority of the vessel is not one thing with the light that is clothed within it. But the souls of Israel ascended in thought, meaning their root is in the aspect of the interiority of the *keilim* [34], and the interiority of the *keilim* is in complete unity with the light clothed in the vessel. Therefore, "*Havayah* is our G-d" without any intermediaries. And this is what it concludes, "*Havayah* is One," that in every single place, He desires only *Havayah* alone. This is drawn down and acts so that there will be "And you shall love *Havayah* your G-d with all your heart and with all your soul" [35]. And it continues also in what is said in the second paragraph of *Krias Shema*, for in this paragraph it relates to the affairs of the world, and yet he feels that he is in unity with *Havayah* is One. Therefore, "lest your heart be seduced" [36] is equal for him to "and you turn away and serve" [37]—meaning that only when his heart turns, when the world gives a certain "turn" away from *Elokus*, is it already equal for him to "and you turn away and serve" [38]. For before he reached the level of "Hear O Israel," "a man's way is right in his own eyes" [39], and it is possible that he is truly righteous. But after he has reached this level, where he feels the aspect of *Havayah* is One, he is then before the King, and "he who points with his finger before the King..." [40]. Therefore, the seduction of the heart is equal for him to "and you turn away and serve." Before *Krias Shema*, when he was not "before the King," they were two separate matters—the matter of "lest your heart be seduced" and the matter of "and you turn away and serve." But when he feels *Havayah* is One, it acts upon him automatically that "you shall love *Havayah* your G-d with all your heart and with all your soul and with all your might," and then there is no difference whether "your heart be seduced" or "you turn away and serve." In general,

## 10

this service of *Krias Shema* is the service of ascent and exit from the *keilim*. It is understood from this that *Krias Shema* is like the matter of sacrifices—ascent from below to above (as mentioned above). However, the matter of *tefillin* is *hemshech* from above to below, for the matter of *tefillin* is that those same paragraphs of *Krias Shema* that one says in speech and thought should be written in physical ink and on physical parchment and in written letters. We find the same regarding the Tablets, for it is written [41], "And the Tablets were the work of G-d, and the writing was the writing of G-d, engraved upon the Tablets." And our Sages said [42], "Do not read *charut* (engraved) but *cheirut* (freedom)," for the Tablets brought about freedom from the evil inclination, freedom from the Angel of Death, and freedom from subjugation [43]. There is a novelty here: that the matter of freedom was brought about not by the revelation that occurred in the utterance of the Ten Commandments—"One thing G-d spoke, two have I heard" [44]—because that was in speech alone. In order for the *hemshech* of freedom to be in the world, it had to be specifically through the writing on physical Tablets—that is, the *hemshech* downward specifically. This is the concept of *tefillin*: the *hemshech* of the aspect of *Havayah* downward, which is the ultimate intent. Therefore, after the ascent of *Krias Shema*, we say in the *Shemoneh Esrei* prayer, "Heal us," etc., so that a new will (*yehi ratzon*) [45] will be drawn down and He will be a healer of the sick, and similarly, one blesses the years—meaning *hemshech* into physicality specifically. This is why the entire Torah is compared to *tefillin* [46]—meaning (also) the *mitzvos* in the Torah, for in all the *mitzvos*, the primary intent is the *hemshech* downward specifically.

**This is** "When you come to the land of your habitations" [47], for the obligation of *nesachim* is specifically in Eretz Yisrael and specifically after inheritance and settlement. For in the desert, there was only the matter of sacrifices, because the service in the desert is in the aspect of ascent alone, for the desert is the opposite of settlement and *hemshech*—"a dry land where there is no water" [48], for water is *hemshech* ("water descends from above to below" [49]), while the desert's essence is ascent. This was also the argument of the spies who did not want to enter the land, because they wanted to perform their service in the aspect of ascent alone, and they argued that service in a manner of settlement is in a manner of "a land that consumes its inhabitants" [50]. Therefore, in the desert, there was no matter of *nesachim*. But when they came to Eretz Yisrael and in a manner of settlement, after inheritance and settlement, in the individual service of each person, then there must be the matter of *nesachim*.

## 11

**However**, just as *nesachim* come with the sacrifices, so too it is impossible for the service of *hemshech* from above to below to be complete without the preceding service of ascent. Therefore, Israel was first in the desert, but this is not the ultimate intent; rather, the ultimate intent is the entry into the land. This is what is meant by [51], "For I desired kindness and not sacrifice," as explained in the *maamarim* of the Rebbe Rayatz [52], that "desired" (*chafatzti*) refers to the *chefetz* (desire), which is the interiority of the will. For "will" (*ratzon*) in general refers to the exteriority of the will, and *chefetz* is the interiority of the will [53]. The interiority of the will and the ultimate intent is specifically in kindness, whose essence is *hemshech* from above to below, and not in sacrifice, which is ascent from below to above. For although at first there must be the matter of ascent, nevertheless, this is not the *chefetz* and this is not the ultimate goal [54]; rather, the goal is specifically in kindness and *hemshech* to the very lowest, to the place where there is nothing lower. This is specifically the truth of the concept of kindness. For if the *hemshech* is downward to one who is on his level, it is not true kindness; rather, it is specifically when the *hemshech* is to the very lowest of all levels. A proof of this is from Abraham our forefather, who was the "man of kindness" [55], and therefore he bestowed also upon the Arabs, etc. [56]. Therefore, Israel did not remain in the desert but entered the land, and upon their entry into the land, and especially after inheritance and settlement, they became obligated in *nesachim*, because the ultimate intent is to make for Him, may He be blessed, a dwelling place in the lowest realms.

**A.** The Rebbe instructed that a *nigun* be played and then delivered a *maamar* beginning with the words, "When you come into the land of your habitations," etc.

***

**B.** Continuing from what was explained above (in the *maamar*) regarding the concept of *nesachim* (libations) in the *avodah* of man—which is the concept of drawing down [Divinity] below—it should be added:

In *Likutei Torah* on this *parsha*, it is stated that "a person must humble himself and say, 'It is enough for the servant to be like his master,' and to make a dwelling place for Him, to be a dwelling for Him in the lower worlds."

That is to say: A person should not say that he has no desire to descend from his level in order to occupy himself with physical and material matters. For "it is enough for the servant to be like his master," and just as Hashem desired to have a dwelling place in the lower worlds, so too must it be fitting for him ("*es zol im onshteyn*") to descend and occupy himself with physical and material matters to make them a dwelling place for Him, may He be blessed.

And since "the *tzaddikim* are like their Creator," it must also be said regarding them, "It is enough for the servant to be like his master." In the present case, this means one must learn from the conduct of the Rebbe, the Rebbe Rayatz, as will be explained below.

**C.** By way of introduction: This Shabbos is *Shabbos Mevarchim* for the month of Tammuz, which contains the special day of 12 Tammuz, the birthday and day of liberation of the Rebbe Rayatz. Therefore, this is the appropriate time to contemplate the Rebbe’s conduct during the time of his imprisonment and liberation:

Even during the time between his release from prison and his liberation on 12 Tammuz—while he was in his city of refuge (Kostroma), a place of exile—the Rebbe continued to occupy himself with disseminating Torah and spreading Yiddishkeit, such as founding a *cheder*, a *mikveh*, and the like.

On Shabbos, the Rebbe would prolong his *davening* in the *beis medrash*—a matter that made

#### 13

a great impression and was a source of great wonder: A Yid *davening* until three or four in the afternoon!... Everyone else had long since finished *davening*, eaten their Shabbos meal, and even had time to sleep ("*m'hot shoin opgedavnt, opgesn un zich oisgeshlofn*"), and here stood a "Rabin" (Rabbi) still *davening*!... The commotion in the town became so great that even the town’s priest came himself to the *beis medrash* to see this great wonder!

And despite all this, the fact that during those days the Rebbe was unable to *write a letter to a Yid* regarding simple matters caused him pain and distress, to the point that he expressed to one of the close *Chassidim* who were with him [the *Chassid* R. Eliyahu Chaim or the *Chassid* R. Michael Dvorkin] that if such is the situation, what is the need for a hand?!—it would be better if the hand were to become thin and withered ("*es volt zein beser di hant zol ch"v opdarn*")!...

Consider this:

Even though we are speaking of *the Rebbe’s holy hand*, upon which he donned *tefillin* and with which he performed many matters of holiness, etc.—the Rebbe says that since a few days had passed in which this hand could not *write a letter to a Yid* [even though during those very days he performed many activities in spreading Torah and Yiddishkeit, in addition to the fact that his stay in the city of refuge was part of the overall imprisonment for his work in spreading Torah and Yiddishkeit], it would be better for it to become thin and withered.

**D.** This conduct serves as a lesson and instruction for everyone regarding involvement with others:

There are those who argue: Why must they descend from their level to occupy themselves with another who is in a low standing and state? Surely it would be better if they utilized this time to occupy themselves with lofty matters in their *avodah* with themselves ("*ton mit zich*").

They know, indeed, that the world requires involvement with lower things—in the language of our Sages, "The world cannot exist... without a tanner"—but each of them argues, why must *he* be the "tanner"?!...

#### 14

Furthermore—they continue to argue—they do not accomplish anything in these lower things, for they *do not see* that they have accomplished anything... and on the other hand, *they see clearly* that descending to occupy themselves with lower matters disturbs and confuses them in their own *avodah*. If so, they argue, what does Hashem want from them ("*vos vil der Oibershter fun zei hobn*"), why must they descend to occupy themselves with lower matters ("*noch vos darf men krikhn in niderike inyonim*"), and risk the possibility that they might, Heaven forbid, come to a state of "your heart be seduced," and the subsequent "and you turn away and serve [other gods]"—at the very least in the most subtle of subtleties, similar to the saying of our Sages, "Yidden in the Diaspora are idolaters in purity."

The answer to all this is: "It is enough for the servant to be like his master."

Despite the greatness of the Rebbe’s level, it was fitting for him ("*im iz ongeshtanen*") to occupy himself with simple matters, and not only that, but he dedicated himself to this involvement to such an extent that he said that if not for this, it would be better for his hand to be thin and withered... If so, if it was fitting for the Rebbe to occupy himself with *simple* matters, then surely involvement with simple matters can be fitting for people of our stature, who, after all the levels... both the true levels and even the imagined levels, "no matter how much he thinks of himself," understand that *he has no value at all* compared to the Rebbe!

If he cannot influence himself to occupy himself with others through other contemplation, he should accomplish this by contemplating that "it is enough for the servant to be like his master."

And more than that: by influencing himself "*to be* like his master," then he "*will be* like his master." In this very thing—first "*like* his master," with the *kaf* of comparison, and afterwards even without the *kaf* of comparison, since his entire existence becomes the existence of his master, similar to a *shaliach* whose entire existence becomes the existence of the *meshale'ach*.

***

**E.** Continuing from what was said regarding the concept of *nesachim*, the drawing down below through involvement with others—it should be added and clarified also from the other side regarding the need and necessity for *avodah* with oneself. In this matter, there is a lesson and instruction

#### 15

from the conduct regarding *Krias Shema* and *tefillin*, which are analogous to *zevach* (the sacrifice) and *nesachim* (the libations), as understood from the saying of our Sages, "Whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin*... is as if he offered... a sacrifice without libations" (as mentioned above in the *maamar*).

One must first preface and explain the custom found among *Chassidim* and men of deed to recite the "small *Shema*" without *tefillin*.

—In my *reshimos*, I noted what I heard from the Rebbe Rayatz, that his father, the Rebbe Rashab, would recite the "small *Shema*" while wearing *tefillin*. Nevertheless, we see in practice that it is the custom of *Chassidim* to recite the "small *Shema*" without *tefillin*—

Seemingly, since the "small *Shema*" is because one fears that he might miss the time for *Shema*—meaning, it is not "like a person reading from the Torah," but rather to fulfill the *mitzvah* of *Shema* from the Torah—how can one recite *Shema* without *tefillin*, contrary to the saying of our Sages, "Whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin* is as if he testifies falsely against himself"?

This has already been discussed by many, and their conclusion is that the definition of "whoever recites the *Shema* without *tefillin*" (which is considered false testimony) applies to one who does not don *tefillin* at all. But one who does not don *tefillin* *at the time of Shema* (but does so afterwards) is certainly not considered false testimony, Heaven forbid.

**F.** And in more detail:

Among the well-known explanations for this—in the name of the Chozeh of Lublin—is that since they said that *Shema* without *tefillin* is like a sacrifice without libations, just as one can bring the libations *after the sacrifice* (for we hold that a person may bring his sacrifice today and his libations tomorrow, up to ten days), so too can one don *tefillin* *after Shema*, and it is not considered false testimony.

However, this explanation is not entirely clear, for *Shema* and *tefillin* are not exactly like a sacrifice and libations. In the case of a sacrifice and libations, one can bring the libations even tomorrow, whereas with *Shema* and *tefillin*, one cannot postpone donning *tefillin* until tomorrow. If he does not don *tefillin* *on that very day*, it would surely be considered false testimony.

And one cannot answer that with *tefillin* there is a *side reason* that prevents combining the donning of *tefillin*

#### 16

of tomorrow with the *Shema* of the previous day (so that it not be considered false testimony) because of the interruption of the night, which is not a time for *tefillin*, because:

Even the libations have a time only during the day and not at night, just as sacrifices are only offered during the day, as it is said, "on the day He commanded." Nevertheless, the night does not interrupt the combining of the libations with the sacrifice of the previous day. Why is *tefillin* different?

And furthermore: regarding libations, the obligation to bring them during the day and not at night is from the Torah, and yet the night is not considered an interruption to the combining of the libations with the sacrifice. How much more so with *tefillin*, which from the Torah are permitted even at night, except that the Sages decreed not to don *tefillin* at night (lest one sleep in them). Certainly, the night is not considered an interruption to the combining of *tefillin* with *Shema*.

**G.** It can be said that the explanation for this is:

The definition of false testimony in *Shema* without *tefillin* is only *when one nullifies* the *mitzvah* of *tefillin* (i.e., he does not don them at all). But one who *does not fulfill* the *mitzvah* of *tefillin at the time of Shema* (but dons them after a while) is not considered false testimony, because *he does not nullify* the *mitzvah* of *tefillin*, since *he fulfills it* after a while.

According to this, it is well-understood why donning *tefillin* tomorrow does not help for the *Shema* of the previous day so that it not be considered false testimony—because since the entire day passed and he did not don *tefillin*, he has *nullified* the *mitzvah* of *tefillin* [and it is obvious that the fact that from the Torah the night is a time for *tefillin* does not help, because since in practice he cannot don *tefillin* at night, he has nullified the *mitzvah* of *tefillin* from the Torah, even if the reason he did not don them at night is a decree of the Sages], and consequently the *Shema* is false testimony.

**H.** There is another aspect to the reason for the custom of the "small *Shema*" specifically without *tefillin* (in addition to what was said *that it is possible* to recite *Shema* without *tefillin* and it is not considered false testimony)—as explained by the Rebbe of Munkatch, that there is a level of holiness in *tefillin* that is

#### 17

in *asiyah* (action) relative to *Shema*, which is (in thought and) in speech (as mentioned in the *maamar*). Therefore, one must recite *Shema* first and afterwards don *tefillin*, because "one ascends in matters of holiness."

By way of introduction: We find this regarding *tzitzis* and *tefillin*. Since the holiness of *tefillin* is greater than the holiness of *tzitzis* [for *tefillin* are "articles of holiness," whereas *tzitzis* are only "articles of a *mitzvah*"; and also: *tefillin* is a *mitzvah* that "a person is obligated to strive and pursue until he performs it," whereas regarding *tzitzis*, "a person is not obligated to buy himself a garment (that is obligated in *tzitzis*) and wrap himself in it so that he can put *tzitzis* on it," but rather he may walk in a garment that is not obligated in *tzitzis*], therefore the order is that "after he has donned the *tzitzis*-clad *tallis*, he should don *tefillin*, *for one ascends in matters of holiness*."

Regarding the objection raised by the later authorities that the rule of "one ascends in matters of holiness" applies only to a single *mitzvah* [like the Chanukah lamp, where "on the first day he lights one, and from then on he adds and goes... for one ascends in matters of holiness and does not descend," and similarly with the *lechem hapanim*], whereas in a case like this where there are two *mitzvos*, we do not say "one ascends in matters of holiness," but rather *the opposite*, that "whatever is more holy than its fellow *precedes* its fellow"—it has already been answered that since both (the *tallis* and the *tefillin*) are on the person’s body, which is sanctified through them, the principle of "ascending in matters of holiness" applies well: first he dons the *tallis* on his body, and afterwards he adds and dons the *tefillin* on his body, through which the person’s holiness is increased.

Similarly, it is understood regarding *Shema* and *tefillin* that even though they are two *mitzvos*, the principle of "ascending in matters of holiness" applies to them, since through them holiness is drawn down upon the person. Therefore, the order must be *Shema* and afterwards *tefillin*, beginning with the holiness of *Shema*, and afterwards adding the more elevated holiness of *tefillin*.

**I.** Now, all the aforementioned discussion is regarding *Shema* *before* donning *tefillin*. But after one has already donned *tefillin*, he can certainly recite *Shema* throughout the day even without *tefillin*, and there is no concern of false testimony.

#### 18

However, we find in the custom of the *Geonim* that they would recite even the *Shema* *of Maariv* while wearing *tefillin*, and at the conclusion of the second paragraph (after the verse "and you shall bind them," etc.) they would remove the *tefillin* [meaning, even though their custom was to pray *Maariv* while it was still day (as found in *Tosafos*), nevertheless, they did not want the *tefillin* to be on them during the entire *Maariv* prayer, since it belongs to the night, which is not a time for *tefillin*]. It must be that even for the *Shema of Maariv* (after they had already fulfilled the *mitzvah* of *tefillin*), they were concerned about the matter of false testimony in *Shema* without *tefillin*, to the point that they preferred to interrupt the middle of *Shema* (between one paragraph and another) for the sake of removing the *tefillin* rather than recite *Shema* without *tefillin*!

Similarly, we find that the *Chasam Sofer* was accustomed not to recite *Shema* while wearing *tefillin* of Rabbeinu Tam, because if the *halacha* is according to Rashi (as is the custom of the world), then *tefillin* of Rabbeinu Tam are not considered *tefillin*, and if he recites *Shema* while wearing them, it is *Shema* without *tefillin*. From this it is proven that the concern of false testimony in *Shema* without *tefillin* exists even after fulfilling the *mitzvah* of *tefillin*.

The explanation for this is that the concern of false testimony in *Shema* without *tefillin* even after donning *tefillin* exists only according to the opinion that the *mitzvah* of *tefillin* is (not only *to don* the *tefillin*, and the moment he dons them, he has fulfilled the *mitzvah*, but) "*to have tefillin* on the head," and every moment the *tefillin* are on him, he is fulfilling the *mitzvah* of *tefillin*. Therefore, even after he has already donned *tefillin*, there is a concern of false testimony if he recites *Shema* without *tefillin*, due to the nullification of the *mitzvah* *at that moment*.

From this it is understood that all of this applies only to the generations before us, when they were accustomed to sit in *tefillin* all day. In our days, however, when the custom is not to don them all day (because one requires a clean body and not everyone can be careful not to pass gas while wearing them), after donning *tefillin*, there is no longer a concern of false testimony in *Shema* without *tefillin*. Therefore, even when we pray *Maariv* while it is still day, we do not recite the *Shema of Maariv* while wearing *tefillin* (unlike in the time of the *Geonim*), and we are accustomed to recite *Shema* even while wearing *tefillin* of Rabbeinu Tam (and similarly *tefillin* of Ra'avad and Shimusha Rabba—for those who are accustomed to don them), as instructed by our Rebbes (unlike the *Chasam Sofer*).

#### 19

**J.** It should be added regarding this—according to the inner dimension of these matters:

The reason that in our days we do not don *tefillin* all day is due to the descent of the generations—"the generations have improved" (in irony)—that we do not have the strength to effect an additional drawing down beyond the drawing down that has already been effected by the act of donning *tefillin*, unlike in the time of the *Geonim*, when at every moment they would effect a new drawing down.

And although the reason according to the revealed Torah is due to the caution of a "clean body" (as mentioned above), the very fact that because of this concern one refrains from donning *tefillin* all day is because, in any case, we cannot add and effect a new drawing down by having the *tefillin* on us throughout the entire day.

[An example of this: It is explained in *Chassidus* that the reason we do not blow the *shofar* on Rosh Hashanah that falls on Shabbos is because the drawing down of the *shofar* blowing is effected by the day of Shabbos. However, at the time the *Beis Hamikdash* existed, they would effect through the *shofar* blowing a new drawing down, more elevated than the drawing down effected by the day of Shabbos. In this time, we do not have the strength to effect a drawing down more elevated than the drawing down effected by the day of Shabbos. That is to say: even though the reason according to the revealed Torah is due to the decree of Rabbah, the very fact that because of the decree of Rabbah one refrains from fulfilling the *mitzvah* of *shofar* blowing (for seemingly, how could a positive commandment from the Torah be pushed aside due to the decree of Rabbah?), is because, in any case, we do not have the strength to effect through *shofar* blowing a drawing down greater than the drawing down of Shabbos.]

It should be added that this matter also touches upon individuals of unique stature who, even in this time, have the power and ability to effect an additional drawing down by donning *tefillin* all day, as in the time of the *Geonim*.

By way of introduction: the saying of the Tzemach Tzedek regarding the Alter Rebbe (and from this it is understood also regarding our Rebbes who succeeded him), that had he lived in the time of the *Tannaim*, he would have been among the greatest of the *Tannaim*, and had he lived in the time of the Prophets, he would have been among the greatest of the Prophets. From his own essence, he was on the level of a *Tanna* and a Prophet, but since the generation was not worthy, this was in a state of concealment.

From this it is understood that our Rebbes could have effected a new drawing down by donning *tefillin* all day, as in the time of the *Geonim*. And nevertheless, it is known that even they

#### 20

were not accustomed to sit in *tefillin* all day, because the generation was not worthy and fit for it ("*m'ken nit durkhshparn di khomriyus fun dem dor*").

**K.** From the above, there is a lesson and instruction regarding the order of *avodah* with oneself and with others—which are analogous to the two matters of *Shema* (ascent, elevation, *avodah* with oneself) and *tefillin* (libations, drawing down, *avodah* with others).

By way of introduction:

There are those who have been appointed to matters of public communal work, and in the course of their work, they began to feel great pleasure ("*a groisn geshmak*") in the work, and they became so immersed in it ("*zei hobn zich oif azoi fil areingelozt in dem*") that they completely forgot about themselves...

—The "*geshmak*" in public communal work is actually the fact that by being a "public activist," one is exempt from thinking about himself, his standing, his state, etc., and certainly he does not need to think about the nullification of himself (and also not about the nullification of another, since he has "servants" and "servants of servants" to do it for him)...

And regarding this comes the instruction from the order of conduct of *Shema* and *tefillin*—that despite the greatness of the level of *tefillin*, and even though their level is greater than that of *Shema*, one does not don *tefillin* all day, and not only that, but one precedes *Shema* to *tefillin*:

a) Despite the greatness of the level of public communal work, one must *limit* the time dedicated to this work so that it is in accordance with what he was commanded (one hour, two, three, or four hours, and the like). For during this time, he effects everything he is able to effect, and consequently, he should not invest additional time in it, because through that, nothing will be added to his accomplishment (just as donning *tefillin* is for a limited time, because one cannot add to the drawing down of *tefillin* by donning them all day).

b) Simultaneously with his activity in public communal work, he must also think about himself, as it is written, "and do not hide yourself from your own flesh," meaning, to occupy himself with the refinement of himself, to immerse in a *mikveh* ("*gein in vaser*"), to study *Chassidus*, and to *daven* at length, etc. In general, as the saying of the *Chassid* R. Gershon Dov, the "tomorrow" must be in an entirely different manner. Moreover, the thought about himself is *the preface* (analogous to the preface of *Shema* to *tefillin*) through which the communal work will be *as it should be*, so that he will not

#### 21

come to a state where he becomes so immersed in the work that he completely forgets about himself ("*er vet nit visn fun zein leib un lebn*").

**L.** It should be added that this matter is also hinted at in the *maamar* "It is enough for the servant to be like his master":

In addition to what was said above (Section B) that "a person must humble himself and say, 'It is enough for the servant to be like his master,' and to make a dwelling place for Him, to be a dwelling for Him in the lower worlds" through involvement with others—this *avodah* itself must be from the knowledge and recognition that this is the *avodah* of *the servant to his master*. That is, he does not do it because of his own "*geshmak*" in public communal work, but solely because of the command and instruction of *his master*, "to be like his master."

And in more detail—one must know that from his own perspective, he is in the standing and state of "a servant finds freedom attractive," and from the nature of this freedom, he has many desires, etc., to the point that the nature of this freedom effects a change in his intellect and senses. Consequently, it is possible that from the nature of this freedom, since he does not desire to occupy himself with himself, he will choose for himself the work of public communal work in which he has a "*geshmak*." Therefore, there is a need for caution not to rely on oneself, but "to be like his master," to act according to his instruction, etc.

The test that his *avodah* is not because of his own "*geshmak*" but because of the command and instruction of his master is when he feels that the work with others is a matter of *descending below*, and from his own perspective *he does not desire* this descent. On the contrary, "the lower waters *weep*, 'we desire to be before the King'" (as we find in connection to *nesachim*, whose matter is a drawing down below). Rather, he is forced to descend below because of the command and instruction of his master.

And specifically when his *avodah* is in such a manner (that he feels the matter of "the lower waters *weep*," etc.), then the ascent above becomes greater—as hinted at by the fact that "b-k-y-m" (weeping) are the initials of "Blessed be the glory of Hashem from His place," which draws down the glory of Hashem from its source and root [even though when angels say this, it is in a manner where they do not grasp, etc.].

#### 22

And through this, that his *avodah* will be "*to be* like his master," then ultimately he "*will be* like his master," and not only "*like* his master," with the *kaf* of comparison, but more than that: his entire existence becomes the existence of the master. That is, instead of being initially in the state of "a servant finds freedom attractive" (which is the concept of a Canaanite servant), he becomes a servant whose entire existence is nothing but the existence of the Master (the master), which is the concept of a "faithful servant," similar to the Master who is called a "faithful servant," and of whom it is said, "In all My house he is faithful."

## B's"D. Shabbos Parshas Chukas-Balak, 12 Tammuz, 5712

#### 26

"Who can count the dust of Yaakov, and number the fourth part of Yisrael?" [1] We must understand the significance of the change in terminology between the first and second halves of the verse: the first half uses the term "count" (*manah*), while the second uses "number" (*mispar*). Similarly, the first half uses the title "Yaakov," while the second uses "Yisrael." This implies that the term "counting" relates to the title "Yaakov," while the term "numbering" relates to the title "Yisrael." [2] We must understand the meaning of this. Furthermore, we must understand the general concept: the verse implies that there is great merit in the fact that Yisrael are a vast multitude, beyond number. Yet, seemingly, it is written, [3] "Hashem did not desire you and choose you because you were more numerous than all the nations, for you are the fewest of all the nations." This implies that the merit lies specifically in their being few—"the fewest of all the nations."

This can be understood by prefacing the explanation given by my revered father-in-law, the Rebbe, in the *maamar* titled *Min HaMeitzar* [4] regarding the verse, [5] "From the straits I called to *Y-ah*; He answered me in the expanse of *Y-ah*." When a person is in a state of straits (*meitzar*)—and the *maamar* details various aspects of this—he calls out to Him, blessed be He, in prayer; "I called" (*karasi*) refers to prayer. [6] This prayer is directed specifically to *Y-ah*. He continues there to explain the verse, [7] "Trust in Hashem forever (*ad ad*), for in *Y-ah* Hashem is the Rock of worlds." The meaning is that trust in Hashem must be *ad ad*—up to the level of *Ad* (Eternity). The reason for this is "for in *Y-ah* Hashem is the Rock of worlds." The implication is that while there are various levels of trust, because "in *Y-ah* Hashem is the Rock of worlds," the trust of created beings in Hashem must reach the level and degree of *Ad*.

#### 27

The explanation of this is [8] that the general concept of trust in Hashem is rooted in Hashem’s love for Yisrael, as it is written, [9] "I have loved you, says Hashem." Therefore, a person trusts that Hashem will take him out of the straits, bring him to the true expanse, and provide for all his needs. However, since the root of this trust is in the *middah* of *Chesed* and the love from Above, and since *Chesed* is only one "line," it is possible for there to be opposition from the *middah* of *Gevurah*. Consequently, it is not certain that the influence originating from *Chesed* will be drawn down below. Therefore, trust rooted in this level is not perfect. In general, trust rooted in the *middos* from Above is not complete. But when trust stems from the *mochin* (intellect) from Above, the trust is more powerful. Because of love as it exists in the *mochin* from Above—especially in the *mochin d'gadlus*—there is no room for the opposition of *Gevurah*. Furthermore, *mochin* are generally in the category of *Rachamim* (mercy) relative to the *middos*; therefore, trust at this level is more powerful.

This can be understood through a parable from what we see below: when a person loves his friend, if the cause of the love is only the *middos* of the heart, then if the friend does not fulfill his will—and certainly if he does the opposite—this will not only weaken the love, but may even cause the opposite of love. However, when the love stems from the *mochin*, then even if the friend does the opposite of his will—where, from the perspective of the *middos* of the heart, there is no reason to love him—nevertheless, because of the *mochin*, he can make the calculation of "Do not judge your friend until you reach his place." [10] He puts himself into the other's situation, understands the reason for his actions, and judges him favorably. Moreover, when he puts himself into the other's situation properly, he is filled with mercy for him, which adds to the love. From this, we can understand the parallel Above: the love of Hashem for Yisrael, as it exists from the perspective of the *middos* from Above, requires Yisrael to be a vessel for it. The vessel for this is the love of Hashem that Yisrael possess; the love from below is a vessel for the love from Above. When the love from below is absent—and certainly when there is the opposite of love—the influence from the love Above will not be drawn down. However, at the level of love from Above as it exists from the perspective of the *mochin*, even when the love

#### 28

from below is absent, the love from Above is still drawn down. From all this, we can understand the matter of trust: when trust is from the *middah* of love that stems from the *mochin* from Above, the trust is more powerful.

Yet, in truth, even trust from the level of *mochin* is not perfect, for even from the perspective of *mochin*, it is not necessarily certain that the influence will be drawn down. As in the aforementioned parable of "Do not judge your friend until you reach his place," it is possible that even if he fulfills this command, he may find no merit in him, and then, even from the perspective of *mochin*, there will be no influence. Thus, even from the perspective of *mochin*, the trust is not absolutely perfect, for he is not certain that the influence will be forthcoming. However, when the trust is from a level above *mochin* and above *middos*, then it is truly perfect trust, for he is guaranteed and certain that the influence will be drawn down.

This is the meaning of "Trust in Hashem *ad ad*..." Based on the above, it is understood that this verse contains three interpretations. The first is that the trust is in *Ze'er Anpin*, the level of *middos*. The second is that it refers to the *mochin*, *Abba* and *Imma*. The third is that *ad ad* refers to *Keter*, the level of *Ratzon* (Will) and certainly *Taanug* (Delight). From this level, the trust is perfect, for he is assured with absolute certainty that the influence will surely be drawn down from there. This is like the love of a covenant and oath between two lovers who swear and forge a covenant with each other. The essence of the covenant and oath is that even if a situation arises where there is no room for love—neither in the feeling of the heart nor in the feeling of the intellect—they nevertheless forge a covenant that there will be no weakening of the love. [11] Such is Hashem’s love for Yisrael; the covenant and oath from Above mean that forever, in any situation or circumstance, there will be no weakening of Hashem’s love for Yisrael, as it is written, "I have loved you, says Hashem." When trust is in the state of essential love, the trust is perfect.

To understand this in the context of man’s *avodah*: the general concept of exiting from the straits to the expanse is rooted in the exile of Mitzrayim and the exodus from Mitzrayim, for in every generation, a person is obligated to see himself as if he personally went out of Mitzrayim. [12] This means that the exodus from Mitzrayim is the root of all redemptions from the straits to the expanse. [13] Regarding the exodus from Mitzrayim, it is written, [14] "The voice of my beloved, behold he comes, leaping over the mountains, skipping over the hills." The departure was by way of leaping and skipping. From this,

#### 29

it is understood regarding the exit from the straits to the expanse in man’s *avodah* that this exit is in the category of leaping. The general *avodah* of man is divided into three modes: standing (*amidah*), walking (*halichah*), and leaping (*dilug*). [15] The innovation of the exodus from Mitzrayim and the exit from the straits to the expanse is that the *avodah* is specifically in the category of leaping.

The explanation of this is [15] that the *avodah* of "standing" does not mean that he remains in one place—"today like yesterday, and tomorrow like today"—with no ascent or addition in his *avodah*, for then it is not called *avodah*. Rather, "standing" is a level in man’s *avodah*. From this, it is understood that "standing" also implies an ascent from level to level; if he always stands in one place, he is not a servant of Hashem, but rather "he has not served Him." [16] The reason this level of *avodah* is called "standing" is because the ascents between these levels are proportional to one another. This is why the angels are called "standing," as it is written, [17] "And I will give you walkers among these who stand." Similarly, souls as they are Above before their descent below are called "standing," as it is written, [18] "As Hashem lives, before Whom I stood." This is because the ascents of the angels and the souls Above are proportional to one another. Even though the camp of *Michael* performs its *avodah* with love and the camp of *Gavriel* with fear, [19] this *avodah* has ascents, yet the ascents are proportional to each other. In contrast, "walking" means an ascent to a level that is not proportional to the previous level.

To explain this in more detail: we see that there are two ways of achieving intellectual comprehension. The first way is going from the easy to the difficult; initially, a person learns a simpler subject, and afterwards, he learns a more elevated one. The better his grasp of the simple intellect, the more it helps him grasp the more elevated intellect better. But there is another way of ascending in levels of comprehension, as it is stated in the Gemara [20] that Rabbi Zeira fasted one hundred fasts (or forty fasts [21]) to forget the Talmud Bavli so that he could learn the Talmud Yerushalmi. From this, it is understood that the ascent from learning Talmud Bavli to learning Talmud Yerushalmi is not like the first way mentioned above. If it were like that way, not only would he not have needed to forget the Talmud

#### 30

Bavli, but on the contrary, his knowledge of the Talmud Bavli would have assisted him in learning the Talmud Yerushalmi. The fact that he needed to forget the Talmud Bavli proves that it is a different way. The reason for this is [22] that the order of the first way is only when the two levels of intellect are proportional to one another. But when the elevated intellect is not proportional to the simple intellect, then not only does the grasp of the lower intellect not assist the grasp of the higher, but on the contrary, the lower intellect confuses it. To be able to reach the grasp of the higher intellect, one must forget the lower one. This is similar to what is explained [23] regarding the soul’s ascent to *Gan Eden* to enjoy the radiance of the *Shechinah*: initially, it must forget the "sights of this world." "Sights of this world" does not mean forbidden desires or even permitted desires, but even the most subtle matters that it had in this world; the soul must forget even these to be able to enjoy the radiance of the *Shechinah*. The same applies to intellectual concepts that are not proportional to one another; the lower intellect confuses the higher one. Therefore, Rabbi Zeira needed to first forget what he learned in the Talmud Bavli to be able to reach the grasp of the Talmud Yerushalmi. With this, we can understand why an ascent from level to level that are proportional to one another is called "standing." Just as with intellectual concepts, the very fact that the lower intellect assists in understanding the higher intellect shows that the lower intellect is already present within the level of the higher intellect (for if it were not present there, it would not only fail to assist in its understanding, but would actually confuse it, as in the example of the Talmud Bavli and Talmud Yerushalmi). The reason is that because the lower intellect is proportional to the higher intellect, and they share a common element, it is therefore present within it. This is understood in the parallel *avodah* of man: when the ascent from level to level is to a level proportional to the lower one, the lower level is still present in the higher one. These ascents, which are proportional to one another, are called *hishtalshelus* (chain-like progression), like the links of a chain [24] where the upper link is connected to the link below it, and this is connected to the link below it, down to the lowest link. Thus, the lowest link is connected to the highest link. Therefore, when he ascends from level to level until he reaches the highest level, he is still proportional to the lower level, and the lower level is still found within him. This is why it is called "standing"—after all his ascents, he is still

#### 31

found at the lowest level; he has not departed from it, but still stands at that level.

The purpose of the soul’s descent into the body is for the soul to become a "walker." Before its descent, the soul is in the state of "standing" (as above), and by descending below, it becomes a "walker." This happens when it connects with the body through the *nefesh habahamit* (animal soul). By itself, the soul cannot connect to the body, but through the *nefesh habahamit*, it connects to the body. Then, it feels the straits of the body and the *nefesh habahamit*, which causes it to exit the straits and limitations of the body. This also causes it to exit the limitations of the *nefesh habahamit*, until the soul transcends its own limitations—the straits of its own self. Before its descent into the body, although it is also in a state of straits and limitation, since these are its own straits, it does not feel that it is in straits. But when it is clothed in the *nefesh habahamit* and the body, and it feels the straits of the body—which is a "foreign" straits—it then exits the straits of the body, and consequently, it also exits its own straits and becomes a "walker."

The explanation is that the straits of the soul itself are the limitations of intellect and *middos*. When it exits the limitation of intellect and *middos*, it is in the state of "walking." The *avodah* of intellect and *middos* is *avodah* according to reason and knowledge (*taam v'daas*). When it exits the limitation of intellect and *middos*, and the *avodah* is above *taam v'daas*, it is in the state of "walking." *Avodah* through *taam v'daas* is in the category of "standing," which is why the ascents in this are proportional. Regarding this, it is written, [25] "A multitude of years shall teach wisdom," meaning that what he reached at a certain level was by acquiring a multitude of wisdoms over all the years. Thus, in truth, even now he is still standing at his previous levels, which is why it is called "standing." But the *avodah* of "walking" is above *taam v'daas*—meaning he departs completely from *taam v'daas*—and therefore it is called "walking." This *avodah* above *taam v'daas* is the purpose of the soul’s descent below, for the purpose of *avodah* is to be in the state of "walking."

Just as it is in the mode of *avodah*, so it is with the influences from Above that are drawn down through man’s *avodah*. Through *avodah* according to *taam v'daas*, he reaches the level of *Ze'er Anpin*, which is from the term *ze'irus* (smallness) and *tzimtzum* (contraction), [26] meaning that the influences are drawn down according to the contraction of the "line of measurement." In general, this is the concept of the light

#### 32

that fills all worlds (*Memaleh Kol Almin*), which is proportional to the worlds, to the point that it is possible for the *Sitra Achra* to have a hold on it as well. But through *avodah* above *taam v'daas*, one reaches the level of *Keter*, which is above the worlds, the level of the light that surrounds all worlds (*Sovev Kol Almin*).

However, even the *avodah* above *taam v'daas*, although called "walking," still has some connection to *taam v'daas*. For the very fact that he reaches *avodah* above *taam v'daas* is because he was obligated to do so by his grasp in *taam v'daas*, because he grasps that he must exit *taam v'daas*. Thus, even his *avodah* above *taam v'daas* is from the obligation of his intellect. Even though this grasp is a "grasp of negation," a grasp of negation is still a grasp. If so, even while he is in *avodah* above *taam v'daas*, he has some connection to intellect and *taam v'daas*. Furthermore, the fact that he is above *taam v'daas* is not in a way that he is completely detached from *taam v'daas* and has no connection to intellect; on the contrary, he is essentially connected to intellect, but because of the obligation of his intellect, he exits *taam v'daas*. Thus, even as he is above *taam v'daas*, it is not that he has no connection to intellect at all, but rather that he is "exiting from intellect," so he has a connection to *taam v'daas* by way of negation at the very least. Consequently, for every *avodah* he performs, he is essentially connected to *taam v'daas* and must make a calculation to exit *taam v'daas*. Thus, in truth, even *avodah* above *taam v'daas*—the *avodah* of "walking"—has a connection to *taam v'daas*. Moreover, even in this *avodah* itself, the *avodah* is by way of war, as he fights with himself to exit *taam v'daas*. Therefore, it is possible that even in this *avodah*, "one nation shall be stronger than the other." [27]

Similarly, this is understood regarding the influences from Above that come through *avodah*. Although through the *avodah* of "walking" a person reaches the level of *Keter*, which is above the worlds—the light of *Sovev Kol Almin* (as above)—nevertheless, even *Keter* has some connection to the worlds. Although it surrounds and encompasses, it is still "surrounding the worlds." [28] Even though the level of *Sovev* is not proportional to the worlds, it still has some connection to the worlds—namely, the very fact that it is not proportional to the worlds, which is a connection by way of negation. Since it is connected to the worlds, it is possible for the *Chitzonim* (external forces) to draw sustenance from it. Just as with *Ze'er Anpin*, which is in the state of contraction according to the line of measurement, it is possible for the *Chitzonim* to draw sustenance from it, so too with the level of *Keter*. Even though it is the root of the *Atzilut* and above contraction, nevertheless, since it is the root of the *Atzilut*—meaning that it contains

#### 33

the level of the *Atzilut* as they are in their root—it is connected to the worlds, and it is possible for there to be sustenance drawn from it.

From all this, it is understood why even *avodah* in the level of "walking" is not sufficient. Although from the perspective of the soul alone, it is sufficient to be in the level of "walking," nevertheless, from the perspective of the soul’s clothing in the *nefesh habahamit* and the body—from which it is possible for "one nation to be stronger than the other" (even in *avodah* above *taam v'daas*, as above), and it is also possible for the *Chitzonim* to draw sustenance from it—therefore, there must be the *avodah* of "leaping," which is above even the *avodah* of "walking."

The explanation is that the *avodah* of "leaping" is exiting *taam v'daas* completely. This means it is not a matter of "argument" for him, but he exits it entirely, like a person fleeing from death to life, which is not by way of argument. This is the *avodah* of *Teshuvah*, which is by way of leaping. [29] As the Rambam writes, [30] that the matter of *Teshuvah* is such that yesterday he was separated from Hashem, etc., and today he is attached to the *Shechinah*. The *avodah* of *Teshuvah* is "in one hour and in one moment," [31] because its essence is a change of nature. *Avodah* by way of "walking" is not a change of nature; since his being above *taam v'daas* is because of the obligation of his intellect, it is not a change of nature, but rather that as he is in his nature, he ascends to the *avodah* above *taam v'daas*. In contrast, the *avodah* of *Teshuvah* is not by way of argument and intellect, but by way of flight, which is a change of nature. This is the content of "leaping"—it is a change of nature. As explained in *Likutei Torah* [32] regarding the three modes of *avodah*—standing, walking, and leaping—standing is standing on the earth with both feet; walking is that even if one foot is in the air, one foot is always on the earth; but leaping is that at that moment, both feet are above the earth. [33] This is the *avodah* of *Teshuvah*, a change of nature, which is completely above *taam v'daas*.

Similarly, this is understood regarding the influence from Above that comes through this *avodah*: the influence through the *avodah* of "leaping" is from the level of the *Pnimiyus* (inner dimension) of *Keter*. Through the *avodah* of "walking," one reaches the level of the *Chitzoniyus* (outer dimension) of *Keter*, *Arich Anpin*, while through the *avodah* of "leaping," one reaches the level of the *Pnimiyus* of *Keter*, *Atik*. *Arich Anpin*, although above *Atzilut*, is still the same category as *Ze'er Anpin*, only *Ze'er* is *Ze'er Anpin* (Small Face) and *Arich* is *Arich Anpin* (Long Face). It is like the difference brought in the *Midrash* [34] between small mirrors and large mirrors, which are still of the same type. Therefore, even from the level of *Arich*, the *Chitzoniyus* of *Keter*, it is possible for the *Chitzonim* to draw sustenance, as above. In contrast, the level of *Atik* is from the term *ha'takah* (transposition/removal), for it is removed and separated completely from the worlds, [35] where it is impossible for there to be any hold, etc.

With this, we can understand [36] why the exodus from Mitzrayim was specifically by way of leaping. Since in Mitzrayim they were submerged, etc., [37] if the influence had been from a level connected to the worlds—even from the level of *Sovev Kol Almin*—it would have been possible for there to be some hold, etc. Therefore, the influence was specifically from the *Atzmut* (Essence), which is through the *avodah* of "leaping," as the statement says, [38] "I, and not an angel; I, and not a seraph; I, and not a messenger; I, Hashem, I am He and no other." The "other" refers to that which is removed from the worlds—namely, the level of *Sovev Kol Almin*. But in the exodus from Mitzrayim, the revelation was from the level of "I am He and no other"—a revelation of the *Atzmut* that is above *Sovev Kol Almin*. As the statement says, [39] "The King of Kings, the Holy One, blessed be He, revealed Himself to them in His glory and His essence and redeemed them." My revered father-in-law, the Rebbe (Rashab), explains [40] that "in His glory" refers to *Malchus* of *Ein Sof*, and "in His essence" refers to the hidden *Tiferes*, and even higher, the *Atzmut* of Him, blessed be He. Specifically through the revelation of the *Atzmut*, in which there is no hold, was the exodus from Mitzrayim possible. The same applies to every straits; there must be a revelation of the *Atzmut* specifically, for then, and only then, is there an exit from the straits.

From all this, we can understand the verse, "Trust in Hashem *ad ad*..." that trust must be up to the level and degree of *Ad*. The word *ad* generally indicates a boundary—up to here is this matter, and from here on begins a more elevated matter. However, within this itself, there are two levels: *ad* and *ad* included (*ad v'ad bichlal*), and *ad* and *ad* not included (*ad v'lo ad bichlal*). [41] *Ad v'ad bichlal* is the level of *Sovev Kol Almin*; although it is in the category of *ad*—a boundary to the light of *Memaleh*—meaning it is above the light of *Memaleh*, nevertheless, it is in the category of *ad v'ad bichlal*, for *Sovev Kol Almin* is also connected to the worlds, as above. But the level of *ad v'lo ad bichlal* refers to the *Atzmut*

#### 35

that is completely above the worlds. This is "Trust in Hashem *ad ad*," as explained above, that the true essence of trust is at the level above *taam v'daas*, the level and degree of *Ad*. The essence of trust is in the level of *ad v'lo ad bichlal*, from the *Atzmut*, because in this, the trust is absolute, for there is no hold or grasp for the *Chitzonim*, and he is certain that the influence will be drawn down below. Then the trust is perfect, that there will be an exit from the straits to the expanse.

This is "Who can count the dust of Yaakov, and number the fourth part of Yisrael?" [42] The matter of "number the fourth part of Yisrael" means that they are without number ("Who can count their number?"), but nevertheless, they are in the category of a number, only there is no one to count them. This refers to the influence of *Sovev Kol Almin*, for although it is above the worlds, it still has a connection to the worlds, as above. Therefore, this influence is in the category of *Yisrael* (Yisrael), the letters of *Li Rosh* (to me is the head), [43] for the head also includes the skull, which is in the category of a *Makif* (surrounding) over the *mochin*. Although it is a *Makif*, it is a *Makif* over the *mochin*, similar to the light of *Sovev*, which although it surrounds, it "surrounds the worlds." This is also the concept of "the fourth part of Yisrael" (*rova Yisrael*); *rova* has two meanings: [44] one is from the word *arba'ah* (four), or that it is a fourth part. The number four refers to the four worlds of *ABYA*, because the light of *Sovev Kol Almin* has a connection to the worlds. But regarding the level of *Yaakov*, it is written, "Who can count the dust of Yaakov," for *Yaakov* is the letters of *Yud-Ekev* (the heel), [45] for the merit of the heel is in the matter of *mesiras nefesh* (self-sacrifice). Although *mesiras nefesh* exists in the entire body, it is more powerful in the foot in general, and in the heel of the foot in particular. [46] This is the concept of *Yaakov*, which is the *avodah* of *mesiras nefesh* and acceptance of the yoke (*kabbalas ol*) that is above *taam v'daas*. Through this, it is "the dust of Yaakov," for dust is one lump, which is above individual grains without number. Although grains without number have no number, they are still distinct and divided from one another, but dust is one lump. This alludes to the unity of "all of us as one" in a way that has no division at all. This is possible only through the *avodah* of *kabbalas ol* that is above *taam v'daas*. And although dust is lower than all the elements, [47] nevertheless, "everything was from the dust," [48] meaning that through *kabbalas ol*, one reaches all levels. Therefore, at this level, it is written "Who can count" (and the matter of the number is not mentioned), for it is stated in the *Zohar* [49] that *manah* (count) is a term for a gift—the drawing down of the *Atzmut*, which is specifically by way of a gift. This means that through the *avodah* of *kabbalas ol* and *mesiras nefesh*, the level of "leaping," one draws down the *Atzmut*.

#### 36

This is "From the straits I called to *Y-ah*; He answered me in the expanse of *Y-ah*," for specifically through the straits, one calls to *Y-ah*, which refers to the powers of *Chochmah* and *Binah* in the essence of the soul, [50] and through this, "He answered me in the expanse," the level of the essential expanse, [51] for through the *avodah* of "leaping" that comes from the straits (as above), one draws down the *Atzmut*. Therefore, in Mitzrayim, when Yisrael were in the straits, they had the *avodah* of "leaping," through which there was the drawing down of the *Atzmut* that is above the category of connection to the worlds, as above: "I, and not an angel; I, and not a seraph; I, and not a messenger; I, and not another." [38] Angel, seraph, messenger, and "other" are the four worlds of *ABYA*, [52] but in the exodus from Mitzrayim, through the *avodah* of "leaping" from the straits, there was the drawing down of the "I" that is above the category of connection to the worlds.

So it will be in the future redemption, that the redemption will be specifically from the *Atzmut*, and this revelation comes through the *avodah* of "leaping," which comes specifically from the straits. This matter the *Nesi'ei Yisrael* (leaders of Yisrael) took upon themselves; they had within them the matter of the straits—the sufferings of the body and the sufferings of the soul—so that through these straits, there would be "He answered me in the expanse," the revelation of the *Atzmut*, through which there will be the future redemption, which is a complete redemption after which there is no exile. [53] Since the revelation is from the *Atzmut*, in which there is no hold, etc., it will be a redemption after which there is no exile. If we had merited it, the exodus from Mitzrayim would have been in such a way, [54] but because [we did not merit it], there was exile afterwards. This is not the case in the future redemption, etc. [55]

**D.** Regarding [12](#_ftn188) the *mitzvah* of the *Parah Adumah* (Red Heifer) which is explained at the beginning of this week’s *parsha* (*Parshas Chukas*)—it is stated [13](#_ftn189): "This is the statute of the *Torah*."

Seemingly, this is difficult to understand: since the subject is a specific *mitzvah*—why is the expression "This is the statute of the *Torah*" used, rather than "This is the statute of the *Parah*" or something similar?

The explanation for this [14](#_ftn190) is that the *mitzvah* of *Parah Adumah* is a general matter that touches upon the entirety of the *Torah*, as follows.

**E.** The *mitzvah* of *Parah Adumah* comes to purify a person from *tumas meis* (impurity of the dead):

*Tumas meis* is the most severe form of impurity, as it is stated in the *Midrash* [15](#_ftn191) that it was specifically regarding *tumas meis* that Moshe Rabbeinu asked Hashem, "If one becomes impure, what is his purification?" to the point that "the face of Moshe darkened." The reason for this is that *kedushah* is *chayus* (vitality), while death (*rachmana litzlan*), which is the *opposite* of vitality, is the complete opposite of *kedushah*.

And this is the virtue of the *Parah Adumah*—that it purifies even from this severe impurity.

And regarding this it is written [13](#_ftn189), "And they shall take *unto you* a red heifer"—that the purification from this severe impurity through the *Parah Adumah* is specifically in the power of *Moshe Rabbeinu*. For although the slaughtering of

#### 40

the heifer, etc., was performed by Elazar the Kohen (its *mitzvah* is performed by a deputy) [16](#_ftn192), nevertheless, the *Torah* emphasizes, "And they shall take *unto you* a red heifer," "unto you" specifically, "it is always called *by your name*, the heifer that *Moshe* made in the wilderness" [17](#_ftn193).

And similarly regarding all the generations that followed—that in all the eight heifers that were made during the time of the Second Temple [18](#_ftn194), and also in the tenth heifer that "Melech HaMashiach will make" [19](#_ftn195), they used (to sanctify the Kohanim performing the heifer) the ashes of the heifer that Moshe made in the wilderness [20](#_ftn196).

**F.** And we find another matter regarding the *Parah Adumah*—that it "makes the pure impure and purifies the impure" [21](#_ftn197):

The concept here is that in order to purify a Jew who has become impure (*rachmana litzlan*) with the most severe impurity, there is a need for the element of *mesiras nefesh*, as is emphasized by the fact that the Kohen who performs the purification *becomes impure* in order to effect the purification.

And this *mesiras nefesh* is emphasized in Moshe Rabbeinu:

Moshe Rabbeinu’s involvement in the *Parah Adumah* that purifies from *tumas meis* constituted a *yeridah* (descent) for him, for Moshe Rabbeinu himself was completely *above* this matter, to the point that he wondered, "What is his purification?" and "his face darkened,"

— The explanation for this is that in order to rectify an undesirable matter, one must have some connection to it (as is known from the story regarding the Mitteler Rebbe [22](#_ftn198)). Since he had no connection whatsoever to *tumas meis*, not even in the slightest degree, he wondered and said, "What is his purification?" until "his face darkened"—

And nevertheless, in order to purify a Jew who became impure with a severe impurity, including ensuring ("to guarantee") the purification of a Jew who would exist many generations later (as mentioned, that the ashes of the heifer that Moshe made were used for all the heifers made throughout all the generations that followed)—Moshe Rabbeinu did not consider his own

#### 41

descent, and he exerted himself ("he threw himself into it") with *mesiras nefesh* in the matter of the *Parah Adumah*.

**G.** And therefore it is said regarding the *Parah Adumah*, "This is the statute of the *Torah*"—because it is a general matter that touches upon the entirety of the *Torah*:

When a Jew feels within himself a matter of *tumas meis* in a spiritual sense, meaning that he lacks vitality in matters of *Torah* and *mitzvos* [and it should be noted that in our time, "*we are all* impure with *tumas meis*" [23](#_ftn199)]—he may, *chas v'shalom*, come to a state of despair, etc.

Well, he must know that he should not despair, because Moshe Rabbeinu guaranteed his purification!

— Moshe Rabbeinu was indeed completely above this matter, but nevertheless, he gave his life to make the *Parah Adumah*, to ensure the purification of *Bnei Yisrael* in his generation and *Bnei Yisrael* in all the generations that followed, so that even if they become impure with the most severe impurity, they will be able to be purified from this impurity.

And therefore, in all times, even when Moshe Rabbeinu is not present ("when Moshe is not here"), one should not fall into despair because of the severity of the matter of *tumas meis*, because "the ashes of the heifer of Moshe *are never exhausted*" [24](#_ftn200), "yours *endures*" [25](#_ftn201).

And although we do not know where these ashes are—they exist, and when Mashiach comes, they will find them and use them for the tenth heifer, and consequently, "that which is lacking in time is not like that which is lacking in deed" [26](#_ftn202).

**H.** And so it is also regarding the *Baal HaGeulah*, the Rebbe, the Moshe of our generation,

— as the Sages state [27](#_ftn203), "An extension of Moshe in every generation," and not just an "extension of Moshe," but Moshe in his entirety ("the whole Moshe"), for in every generation it is the same Moshe ("the same Moshe"), only *the bodies* are divided [28](#_ftn204) in accordance with and relative to the people of the generation—

#### 42

who involved himself in all matters—even in simple matters—in order to purify *Bnei Yisrael*, up to the point of purification from matters that are akin to *tumas meis*!

— The Rebbe, from his own perspective, was completely above it and had no connection to these matters, not even in the slightest degree, and nevertheless, he lowered himself ("he lowered himself") into the simplest matters, a thing that for him was bound up with *actual mesiras nefesh*... And why so much? To *purify a Jew from tumas meis*!...

And therefore, even now one should not despair and fear anything, not even *tumas meis*, because even now his (the Moshe of our generation’s) ashes *endure*!

**"You have given a banner to those who fear You, to be displayed because of the truth, Selah."**[1] My revered father-in-law, the Rebbe, brings in his maamar[2] on this verse the explanation of the Targum (and other commentators) that "a banner to be displayed" refers to the matter of *nisyonos* (trials). The concept of a "banner to be displayed" implies that these trials effect an elevation in a person. The strength for this comes from Avraham Avinu, who is called by the name *Koshet* (Truth), for he was a man of truth. Thus, "because of the truth, Selah"—the strength for the *avodah* of trials comes from Avraham Avinu. This interpretation does not contradict the simple meaning of the verse, where "banner" refers to miracles. The verse "You have given a banner to those who fear You, to be displayed" implies that through the miracles that Hashem shows, He effects an elevation and a "displaying" in all of Israel, and especially in those who fear Hashem (*yireicha*). The root of this effect in the world is "because of the truth, Selah," meaning the aspect of the truth of *Elokus*, where the aspect of "the truth of Hashem is forever" is recognized.[3]

**To understand** the explanation of this matter—that through miracles, the aspect of "the truth of Hashem is forever" is drawn down—we must first preface the essence of the concept of miracles.[4] The simple definition of a miracle is the opposite of nature. Nature is the world following its established course, to the point where one might think that this "palace" has no leader. But through a miracle, whose nature is the overturning of the systems of nature and the changing of nature’s course, it awakens the person who contemplates it (and when the miracle is great, contemplation is not even necessary) that there is a Leader to this palace. But to understand more deeply the nature of the *hemshech* (drawing down) effected by miracles, the Gemara states:[5] "A person should not stand in a place of danger and say, 'They will perform a miracle for me,' for perhaps they will not perform a miracle for him, and if they do perform a miracle for him, they deduct it from his merits." From this it is understood that the matter of a miracle is the same as the matter of the reward for *mitzvos*; therefore, in place of the reward for *mitzvos*, they perform a miracle for him (and because of the performance of the miracle, they deduct from his reward for *mitzvos*).

**The content** of the matter of the reward for *mitzvos* can be understood based on what is written in *Emek HaMelech*,[6] that the intent of the creation of the world is to draw down, through Torah and *mitzvos*, the aspect of the *Ohr Ein Sof* that is before the *Tzimtzum*, so that it will shine in revelation, etc.[7] The creation of the worlds was through the first *Tzimtzum*, at which time a short and thin *Kav* (line) was drawn down,[8] but the ultimate intent is that through Torah and *mitzvos* they will draw down also the light that is before the *Tzimtzum*. In general, these two matters—the light of the *Kav* that is drawn down after the *Tzimtzum* and the *Ohr Ein Sof* before the *Tzimtzum*—are the two levels of *Memalei Kol Almin* (filling all worlds) and *Sovev Kol Almin* (surrounding all worlds).[9] The light of the *Kav* is the light that shines by way of *hishtalshelus* (the chain-like order of descent), meaning it is drawn down in order and gradation down to the lowest level. It is like the analogy of a chain, where the upper link is connected to the link below it, and that link is connected to the one below it, down to the lowest link. From this it is understood that the *Kav* contains a division of details, the division of "above" and "below,"[10] just as in a chain where each link is distinct in its level from the next. Furthermore, all the levels within the light of the *Kav* are relative to one another, as in the chain analogy where all the links are connected to each other. However, above *hishtalshelus*, and especially the *Ohr Ein Sof* before the *Tzimtzum*, there is no division of "above" and "below," and more deeply, there is no division of details at all there. This is the difference between the light[11] of *Memalei* and the light of *Sovev*.[12] The light of *Memalei* is an internal light, and because it is an internal light, even as it is drawn down into the worlds (into the *keilim*), it is drawn down internally and unites with them. Therefore, it contains a division of details, for it is drawn down into each world according to its level. From this it is also understood that this light is only in a state of revelation and is not an essential light, for every essence is indivisible.[13] Not so the light of *Sovev*, which is a surrounding light, meaning it is surrounding by its own nature, and it has no division into details—neither the division of "above" and "below" nor the division of "width."[14] Therefore, even as it is drawn down into the worlds, it is in the state of being a *Sovev* (surrounding) to the worlds. However, this does not mean it is only in a state of being "surrounding," but rather that it is present within the very interior of every created being internally. Yet, because it does not shine in revelation within the worlds—since it has no division and the worlds are in a state of division and the order of *hishtalshelus* (one below the other)—it is called *Sovev Kol Almin*.[15] And although the primary vitality of the worlds is from the light of the *Sovev*

#### 46

specifically,[16] nevertheless, the light of the *Sovev* must be in the worlds in a state of concealment. For if it were in revelation, there would be no *hishtalshelus* or worlds, and being in the category of "worlds," they cannot receive the revelation of the light of the *Sovev*. The light of the *Memalei* is in revelation within the worlds. And even though in this world we do not feel the light of the *Memalei*, the meaning of this is only that we do not know that this is Divine vitality; however, the created beings feel that they have a vitality that enlivens, creates, and sustains them.[17] And this is not just that they understand intellectually that they have vitality, but rather that the vitality is their very existence. Yet, they do not know the essence of the vitality; it seems to them that it is just "vitality," while in truth it is Divine vitality. And since the vitality is in revelation within them, only that due to the concealment and hiding of the material they do not know that it is Divine vitality, it is understood that through the *avodah* of refining the material, it is possible to feel that it is indeed Divine vitality. Since in essence the vitality is in revelation in the world, and only due to the concealment of the material do they not understand that the vitality is *Elokus*, it is understood that by refining the material, one feels that it is Divine vitality. A proof for this is what is written regarding Adam HaRishon before the sin, for it is stated in *Pirkei D'Rabbi Eliezer*[18] that Adam HaRishon said to all the created beings, "Come, let us bow down and kneel, let us bend the knee before Hashem our Maker,"[19] and the created beings were in a state of *bitul* (nullification) due to the aspect of "Hashem our Maker," which is the light of the *Memalei*. That is, the created beings, as they were in their existence, felt the light of the *Memalei*. And even now, the light of the *Memalei* is in revelation in the created beings, for they feel the vitality, but they err in their perception of the matter, for they do not know that it is Divine vitality. All this is specifically regarding the light of the *Memalei*, which is in revelation in the created beings (meaning it is internally within the created beings). Not so the light of the *Sovev*, which is in concealment, for the created beings, as they are in their existence, cannot endure the light of the *Sovev*, and if it were to be revealed within them, they would be nullified from their existence.

**Now,**[20] the matter of the reward for *mitzvos* is the drawing down of the light of the *Sovev*, as mentioned above (from what is written in *Emek HaMelech*), that through *mitzvos* one draws down the light that is before the *Tzimtzum*. And even though a surrounding light is drawn down upon the person who performs the *mitzvah*, as it is stated in *Tanya*,[21] and therefore one must stand before one who is engaged in a *mitzvah* (even if he is an *am ha'aretz*),[22] this is also entirely in concealment,

**And just as** it is in this world and in the worlds of *Beriah, Yetzirah, Asiyah*, that the light of the *Sovev* and the *hemshech* through Torah and *mitzvos* are in concealment, so too it is in the world of *Atzilus*. For in the future, the *Ikar Shechinah* (the essence of the Divine Presence) will be in the lower realms. The *Ikar Shechinah* is the essence of the *Atzmus* (the Essence), which is not the light of the *Memalei*, but the light of the *Sovev* and even higher, the *Atzmus* of the Holy One, blessed be He.[29]

The reason for this is that the light of the *Memalei* contains a division of details and is drawn down internally into the created beings; therefore, even through the refinement of the material, the light as it is below is not like it is above. **From all the above,** it is understood why the *hemshech* through miracles is a substitute for the *hemshech* of the reward for *mitzvos*. This means that even though one grasps that he has a Source that brings him into being, nevertheless, because he has no grasp of the essence of the Source, he calls it by the name *Ayin*. To the point that it is possible that the created being will not feel its Source at all, as mentioned above that nature can be in the manner of "He drowned them in the Red Sea," where the Source that brings into being is "drowned" in the created being until it does not feel its Source. However, *Da'as Elyon* is that creation is from *Yesh* to *Ayin*, that the *Yesh* is the true *Yesh* whose existence is from His Essence, and everything that was created is as nothing compared to Him, for "all is considered as nothing before Him." And below this, there is a level regarding which "all is considered as nothing (*k'lo*) before Him," with the *kaf* of comparison,[41] but nevertheless, it is still also the aspect of *Ayin*. Not so in *Da'as Tachton*; the *Yesh* is not a *Yesh* in truth, for it is a created *Yesh*, and its entire existence is only from the power of the One who brings it into being, and from itself it is no existence at all. **J.** The days of 12-13 Tammuz, the *Chag HaGeulah* of the Rebbe, are "days of *segulah*"—as we find this expression regarding a redemption similar to it, and all the more so that the days of redemption of the *Nasi* of the generation are certainly "days of *segulah*."

**K.** And by way of introduction to the explanation of the content of the matter of "*segulah*":

In a "*segulah*"—in contrast to "medicine" which is done in the way of nature—"it is not known to us how the healing comes about through doing this thing"[36], because, "there are matters that heal in the way of medicine and nature, and there are those that heal in the way of *segulah*, where according to the way of nature there is no way for healing to come about through this, and nevertheless there is some *segulah* in a distant and wondrous way in that which is not grasped by the comprehension and intellect of natural doctors to be the cause of this healing"[37]. That is to say, we do not understand the *property* in this thing that makes it a "*segulah*," but the sense testifies—*from the result* (the action of the thing)—that this is a "*segulah*." It turns out that a "*segulah* is a thing *that is above the grasp of the intellect*"[38].

And therefore we find a difference between medicine and *segulah*—in the laws of Shabbos:

#### 61

Regarding medicine in a place where there is a desecration of Shabbos—one relies on the opinion of the doctor[39] (and one also relies on the opinion of the patient, even *contrary* to the opinion of the doctor[40]), and in a place where there are differences of opinion—one follows the majority[41]. Not so regarding a "*segulah*"—as long as it is not known clearly and certainly that this is a *segulah* (a tested one, which has already healed others), it is forbidden to do it on Shabbos[42].

And the reason for this—because "medicine" is according to intellect, and therefore the order and manner of its action is determined according to the rules of intellect (the opinion of the doctor, following the majority, etc.). Not so a "*segulah*," which is above the intellect; the rules of intellect do not apply to it. That is to say, the boundaries of the intellect are not proof regarding a *segulah* which is above the intellect, and consequently, it is forbidden to use it on Shabbos until one knows with certainty that this is a *segulah*.

**L.** And this is also the relevance of a "*segulah*" to the Giving of the Torah, at which time all *Bnei Yisrael* were called by the name "*segulah*," as it is written[43], "And you shall be to Me a *segulah* from all the peoples..."—because, at the Giving of the Torah, a matter that is above the intellect was renewed among *Bnei Yisrael*.

By way of introduction:

The innovation of the Giving of the Torah is that at that time Hashem nullified the decree separating the higher realms and the lower realms, "And He said, the lower realms shall ascend to the higher realms, and the higher realms shall descend to the lower realms"[22]. That is, a connection and bond was made between spirituality ("higher realms") and physicality ("lower realms"), between the Torah and the reality of the world—that the Torah becomes the *master* over the world, to change the reality of the world and elevate it to the level of the Torah[44].

And this innovation that was made at the Giving of the Torah in the world at large, is accomplished in detail through the *avodah* of *Bnei Yisrael*—that through the fulfillment of Torah and *mitzvos*, a Jew accomplishes the refinement and purification of his portion in the world[45], and consequently he also elevates the world at large, as the ruling of the Rambam[46]: "Every person should see... the whole world as half meritorious and half guilty... he performed one mitzvah, behold he has tipped... the entire world to the side of merit and caused... for them salvation and rescue." It turns out that through the refinement of his portion in the world, he tips the world at large to the side of merit.

**M.** And this matter (the action of the Torah in the world through the *avodah* of *Bnei Yisrael*) is *above the intellect*, because:

According to intellect, that is, according to the *Seder Hishtalshelus*, there is no connection between physicality and spirituality. Therefore, until the Giving of the Torah, the decree was that "the people of Rome shall not descend to Syria, and the people of Syria shall not ascend to Rome"[22] (higher realms shall not descend to lower realms, and lower realms shall not ascend to higher realms). And only at the Giving of the Torah, when there was the revelation of the *Atzmus* (which is not defined at all by the definitions of the *Seder Hishtalshelus*), as it is written[47], "*Anochi* (I) Hashem your G-d..."—behold, through this specifically the decree was nullified[48], and a connection was made between spirituality and physicality, so that the Torah can act upon the world.

And in addition to the fact that the action of the Torah in the world at large is a matter that is above the intellect, behold, also the fact that the action of the Torah in the world is accomplished through the *avodah* of *Bnei Yisrael* is a matter that is above the intellect—for since *Bnei Yisrael* are "*the fewest* of all the peoples"[49], and not only that, but it is also so within the Jewish people themselves, that those who observe Torah and *mitzvos* are a *minority* relative to the rest of *Bnei Yisrael* (and as our Sages said[50], "Hashem saw that the righteous are *few*..."), and not only that, but among those who observe Torah and *mitzvos* themselves, most of their time goes to physical matters, and only a *minority* of their time is dedicated to spiritual matters[51]—how is it possible that with a *minority* of time dedicated to the fulfillment of Torah and *mitzvos* by a *minority* of *Bnei Yisrael* who observe Torah and *mitzvos*, they can breach ("*durchbrechen*") the concealment and hiding of the world at large, and elevate the world at large?!

Rather, this is a matter that is above the intellect, above the *Seder Hishtalshelus*—for, through the fulfillment of Torah and *mitzvos*, the *dveikus* of *Bnei Yisrael* with *Hashem* is accomplished [as it is written[52], "And you who cleave to Hashem your G-d...", *dveikus* specifically, that is, not only in the manner of a connection, which indicates two things that a connection is made between them, but in the manner of *dveikus*, that they become one thing literally[53]], as it is above the *Seder Hishtalshelus*.

And more specifically—that through the study of Torah, the *dveikus* of *Bnei Yisrael* with the level of "His life," the lights, is accomplished, and through the fulfillment of *mitzvos*, the *dveikus* of *Bnei Yisrael* with the level of "His limbs," the vessels, is accomplished[54]. And since "He and His life are one, He and His limbs are one"[55], therefore,

through the *dveikus* with the level of "His life" and "His limbs" (through Torah and *mitzvos*), the *dveikus* (also) with the level of "*He*" which is above "His life" and "His limbs" (lights and vessels), His essence and being, which is above the *Seder Hishtalshelus*, is accomplished.

**N.** And based on this, one must explain the continuation of the verses[56], "Hashem your G-d chose you to be for Him a *segulah* people... not because of your multitude... for you are *the fewest* of all the peoples":

According to intellect—there is no place for the action of refinement and elevation of the world by Israel who are "*the fewest* of all the peoples." But from the perspective of the matter of *segulah* ("He chose you... to be for Him a *segulah* people") which is above the intellect—this is above the limitations that are from the intellect, such as majority and minority. Thus, even in being "*the fewest* of all the peoples," *Bnei Yisrael* accomplish the refinement and elevation of the world.

That is to say: A matter that is according to intellect is limited by the limitations of the intellect. Consequently, if there is room in the intellect that this thing can accomplish a certain action—then it has the power to accomplish it, and if there is no room for this in the intellect—it does not have the power to accomplish it. Not so a "*segulah*"—since its entire matter is above the intellect, for the intellect has no concept of the essence of this *segulah*, the limitations of the intellect do not apply to it, such as the limitation of majority and minority, etc. Rather, the power of this *segulah* is above limitation, above to infinity and below to infinity[57].

And in the language of *halacha*—the status of the reality of *Bnei Yisrael* is "*kavua*" (fixed)[58] [just as Hashem is *kavua*, as is understood from our Sages[59], "He is the place of the world"], and "a *kavua* is not nullified"[60]. That is, in a "*kavua*" one does not take into account majority and minority, etc.

**O.** And according to what was said above (Section E) that all matters of *Bnei Yisrael* are drawn down from the Patriarchs—also the matter of "*segulah*" that was renewed in *Bnei Yisrael* at the Giving of the Torah is through the power of the Patriarchs:

"The three Patriarchs are called *segulah*... and this is what is written, 'And you shall be to Me a *segulah* from all the peoples'"[61]. "*Segulah* is also from the matter of *segol*, which are three

dots, which are *Chagat*, which are the level of the three Patriarchs... when they are held together"[64]. "And this is the inclusion of *Chagat*"[65], as hinted in the image of the "*segol*" (or "*segolta*")[66]—three dots *together*, "the level of right, left, and middle, where the middle line is the one that connects and also unifies the two lines of right and left to be included together, etc."[67].

And the matter in this:

The innovation in the image of the three dots of "*segol*" relative to the image of the three dots of "*shuruk*"[68]—is that the three dots of "*shuruk*" are *one below the other*[69]. That is, the three lines, *Chesed, Gevurah,* and *Tiferes*, are (not at once, but) *one after the other*. Not so the three dots of *segol*, which are *together*. That is, the inclusion of the three lines, *Chesed, Gevurah,* and *Tiferes*, *together*.

And the difference between them—that the three lines one after the other is a matter that is according to intellect and limitation, and it also exists in the matters of the world. And simply, that at a certain time a person is in a movement of *Chesed*, and afterwards he is in a movement of *Gevurah*.

But the three lines together (*segol*) is a matter *above* the intellect and above limitation—a matter of "*segulah*"—because: according to intellect and limitation, it is impossible for opposite lines to be together, for at the time that a person is in a movement of *Chesed*, he cannot be in a movement of *Gevurah*, and at the time that he is in a movement of *Gevurah*, he cannot be in a movement of *Chesed*. And only in matters of holiness, that is, in matters of G-dliness, can all the lines be together—because holiness and G-dliness are *above limitations*, and in the well-known expression: "The impossible of the impossibles."

– This matter is one of the innovations of the teachings of Chassidus, for in the books of philosophy we find debate on the matter, and according to many "the impossible has a fixed nature in the bosom of the Creator"[70], whereas in the teachings of Chassidus it is a simple matter that Hashem is "the impossible of the impossibles"[71].

It turns out that also the matter of the "*segulah*" at the Giving of the Torah is through the power of the Patriarchs—because the Patriarchs bequeathed to *Bnei Yisrael* the three lines of *Chesed, Gevurah,* and *Tiferes* all together, which is a matter above limitation, "*segulah*."

**Now,** it is the same regarding the level of the *keilim*, that they are existent with an intrinsic existence; this is "His Name is existent." The matter is that the concept of the *keilim* in general, and especially the *keilim* of *tikkun*, is that they unite with the light that is enclothed within them. Since the light is "from the *ma'or*" whose reality is from His Essence, therefore also in the *keilim* there exists the matter of "its reality is from His Essence," that they are existent with an intrinsic existence, because they are united with the light. It is the same regarding the souls that were brought into being from the *oros* and the *keilim*, that they too are existent with an intrinsic existence; this is "His throne is established," as above.

**The reason** that the *oros*, *keilim*, and souls of holiness are existent with an intrinsic existence is because they contain the element of *bitul*, and through *bitul* they are unified with the *Atzmus*, as the statement, "He, His life, and His essence are one." It can be said that "He" refers to the light, "His life" are the *keilim*, and "His essence" are the souls, and all of them are one—that is, in unity with the *Atzmus*. For the light is always attached and unified with its source,

#### 91

just like the light of the sun; when there is something that interrupts between the light and the sun, the light is nullified, because the entire reality of the light is when it is unified with the *ma'or*. It is the same Above, that the entire reality of the light is that it is a revelation of the *Atzmus*, and because it is in *bitul* and unity with the *ma'or*, it is therefore existent with an intrinsic existence. It is the same also with the *keilim*, which are nullified to the light that is enclothed within them, which causes them to be existent with an intrinsic existence. It is the same also with the souls, which also contain the element of intrinsic *bitul* to His Essence, may He be blessed, which causes them to be existent with an intrinsic existence. As it is stated in the Midrash on the verse, "Hashem is my portion, says my soul," a parable of a king who entered a province with his dukes and governors; one chose a duke for himself, and another chose a governor. There was one wise man there who said, "I will choose the king." "Why?" "Because all of them are replaceable, but the king is not replaceable." The concept of "the king is not replaceable" refers to the *Atzmus*, which is existent with an intrinsic existence and its reality is from His Essence. And from the *bitul* of Israel to His Essence, may He be blessed—"Hashem is my portion, says my soul"—they also possess the element of intrinsic existence and reality from His Essence. This is "Hashem is my portion, says my soul," for since it is a portion of Hashem, and a portion is the very same thing as the essence, only that it is just a part of it, it is therefore in intrinsic existence like the essence.

**However,** all this is specifically in holiness, for in holiness there is *bitul* and unity. Whereas in the *sitra achra*, although they also have divine vitality, nevertheless, because they are not nullified to Divinity and, on the contrary, they oppose Divinity, the divine vitality does not cause them to be existent with an intrinsic existence. From the perspective of their lack of *bitul*, the divine vitality is in a manner of *makif* (encompassing) upon them, and what is drawn into them internally is in a state of exile within them. Whereas in holiness, not only is the divine vitality in holiness not in a state of exile, but it is also not in the state of "bread of shame" (light drawn without *avodah*), for they have *avodah* and *bitul* to Divinity. Whereas in the *sitra achra*, where the *kelipos* are the opposite of His will, may He be blessed, the vitality in them is in a state of exile, and all their vitality is what they receive through the act of the sin.

**The explanation of the matter** in more detail: The difference between holiness and *kelipos* is in three matters: in the root of their creation, in their creation, and in their existence. The difference in the root of their creation is that everything that was brought into being is from the perspective of His will, may He be blessed. Even the *kelipos* and *sitra achra*, which are the opposite of the will, were also brought into being from the perspective of His will, may He be blessed—that is, from the perspective of

#### 92

"that which is judgment is not pleasing to Me," meaning that there was a revelation of the will regarding the *kelipos*, and from this perspective they were brought into being. However, because the will is "that which is judgment is not pleasing to Me," meaning that there should be no reality to the *kelipos*, therefore the creation is according to His will, may He be blessed, that they should be a reality of absence. That is, the will of "judgment is not pleasing to Me" caused the creation of the *kelipos* in a manner of absence. This is the difference between them in the root of their creation: the creation of holiness is from the perspective of "judgment is pleasing to Me," and the creation of the *kelipos* is from the perspective of "judgment is not pleasing to Me." The difference in the creation itself is that the creation of everything in holiness is in a manner where the divine vitality shines within it, for since it is nullified to the divine vitality, its creation is in a manner where the light shines within it. Whereas the creation of the *kelipa* is in a manner where the divine light does not shine within them, but is in a state of exile within them, because the *kelipa* opposes Divinity. From this difference, a difference is also drawn in the manner of their existence: in holiness, where the creation is through the revelation of vitality, this causes it to have an intrinsic existence. Whereas in the *kelipos*, where their creation is not through the revelation of vitality but through the vitality being in them in a state of exile, they have no existence at all, and their reception of vitality from holiness is through the act of the sin.

**This is** "And Hashem spoke to Moshe... when you cross... into the land of Canaan," and make the land of Canaan into the land of Israel, then "You shall designate cities for yourselves," meaning cities of refuge. For cities of refuge are the *tikkun* (rectification) for the matter of killing a person, which is the case in every sin (for its matter is "he who sheds the blood of man, by man," as above). This *tikkun* must be specifically through preparation, and this is "You shall designate for yourselves," because all matters of holiness come specifically through *avodah* and preparation. And the *tikkun* is not only to remove the sin and the punishment, but to effect *birur* (refinement) and purification specifically in the interior, which is the concept of *miklat* (refuge), a term for absorption and interiority. It is the same also in the individual *avodah* in everyone, that words of Torah specifically "absorb." For the fulfillment of *mitzvos*, although it also contains salvation, as the *mitzvos* also protect for the time being, nevertheless, besides the fact that it is only for the time being, it is also in a manner of *makif*. For the *mitzvos* are garments that encompass man and protect him, but this is not in the interior. Whereas in the study of Torah, through the study, man unites with

#### 93

the Torah in a wondrous unity like no other, where his intellect encompasses the concept and the concept encompasses the intellect, as explained in *Tanya*. And although it is implied in *Tanya* that this is only at the time of study, in truth it is so also after the study, for since the intellect of the Torah is above him, his intellect is encompassed by it also after the study. This is the advantage of Torah over *mitzvos*: *mitzvos* are garments, whereas Torah, besides being a garment for the soul, is also a level of food, for through the study of Torah, man unites with the Torah. Therefore, "words of Torah absorb," for through the study of Torah, the *birur* and purification are effected in the interior, and the concept of the soul’s *dveikus* (cleaving) to the *Ein Sof*, Blessed be He, is revealed, as it is written, "And you who cleave to Hashem your G-d, you are all alive today."

## Sicha of Shabbos Parshas Matos-Masei, Mevarchim HaChodesh Menachem-Av, 5712

#### 94

**A**. The Rebbe instructed that a *nigun* be played, and then delivered a *maamar* on the verse, "And you shall designate for yourselves cities of refuge..."

***

**B**. The roads that led to the *arei miklat* (cities of refuge) — which are discussed in this week’s *parsha* — were well-maintained, thirty-two *amos* wide, and at every crossroads there was a signpost inscribed with the words "Miklat, Miklat," to indicate the path leading to the *arei miklat*.

Now, since this was the case in the physical realm, it is understood that these same details exist in the spiritual *arei miklat* — which are the words of Torah, as our Sages stated, "Words of Torah... provide refuge," in that they provide refuge for the "manslayer" in the spiritual sense (as discussed earlier in the *maamar*).

**C**. To preface the order of things in the physical and spiritual realms: It is not that a matter exists in the physical realm and therefore exists in the spiritual; rather, the *opposite* is true. The essence of the matter is in the spiritual, and because it is so in the spiritual, it descends and comes into being through *hishtalshelus* (the chain of progression) in the physical as well.

As explained in the *Shelah* regarding the fact that the Torah speaks in the lower realms while alluding to the higher realms — in truth, the Torah speaks of the higher realms, and we take the allusion that pertains to the lower realms.

Just as this applies to all matters of Torah, so too it applies to the *arei miklat*: all the details that exist in the physical *arei miklat* exist in the spiritual *arei miklat*. On the contrary, their essence is in the spiritual, and through *hishtalshelus* they descend and manifest in the physical.

**D**. If we were worthy, we would "take" the matter from above to below—that is, we would understand the matter in its spiritual sense, and consequently understand that since these matters exist in the spiritual, they must surely exist in the physical as well.

One can "take" all the affairs of the world from above to below—that is, to see the matters as they are in the spiritual, and thereby understand that they must surely be the same in the physical.

— The Rebbe Rayatz once related that in the years 5654-5, medical scientists discovered a new artery in the brain that aids memory and depth of thought. One of the members of the household told this with great excitement to the Rebbe Rashab:

— The Rebbe Rashab was in the middle of *netilas yadayim* for a meal, and he generally could not tolerate ("*plegt nit velen leiden*") being spoken to at such a time. Even though according to *din* only speech or a certain amount of time is considered an interruption, he could not tolerate being interrupted even in his thoughts alone —

The Rebbe Rashab listened ("*hot oisgehert*") and said nothing. After *Birkas Hamazon*, he asked that they wait a moment. He went into his room, brought out a small volume ("*a klein bichel*") of Chassidus—a manuscript of the Mitteler Rebbe—and showed that it is brought down there, in six or seven lines, regarding this artery.

The Mitteler Rebbe explains there that in the brain there is an artery filled with vapors that moves back and forth ("*hin un tzurik*"), and this artery aids in memory and depth of thought. When the artery moves toward the *moach* of *Chochmah* and *Binah*, it aids memory; when it moves toward the *moach* of *Da'as*, it aids depth of thought. Therefore, we

#### 96

see that when a person wants to remember something, he *raises* his head, whereas when he wants to delve into something, he *bows* his head.

They asked the Rebbe Rashab: "If so, the Mitteler Rebbe was a great professor?" The Rebbe Rashab replied: "He—the Mitteler Rebbe—knew this because above, in the 'Supernal Man,' this matter exists, and therefore it certainly exists in the human below."

How much more so with matters *of Torah*—we can certainly take them from above to below, that is, to take the matters in the spiritual and deduce from them the physical, as explained in *Iggeres HaKodesh* regarding the study of the *halachos* of the future, that "they will know from the inwardness of Torah (the spiritual aspects) all the bodies of the revealed Torah" (the physical).

However, since we are not worthy of that, we must take the matters from below to above: to see the matters as they are in the physical, and deduce from them the content of the matters in the spiritual.

**E**. The point of this is:

Just as in the physical, the *arei miklat* were effective (not only for the unintentional manslayer, but) also for the intentional manslayer, for even the intentional manslayer had to flee to the *arei miklat* until the investigation and interrogation (so that the blood-avenger would not kill him in the meantime) — so too in the spiritual, "words of Torah... provide refuge" even for the intentional manslayer, that is, one who commits a transgression intentionally.

And just as in the physical, besides the fact that the roads leading to the *arei miklat* were well-maintained and wide so the murderer could flee easily, there was also a signpost at every crossroads to show the way, because the fact that the road is well-maintained and wide is not enough if the manslayer does not know which direction to go — so too in the spiritual: besides the fact that the path to words of Torah is an easy and wide path, Hashem shows the person which direction to go. Because of the free choice given to man, as it is written, "See, I have set before you today life and good, and death and evil," if He does not show him the way, he might, *chas v'shalom*, walk on the left path and might

#### 97

choose death and evil. Therefore, Hashem stands, as it were, with a "signpost" and says to him: "*Miklat, Miklat*," warning him to walk on the right path, as it is written, "And you shall choose life."

This is the concept that "Hashem helps him." Since, due to the free choice given to everyone, he can choose, *chas v'shalom*, even evil, he needs the help from above—that Hashem should say to him: "*Miklat, Miklat*," go on the path that leads to life and good. As we find that even Rabban Yochanan ben Zakkai said, "I do not know which [path] they are leading me on," and certainly others, and certainly those who are in a time of double and redoubled darkness, who certainly cannot know which path they are walking on, and thus require the help from above, that Hashem should tell them where to go.

**F**. However, if a person wants Hashem to show him where to go, he himself must behave in this manner, for Hashem’s measure is "measure for measure":

One must go out to the "crossroads" where Jews are standing who do not know which direction to go. One must go to them and proclaim and shout ("*shreien*"): "*Miklat, Miklat*," walk on the right path that leads to the "refuge," for there is the place of "refuge" from the "blood-avenger," the *Satan*, who "descends and seduces, ascends and accuses," and do not walk, *chas v'shalom*, on the left path, which leads to the grave and destruction!

This is the task incumbent upon everyone—to station oneself at a "crossroads" and shout: "Miklat, Miklat," that is, to show *Bnei Yisrael* the path they must walk. And even if for this he must be found all the time at a "crossroads," in a place where there exists a path leading to the left side—he should not be discouraged by this, for precisely through this he fulfills the task incumbent upon him.

As the saying of R' Mordechai the Tzaddik in the name of the Baal Shem Tov: "*A neshamah descends to the world and lives seventy or eighty years, in order to do a favor for a Jew in the physical, and especially in the spiritual*," from which it is understood that it is worth standing *all the time* at a "crossroads,"

#### 98

in order to show (even) *one time* to a Jew the path that leads to the Torah-"refuge" ("*tzum Torah-miklat*")!

**G**. And in the merit of this, Hashem will show *him* the path that leads to life and good:

When a Jew comes to a "crossroads" and stations himself like a "signpost," and shouts: "Miklat, Miklat,"

— He does not know if the listener will obey him, he does not even know if there is anyone listening at all to his words, he does not see people at all... and nevertheless, it does not concern him, for he is like a "signpost" standing at a "crossroads" and showing the way to the "refuge," that is, the path leading to a place of refuge from the *yetzer hara*, refuge from the *Satan* —

Even though it is possible that he accomplished nothing, nevertheless, since he fulfilled his task—to stand at a "crossroads" and show *Bnei Yisrael* the way to the "refuge"—Hashem will show *him* the path of "And you shall choose life." Even though he is only at the level of an "*oved* Elokus" (present tense), and not an "*eved* Hashem" (past tense)—meaning, he is still *in the middle* of the *avodah*, because he still has a *yetzer hara*, and not only did he not achieve in his *avodah* any weakening of the *yetzer hara* that remains with him from birth, but on the contrary, "he has *strengthened* himself more over time by using it much in eating and drinking, etc.," and from the side of the *yetzer hara* he can choose, *chas v'shalom*, death and evil—Hashem shows him the path to life and good, Hashem helps him "to save him from those who judge his soul."

**H**. The Rebbe Rayatz once said that the difference between the Baal Shem Tov and the great scholars of the *nigleh* (revealed) Torah of his time was that the order among the scholars of *nigleh* was that the students would come to them to learn, whereas the Baal Shem Tov and his students would not wait for them to come, but would travel to the cities and settlements where *Bnei Yisrael* were found.

#### 99

— There is an explanation from the Alter Rebbe and the Mitteler Rebbe (already printed) regarding the difference between the scholars of *nigleh* and the Baal Shem Tov. —

It can be said that the aforementioned difference between the scholars of *nigleh* and the Baal Shem Tov also applies to what was said above regarding *arei miklat*, as there is a difference as the matter is according to *nigleh* and according to Chassidus:

According to *nigleh*, there was a pillar, a stone, or the like at every crossroads, upon which was written: "Miklat, Miklat." The pillar did not "speak" to the other; rather, when someone passed by the pillar and looked at what was inscribed on it, and knew how to read at least—then he knew the path he needed to walk.

The same is true in the spiritual: there is a Jew who will indeed travel from his place and arrive at a "crossroads," that is, to Jews who are standing at a "crossroads," and while there, when someone asks him—someone who knows how to "read" at least—then he will show him the path to the "refuge."

He indeed had *mesiras nefesh* ("*mesiras nefesh hot er take gepravet*") to travel to a "crossroads," but he did not move from his place ("*er hot zich nit gerirt fun zein ort*"), and he is nothing more than a standing pillar, "domem" (inanimate).

— They once used to say about a clock: it walks back and forth, incessantly, and yet, it does not move from its place. —

However, according to the explanation of Chassidus, a person must not be like an "inanimate" object, but must be a "walker"; he must be a living and warm person ("*a lebediker un a warmer mentsh*"), and consequently, he should not wait until they turn to him and ask him, etc. There must be vitality and activity in a person, and when he sees that there is a Jew in some place whom he might be able to help—he is obligated to run to him and show him the path to the "refuge."

This is the task that the Rebbe Rayatz imposed on the students in particular—that they should travel to various places for several weeks and shout: "Miklat, Miklat,"

#### 100

this is the path that leads to life and good; that the students should be like "signposts" that show the way, and "living signposts," meaning, not "stone-signposts" that do not move from their place, but "living signposts" that walk with vitality and show everyone the path to the "refuge."

**I**. It is stated in the Gemara—and brought as *halacha* in the Rambam—that a person should always see himself as balanced, and the world as balanced. One must always look at every person as being in a balanced state, and consequently, the action he is about to do will tip him to merit or the opposite. And more than this: one must also think that *the world* is balanced, meaning, that he, and all the generations that will be after him, and all the generations that were before him—all are balanced, and upon this action he is about to do now, the entire world depends.

From this, it is understood also regarding our matter:

When a *bochur* travels to a "crossroads" to show the way to bring the hearts of Yisrael to their Father in Heaven, he should contemplate that the entire world, all the generations that have already passed and all the generations that will be in the future, *are balanced*, and with every action he influences a Jew, he is *tipping the entire world*—then he will not be like an "inanimate-signpost" that does not move from its place, but will be a "living-signpost"; he will walk and run with vitality, lest he succeed in influencing something on a Jew, through which he will tip the entire world!

He will run to every place, and upon meeting a Jew, he will explain to him that he must know that he is balanced. In whatever state he is found—he is balanced. Even if he is a great sinner, it is in his power to do *teshuvah*, and being balanced, through one action, "in one hour and in one moment," it is in his power to tip his entire state to the good, since he is balanced. And on the other hand—he will explain to him—he must know that with one action he can tip his entire state to the side of the *le'umat zeh* (the opposing side), *chas v'shalom*; from the side of the free choice given to him, he can choose, *chas v'shalom*, evil, and consequently he must be careful to do

#### 101

the things that lead to life and good, and he—the student—will show this Jew the path of "And you shall choose life."

**J**. There are those who think they are not accomplishing anything. But, in truth, this—whether they are accomplishing or not—is not what determines:

To preface the *din* regarding *biur chametz*: "Even if the searcher searches and finds no *chametz* in his search, he has not made a blessing in vain, for such is the *mitzvah* to search for *chametz* and look for it, lest he find something of it, and if he did not find, there is nothing wrong with that, and he has already fulfilled the *mitzvah* as it should be."

What to boast about—he indeed has none. He was awake all night, did not study during the night and did not sleep, he turned the house upside down, and after all this, he accomplished nothing. But, the *mitzvah* that was incumbent upon him—he fulfilled it.

The same is true regarding the search for *chametz* *in the spiritual*:

A *bochur* who travels *and thinks* he accomplished nothing,

— He spent his time ("*er hot farbracht zein tzeit*") not in his place of residence, but somewhere in the middle of the road, at a "crossroads," in a place where they can walk to the left side, *chas v'shalom*; he became properly tired, and after all this, he accomplished nothing. And furthermore, when he returns from the trip, they will even come to him with complaints, and his friends will mock him—

Indeed, what to boast about—he has none, but, the task incumbent upon him—he did, and "the reward of a *mitzvah* is a *mitzvah*"—that through the fulfillment of the *mitzvah*, a *tzavta* and connection is made.

**K**. Furthermore:

Even though he thinks he accomplished nothing, besides what was said that the essence is the *act* itself, in truth, *he did accomplish*, only he himself does not know of it. Since he acted—he certainly accomplished ("*veibald az er hot geton hot er gevis oifgeton*"), as the words of the Rebbe Rayatz in the name of his father: it is a *chazakah* (presumption) for *ta'amulah* (propaganda/outreach) that it does not return empty.

#### 102

For example—one of the actions in this:

Besides the fact that through his walking or running on a *mitzvah* mission, he achieved a refinement in the stones he walked upon, and also purified the air of that place,

— But, this is an action he does not see, and it will not be recorded here below in the report; and his desire is to accomplish in the mission he was sent on, to bring the hearts of Yisrael to their Father in Heaven —

In truth, he succeeded in accomplishing also in the matter of the mission:

When a Jew sits in his house and sees—through the window—how a *bochur* with a beard is running in the street, he remembers that his father also looked like that, and he remembers that his father commanded him: "Every day, when you rise in the morning, say 'Modeh Ani,' and when you lie down to sleep, say 'Shema Yisrael,'" and in the moment of action he repeats—this Jew who remembered his father—in his thought or his speech the verse "Shema Yisrael" or the formula "Modeh Ani," a matter that brings him to *hisorerus* (arousal).

And likewise when a child comes home and relates that he met a *bochur* with a beard who was giving out "talks and tales," who wanted to "insert" ("*areinruken*") into his hand the pamphlet, but he did not accept it—when the child relates this to his father or mother, they remember—in the moment of action—about their parents, and in one hour and in one moment, etc.

**L**. The truth is that he should have a "broken and contrite heart" over the fact that his actions are not apparent, for if he were worthy, he would accomplish internal actions ("*pnimiyus'dike peulos*"), and not only encompassing actions ("*makif'dike peulos*");

#### 103

however, to say that he accomplished nothing—this is not correct, for the very fact that they saw how a *bochur* with a beard travels from place to place to awaken *Bnei Yisrael* to Yiddishkeit—this fact itself certainly accomplished, at least in an encompassing way.

**M**. An *avreich* from the *Temimim* told me about a Conservative rabbi who was present here once during a Chassidic *farbrengen*. He did not become a Chassid as a result, but the matter found favor in his eyes, and he began to take an interest in Chassidim. This rabbi is a teacher (or principal) in a school, and since he needs to tell the children stories to interest them—he told them that there are such Jews called "Chassidim," and began to describe their conduct to them: they study before *davening*, afterwards they *daven* at length, afterwards they have study sessions, and only after all this, they drink "juice"...

The children listened to all the things, and asked if he was talking about people who lived in the past, or in Europe? The rabbi replied that they are found even today, and in America... The children said they did not believe his words, for it is not possible—the children argued—that today in America one would find such people...

The rabbi began to look for ways to verify the story in the eyes of the children, until he remembered one of his acquaintances, a Chassidic *avreich*—this is the *avreich* who told me the story. The rabbi traveled to him, told him the whole event, and asked him to come and visit the school. "You don't have to do anything," the rabbi said to the *avreich*, "only show yourself"... The *avreich* agreed to this.

When the *avreich*—an *avreich* with a large beard—entered the school, the rabbi turned to the children and said: "Do you remember that I told you a story about Chassidim, and you didn't believe? Well, look and see! This *avreich* who just entered the school is one of the Chassidim!"

The children looked at him—how a Jew with a beard looks, etc.—and asked him if he is a Chassid. When he replied in the affirmative—the children began to ask him about the conduct of the Chassidim, etc. And in the afternoon, the children told

#### 104

in their homes about the event with excitement, and began to present questions to their parents, etc., etc.

**N**. [And the Rebbe concluded:] All the *bochurim* who are traveling—should say "L'chaim," and through this, vitality will be drawn into all the places they will travel to.

[The Rebbe spoke with some about the trip, and afterwards said:]

Once, the order was that when a group would travel from its place, they would accompany them with a "march."

[And he concluded with a smile:] Why do you need to wait for someone else to accompany you—it is better that you accompany yourselves ("*bagleit beser zich alein*")...

Play a happy *nigun* ("*zogt a freilichen nigun*"), and this will be the beginning of a "march."

**O**. Regarding the condition of the tribes of Gad and Reuven—about which it is told in this week’s *parsha*—we find a change between the words of the tribes of Gad and Reuven and the words of Moshe Rabbeinu:

The tribes of Gad and Reuven said, "We will build sheepfolds for our livestock here and cities for our children (and afterwards) and we will arm ourselves quickly before the children of Yisrael, etc.," whereas Moshe Rabbeinu said to them, "If you arm yourselves before Hashem for war, etc. (and afterwards) then this land shall be for you as a possession, etc.," "If they cross, etc., and you shall give them, etc."

And among the reasons for why Moshe Rabbeinu changed from the words of the tribes of Gad and Reuven and prioritized the going out to war:

The tribes of Gad and Reuven prioritized the act over the condition, and therefore Moshe Rabbeinu changed and prioritized the condition over the act, from which we learn that there must be a "*condition preceding the act*" ("a condition upon which the act is dependent, mentioned before the act; if they cross, this is the condition, and you shall give them, this is the act"), "to exclude... an act preceding the condition,"

#### 105

for then "the condition is void" ("and the act is fulfilled even if the owner of the condition did not fulfill the condition"), and consequently the tribes of Gad and Reuven would not have been obligated to go out to war.

And furthermore, they could have argued that since they had just built houses, they were not obligated to go out to war, for one who builds a new house returns from the ranks of war. And even though the war of conquering the land is a "war of *mitzvah*" in which "everyone goes out" (even one who builds a new house, etc.), besides the fact that there was then a possibility that the conquest of Eretz Yisrael would be in the manner of "all the inhabitants of the land melted away before you," without the need for war at all, even in a war of *mitzvah*, the going out to war of one who builds a new house (etc.) is not the same as all the others who go out to war.

**P**. And regarding our matter:

**B.** Continuing from what was discussed above [19] regarding the virtue of Birkas Kohanim — it should be added and explained:

a) Its connection to *gratitude (hoda'ah)*, as the Gemara says [20] "And what did they see to say Birkas Kohanim after gratitude?" [21]

b) And consequently, also its connection to the *resurrection of the dead* which comes through the preceding gratitude, for which reason, one who does not bow during Modim will not rise at the resurrection of the dead [22].

And the point of the explanation regarding the connection of the three aforementioned matters (Birkas Kohanim, gratitude, and the resurrection of the dead) — is that they are matters that are above the Seder Hishtalshelus, as follows.

**C.** *Resurrection of the dead*:

The resurrection of the dead is a matter *above* intellect, for although there are

#### 113

intellectual proofs for the matter of the resurrection of the dead, such as: "Those who were not alive, that they are alive, is it not all the more so [that those who were already alive will return and live]?" [23], and others [24] — this is only a decisive proof that the dead will live, but there is no explanation or clarification here that provides a concept and understanding of how the mode of resurrection will be, and the like.

And just as the general matter of resurrection is not understood by the intellect as to how and what it is, so too it is not understood by the intellect how the mode of life will be after the resurrection:

Our Sages said [25] "The World to Come has no eating or drinking... but the righteous sit with their crowns on their heads and enjoy the radiance of the Shechinah."

Now, the opinion of the Rambam [26] is that the intention of "World to Come" here is Gan Eden, which is the reward for souls without bodies. But the Ramban [27] has already challenged him, that "if it is as he says, then what novelty has the Gemara taught us? Is it possible for a soul without a body to eat, etc.?" [28] Therefore, the Ramban holds — and such is the conclusion in the books of Kabbalah and Chassidus [29] — that the intention of "World to Come" here is the world of resurrection, which is the reward for souls in bodies, and this is the novelty, "that a person will be *in a body*, and nevertheless there is no eating, etc."

And the reason for this is because in the world of resurrection, the body will also enjoy and receive its vitality "from the radiance of the Shechinah" [30], meaning, just as in this time, the vitality of the body (the connection of the soul with the body) is through physical eating and drinking, so in the time of resurrection, the vitality of the body will be from the radiance of the Shechinah.

And more than this: just as in this time, physical food acts *on the body first*, that the food becomes "blood and flesh of his flesh" [31] and strengthens the body, and thereby it also acts on the soul to connect with the body, so too will be the order of the drawing down of vitality in the future when the body will enjoy "from the radiance of the Shechinah" — *that first* the *body* will enjoy and be nourished from the radiance of the Shechinah, and through it, the *soul* [32] will also receive the radiance of the Shechinah.

#### 114

And this matter, that a *physical* body will be nourished from the radiance of the Shechinah, and furthermore, that even the enjoyment of the *soul* from the radiance of the Shechinah is through the *body* — is not grasped by the intellect at all.

**D.** And the explanation of these things, how it is possible that in the resurrection of the dead the body will be nourished from the radiance of the Shechinah — is because in the future (in the time of resurrection) there will be the revelation of the light that encompasses all worlds (Ohr HaSovev) [33], unlike in this time when there is only the revelation of the light that fills all worlds (Ohr HaMemaleh), while the revelation of the encompassing light is not in an internal and essential manner.

And the matter of this is:

The general difference between the light that fills all worlds and the light that encompasses all worlds is [34], that the light that fills all worlds is a light that is grasped by a vessel, and therefore the vessel must be *commensurate* with the light, unlike the light that encompasses all worlds, which is above being clothed in vessels, and is not in the category of being grasped at all.

Therefore: from the perspective of the filling light, which is *according to the measure* of the vessels — it is understood simply that a physical matter must be drawn *first to the body*, which is physical, and afterwards also to the soul; and a spiritual matter must be drawn *first to the soul*, which is spiritual, and afterwards also to the body.

But from the perspective of the encompassing light, which is above being clothed in vessels and is not in the category of being grasped at all — it is not applicable to say that it must be drawn *first to the soul*, for even the soul does not grasp the encompassing light to the same degree (and even more so) that the body does not grasp the filling light, and consequently, it is not applicable to say that the encompassing light belongs to the *soul* more than it belongs to the body.

*And on the contrary* — for this reason, that even the soul does not grasp the encompassing light, the encompassing light belongs *more to the body*, because the soul, whose nature is a "vessel" to grasp the light that is clothed within it, cannot receive a light that is above vessels, meaning that in the soul there is a matter that opposes the revelation of a light that is above vessels, unlike the body, which from the outset is not in the category of a "vessel" for the revelation of light, and consequently, the encompassing light can also dwell within it.

[And similar to what is explained regarding the power of mesiras nefesh and the power of will that is found *in the heel* more than in the head [35] — because these powers are *encompassing* powers that are above being clothed,

#### 115

and the intellects in the head (which are "vessels" for the light of the intellect) are "opposed" to the revelation of encompassing powers that are above comprehension].

And since the encompassing light belongs to the body more than to the soul, therefore, in the future when there will be the revelation of the encompassing light, the *body* will be nourished from the radiance of the Shechinah *first*, and from it, it will also be drawn to the soul.

And this is also the reason that the matters of the resurrection of the dead are not understood by the intellect (both regarding the matter of resurrection itself, and regarding the mode of life after the resurrection, as above) — because the general matter of the resurrection of the dead is the revelation of the encompassing light, which is above the category of comprehension.

**E.** And the preparation and introduction to the resurrection of the dead — is the matter of *gratitude (hoda'ah)* [36]:

Gratitude — that even though he does not grasp the matter with his intellect, nevertheless he *acknowledges* that the truth is so — its nature is a nullification (bitul) *that is above intellect*. And therefore, gratitude is a preparation for the resurrection of the dead — whose nature is the revelation of the encompassing light *that is above comprehension*.

And the connection to the resurrection of the dead will be sweetened more (in addition to the very matter of gratitude) also from the *mode* of gratitude in Modim — that the mode of nullification of gratitude is that he bows his *body first*, and thereby accomplishes nullification also *in his soul* — "he bows from himself" [37], similar to and in the image of the mode of revelation of the resurrection of the dead, *that first* the *body* will be nourished, and through it, the soul will also receive.

And this is also the connection of *Birkas Kohanim* to gratitude (and consequently also to the resurrection of the dead) — because Birkas Kohanim is also a drawing down that is above the Seder Hishtalshelus, as discussed at length above [19].

**F.** And it should be added, that the matter of gratitude as a preparation and introduction to the resurrection of the dead is also expressed in the revelation of the teachings of Chassidus in the latter time that precedes the revelation of Moshiach and the resurrection of the dead:

The teachings of Chassidus — its nature is comprehension, and together with this, the matter of "the ultimate of knowledge is that we do not know" is felt within it.

And on the face of it, it is not understood: since "the ultimate of knowledge *is that we do not know*" [38], and in the end, the matters of the inner dimension of Torah do not come into complete comprehension like

#### 116

the matters in the revealed dimension of Torah — why, then, must one strive and dedicate so many powers ("אַוועקלייגן אַזויפיל כחות") for the *comprehension* of the inner dimension of Torah?!

Rather, the matter of this is — that the intention is *that the person's own comprehension* should "agree" and "acknowledge" that "the ultimate of knowledge *is that we do not know*."

And this is the preparation and introduction to the revelation of the future — the revelation of the encompassing light in an internal and essential manner, meaning, *that within comprehension itself* there will be the revelation *that is above* comprehension. Only that in the future, when the encompassing light will be revealed in actual revelation, it will be in complete comprehension, unlike now, where it is not in actual comprehension, but only in a manner of "gratitude" alone, which, as mentioned, is a preparation and introduction to the resurrection of the dead.

**G.** And one should connect this with the matter of the "Yahrtzeit," whose nature is the ascent of the soul:

And by way of introduction [39] — that there are also ascents every single day, as our Sages said [40] "The righteous have no rest either in this world or in the World to Come, as it is said [41] 'They go from strength to strength, etc.'," only that the ascents of every day are ascents *of degree*, whereas the ascent on the day of the "Yahrtzeit" is an ascent *that is beyond degree* at all (for which reason one says "Kaddish" specifically on this day).

Now, the ascent of the soul (every day, and especially on the day of the "Yahrtzeit") is through its *desire* to ascend to a higher level, a desire that comes because of its lack of satisfaction with the level it is in, for, from the perspective of this level (which it is in) itself, it knows that this is not the ultimate of elevation (similar to what was said above regarding the study of the teachings of Chassidus, that through comprehension itself, he grasps that the ultimate of knowledge is that we do not know).

[And this is the general difference between angels and souls — as is known [42] that angels are called "standing," and specifically souls are called "*walking*," because in every level that the soul stands in, it grasps that this level is not yet the ultimate of elevation, and therefore it desires to ascend to a higher level, which is the matter of walking.]

And the soul's desire to ascend higher throughout the entire year is

#### 117

the preparation for its ascent on the day of the "Yahrtzeit" in an elevation *that is beyond degree* at all (similar to what was said that the study of the teachings of Chassidus in this time is a preparation for the revelation of the encompassing light in the future).

And the ascent of the soul on this day accomplishes an ascent also for all those who are connected to it [43] — that an ascent that is beyond degree at all is accomplished for them in all their matters.

**H.** In one of the "Bichlach" of Chassidus (which consists mostly of the maamarim of the Tzemach Tzedek) that arrived recently [44], there is a maamar titled "The Matter of Kayin and Hevel" [45], in which the matter of "if they merit, I will hasten it; if they do not merit, in its time" [46] is explained in relation to *individual* people (meaning, not in relation to all of Israel, but in relation to every individual person of Israel) — in connection with the departure of the soul from the body.

And it is explained there, that just as in the complete redemption of all of Israel there is a virtue in "hastening it" over "in its time" (for which reason "if they merit, I will hasten it; if they do not merit, in its time") [47], so too in the matter of the departure of the soul from the body for every individual person, there is a virtue in "hastening it," that the departure is *before the time* (like the one who has the "Yahrtzeit" of 20 Menachem-Av), as follows.

**I.** And this is the content of the maamar [48] in explanation of the virtue of "hastening it" regarding the matter of departure:

Regarding the departure of Yaakov it is written [49] "And he gathered his feet into the bed, etc.," and on the basis of this "some of the sages of Kabbalah said — and it is brought in the Acharonim [51] — that every person must be careful at the time of his death that nothing of his limbs remains outside the bed... because around the aforementioned bed there are types of destroyers and calamities... therefore he must

#### 118

be gathered to his people and not outside the powers of impurity, etc.," meaning, that there should be no hold for the "other side" (sitra achra), Heaven forbid.

However, the possibility that there could be a hold for the "other side" at the time of departure (for which reason one must be careful that nothing of his limbs remains outside the bed) is only in a death that is "*in its time*," "the death that a person dies on his bed *when his time and season arrive* specifically," when he has *completed* all his work throughout all the years of his life (for which "he was given days and years in number and measure according to the need for the correction that he needs to correct in his image," when "the time of the end of the days of his life must necessarily come"), for, the order of creation is that "the sucking of the kelipos begins specifically *at the end* of the flow of the light of holiness... and if so... through death... in its time and season, which is after *the correction* of the image of the soul has already been finished... there is necessarily a beginning for the sucking of the externals, etc."; unlike when the departure is in a manner of "*hastening it*," "that he dies suddenly... *before his time and season*... then there is not yet a place and root of beginning at all for the sucking of the externals, for their beginning is only *from the end* of the lights of holiness specifically, etc."

And this is "if they merit, I will hasten it; if they do not merit, in its time": "*If they merit*" — when the soul is in a state of refinement ("merit" from the word refinement), then it departs *before its time* — "*I will hasten it*," so that at the time of departure the soul will be before the end of its work in the world of holiness, and consequently there will be no hold for the "other side" from it. "*If they do not merit*" — when the soul is not in a state of refinement, then the soul departs "*in its time*" and season, after the end of its work in holiness, and consequently there is a possibility of a hold for the "other side."

[And this is also what we find [52] in the explanation of the reason for the addition of cities of refuge in the future [53], that this is for those who kill a person unintentionally who will be then, because "all the seed of Hevel were all obligated to death... if they merit and the end is hastened, then the correction of Hevel is not yet completed... then 'you shall add three more cities for yourself' [54] in order to complete the seeds of Hevel... they will be killed by an accident unintentionally and the killers will be obligated to exile" — for "that which his seed was finished specifically through those who kill a person unintentionally is understood from the aforementioned reason, so that the lights of the sparks of the souls whose root is from the aspect of Hevel should not fall to the externals when they were departing in their time and season"].

**J.** And this matter — departure before the time in a manner of "if they merit, I will hasten it" — we find with several of the great men of Israel:

#### 119

The story is known [55] regarding the departure of the Alter Rebbe — that as a result of the mistake that was made during the journey when they reached the crossroads, our Rebbe the Alter Rebbe departed *before his time*.

— The son of the Rebbe Maharash, R' Avraham Sender — who was extremely wondrous, and the Rebbe Tzemach Tzedek played with him very much — departed when he was eight years old, and before his departure he said that the Alter Rebbe would come to be together with him. The soul of R' Avraham Sender was the soul of the Alter Rebbe, and he lived the number of years that the Alter Rebbe departed before his time [56].

**K.** And it should be added and explained regarding the matter of "if they merit, I will hasten it" in the matter of departure — in the service of man during his lifetime in this world. And by way of introduction:

If he waits to engage in physical matters until he *cools down* from the enthusiasm in prayer, his connection to physical things will be *simply*, and consequently there can be a very great sucking for the externals. Unlike when his descent to matters of the world is by way of *leaping* — then the effect of the feeling and vitality that is in prayer will also be drawn down at the time of engagement in physical matters, to nullify or at least weaken the desire that is in physical things. And in a slightly different style: *the virtue* of slowly, slowly in the order of ascent, that in such a manner *he holds internally* to the level he is ascending to, is the very *deficiency* of slowly, slowly in the order of descent — that the lower level he descends to *is his level* ("אין דער מדריגה וואו ער איז – איז ער טאַקע", both for good and for bad); unlike when the descent is by way of leaping — the lower level is not his level, and furthermore he can always leap and return to his higher level ("ער קען זיך אַלעמאָל צוריק אַרויפכאַפן").

The second mode is like the *avodah* of the *ofanim*[51](#_ftn761), who do not comprehend their source, but know and comprehend that there is something that is wondrous, but they have no comprehension of it at all. Therefore, they say, "Blessed is the glory of Hashem from His place"[52](#_ftn762); because they have no comprehension of it, and they do not know what He is and where His place is, they say "from His place" generally[53](#_ftn763). And since they know that it is a wondrous thing that has no limitations of above and below, and therefore it can be drawn down below as well, they say "Blessed is the glory of Hashem from His place," that it should be drawn down below. And so it is also in *avodah*, that the person does not comprehend anything but knows that there is a wondrous thing.

**Now**, in this *avodah* of the blessings of *Krias Shema*, the comprehension is in the root of the *Nefesh HaBahamis*[54](#_ftn764). However, above this is the matter of *Krias Shema*, which comes after the blessings of *Krias Shema*, and this is the matter of comprehension in the root of the *Nefesh HaElokis*. This is "Hear, O Israel"[55](#_ftn765)—*Shema* from the expression of "hear and contemplate"[56](#_ftn766); *Israel* means the root of the *Nefesh HaElokis*. But even this is only a matter of comprehension, whether human intellect or Divine intellect, and in the souls it is the comprehension of the *Nefesh HaBahamis* or the *Nefesh HaElokis*. Nevertheless, it is only the *bitul* of comprehension, which is *bitul hayesh*, and it has not yet reached the top of the ladder of *tefillah*, "and its top reaches the heavens." But the matter of the *Shemoneh Esrei* is *bitul b'metzius*, that he stands "like a servant before his master"[57](#_ftn767). This means that the *bitul* changes his entire reality. Just as the action and drawing down of *tefillah* in the simple sense effects a change in physicality, to be a "healer of the sick" and "blesser of the years," so too

#### 141

in the *avodah* therein, he effects a change in the reality of the person praying, who becomes nullified in existence entirely. However, in truth, even the *bitul b'metzius* of *Shemoneh Esrei* is not *bitul atzmi*, for since he reaches this *bitul* through the introduction of the previous levels, which are levels of comprehension, it is understood that this is not *bitul atzmi*. As explained above, since the *avodah* of *tefillah* is from below to above, even in its highest level it is only in the category of *bitul hayesh* and not *bitul atzmi*. [And this is the reason why there must be *nefilas apayim* after *Shemoneh Esrei*, which is *bitul atzmi*, encompassing the head and the foot equally, like the *bitul* of *Modeh Ani*.]

**Now**, from all the above, the difference is understood between the two modes of *avodah*: Torah, which is from above to below, and *tefillah*, which is from below to above. It can be said that the reason these two paths exist in the *avodah* of man is because the matter of *avodah* was given to man, who is composed of a soul and a body—for only then is he called "man"[59](#_ftn769). The soul has the nature of ascent, as it is said[61](#_ftn771), "The spirit of man, which ascends above," and the nature of the body is the nature of descent, "the spirit of the animal, which descends below." The ultimate intent is that there be a connection between the soul and the body, and this is when one effects in each of them that it be the opposite of its nature: that in the soul, which is by nature of ascent, there should be *avodah* by way of descent and drawing down, and in the body, which is by nature of descent, there should be the matter of ascent. Therefore, the two paths of *avodah* were given—from below to above and from above to below—which are *tefillah* and Torah. Now, since Torah and *tefillah* are like soul and body, therefore, just as in Torah and *tefillah* both must be present—and one thing cannot take the place of the other, as our Sages said[62](#_ftn772), "They abandon eternal life and occupy themselves with temporal life"—and so too it is written[63](#_ftn773) that Abba Binyamin would pray that his *tefillah* be adjacent to his bed, meaning that his Torah study should be specifically after *tefillah*—so too it is in the *avodah* of the body and the *avodah* of the soul, that there must be a correct blending. The soul, which has the nature of ascent, must be worked upon so that it is in the manner of drawing down, and the body, which is by nature of descent, must be worked upon so that it is in ascent. And this itself must be in a correct blending[64](#_ftn774).

#### 142

**To explain** the matter[65](#_ftn775): sometimes it is possible that a person contemplates Divinity and comes to *hispaalus*—the *hispaalus* being from the soul—and after a time, and sometimes even during the act, he cannot overcome the desires of the body and also matters of the opposite. The reason for this is because the *hispaalus* is from the soul, which does not relate to the body, and therefore when the body desires, the soul does not overcome it. Especially if it is a very high soul and the body is very coarse, then it is possible that there will be no connection of the soul with the body. This is not according to the intent, for the intent is that there be the opposite of the soul's nature—that the soul should be in drawing down and descent—and when the soul has no connection to the body, it is not according to the intent. Similarly, when the soul is very high and the body is also very refined—seemingly this is a very high mode, but in truth this is also not a correct blending. Since the body is a refined body and is drawn to Divinity by itself and not by the soul, it turns out that it does not need the *avodah* of the soul; since it is a refined body and the soul is high, it does not need *avodah* at all, and it immediately becomes *ratzo* (running) toward Divinity. This is[66](#_ftn776) similar to what our Sages said, "Wicks that the Sages said one may not light with on Shabbos, because the flame flickers on them"[67](#_ftn777). When the body is coarse, it needs the *avodah* of the soul to effect in it that it be drawn to Divinity; this contemplation is an internal contemplation that effects in the body that it be drawn to Divinity. But when the body is a refined body, even though it also needs arousal from the soul, it is only arousal, and the contemplation in this is a general contemplation and by way of *makif*, but it is not the *avodah* of the soul. Since the body is a refined body, it sees Divinity even in worldly matters, such as *hashgacha pratis*, and consequently it is not according to the intent. Rather, the intent is that the soul should effect in the body the opposite of its nature—that the body, which is by nature of descent, the soul should effect in it that it be drawn to Divinity, the opposite of its nature. But when the soul is high and the body is a refined body, it only arouses the body to be drawn by its nature to Divinity. And so it is also when the soul is low and the body is a coarse body, that even then the soul cannot effect in the body the matter of ascent, the opposite of its nature. The intent is that there be a correct blending, for then there is the connection of the soul and the body as it should be—meaning that in each of them there should be the opposite of its nature. This means that the blending—that the soul should be according to the capacity of the body—comes from above. And when there is the correct blending, then there must be the *avodah* of man to connect the *avodah* of the soul with the body, to effect in each of them the opposite of its nature.

#### 143

**Now**, just as it is in the general *avodah*, so too it is in the *avodah* of *teshuvah*[68](#_ftn778). Even though the matter of *teshuvah* in general is a departure from the boundaries of the *keilim*, nevertheless, it must be in a way that it is relevant to physicality. That is, when the contemplation of *teshuvah* is that he feels that physical matters are a hindrance to the fulfillment of Torah and *mitzvos*, and especially matters of the opposite, the path of *avodah* is not that he should run away from physical matters, but that he should effect in the physical matters that they should not be a hindrance to Torah and *mitzvos*. And similarly, when he feels the good of Divinity, this should not cause only *ratzo* toward Divinity, but that in physicality itself he should feel the Divinity that is in it. In order for the *avodah* of *teshuvah* to be as it should be—that it should not be in the category of *ratzo* from physical matters, but that he should feel the Divinity in them—this is through the correct blending of soul and body that is done from above. Like in the *ikvesa d'meshicha* (the footsteps of Moshiach), where the souls are very low—souls of the category of "heels"—the bodies are now more refined, so that the soul and the body should be in proportion. Through this, that they are in proportion and in connection with one another, it is possible for the *avodah* of *teshuvah* to be as it should be, that there should not be *ratzo* from physicality.

**This is** "I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me, who grazes among the roses," for "who grazes among the roses" is the bestowal of strength from above as mentioned above. Even though the *avodah* of *teshuvah* in general, and the *avodah* of the month of Elul in particular, is from below to above, nevertheless, in order for the *avodah* to be as it should be, there must be the blending of soul and body by way of a correct blending, which comes from above, that they give him a refined body (as mentioned above). This is "I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me, who grazes among the roses," that in order for the *avodah* of "I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me"—which is the *avodah* of *teshuvah*—to be as it should be, this is through "who grazes among the roses," which is the correct blending that comes from above, which is a bestowal of strength for "I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me."

## Sicha of Shabbos Parshas Re’eh, Mevarchim HaChodesh Elul, 5712

#### 144

**A.** The Rebbe instructed that a *nigun* be played, and then delivered a *maamar* on the theme of "I am to my beloved and my beloved is to me."

***

**B.** The point mentioned above (in the *maamar*) regarding the general *avodah* of connecting the *neshamah* and the body specifically, is also a matter of comfort—in the words of the Alter Rebbe in *Tanya*: "And this shall be their comfort, to comfort them doubly." This refers to the general descent of the *neshamah* into the body.

For before its descent below and its enclothement in the body, the *neshamah* was in an *exceedingly lofty* level—"hewn from beneath the Throne of Glory" (as the Sages state).

And more than this—as those who have studied *mussar* seforim, and especially *Chassidus*, know: the *neshamah* is higher than the level of "hewn from beneath the Throne of Glory" as interpreted in the *nigleh* of Torah. For that level is only after the *neshamah* has already become a "created being," as it is written, "You created it." Yet at its root, it is in the state of "It is pure," which refers to the level of the *neshamah* as it exists in *Olam HaAtzilus*.

And higher than that—"The *neshamah* that You placed within me," which refers to the level of the *neshamah* that is higher even than the level of "It is pure."

And higher than that—the level of the *neshamah* about which it is said, "Yisrael arose in [the Divine] thought." And within *machshavah* itself—"arose in *machshavah*," in the most exalted level of *machshavah* (as the Rebbe [Rayatz] explains in his *maamarim* for the holiday of Pesach, the details of the levels in *machshavah*).

#### 145

And even to the point where the *neshamos* of Yisrael are rooted in *Atzmus mamash*, and there, the *neshamos* of Yisrael are rooted even deeper ("tifer") than the Torah.

From this lofty level, the *neshamah* descended below: first is the descent to the level of "You created it," then "You formed it," and then "You breathed it into me," and there it must be "And You preserve it within me." And after all this, the Sages said, "May your departure from the world (without sin) be like your arrival into the world!"...

Especially when one contemplates his conduct below—that even when his conduct in all matters of *machshavah*, speech, and action is as it should be, according to *Shulchan Aruch*, ultimately only a minority of his time (or only a minority of a minority) is dedicated to spiritual matters. Meanwhile, for the majority of the time, the majority of his years, he is occupied with *gashmius* matters—eating, drinking, sleeping, and the like.

And even when his involvement in *gashmius* matters is only as *required* by Torah—the Torah rules that a person is required to sleep once every three days... and is required to eat once every seven days... and even this he does (not to fulfill the desires of his body and *nefesh habehamis*, and not even for the sake of sustaining his body, but) *leshem shamayim*, so that in an hour (or similar) he will be able to *daven* or learn with the strength from that food—ultimately, he is engaged in *gashmius* matters. They are not material matters, but they are at least *gashmius* matters that are from *kelipas nogah*—matters to which the *neshamah* by its own nature *does not belong at all*. Yet now, in its descent below, he is occupied with them, and not only that, but *he derives pleasure from them* (which is why he is obligated to make a *bracha* on them). For even though his intention is *leshem shamayim*, ultimately he feels (not, G-d forbid, etc., but) the enjoyment and *taanug* ("dem geshmaak") that is in the *gashmius* things!

And all the more so if it is possible that he stumbled at times—then he is "stuck" ("ligt er doch") not only in matters of *kelipas nogah*, but also in matters of the three *kelipos hatemeios* entirely!...

And if so, when he contemplates his standing and state now in relation to the standing and state of his *neshamah* before its descent below—he can fall into despair ("ken er doch aropfalan ba zich").

#### 146

—To this, the Alter Rebbe writes: "And this shall be their comfort, to comfort them doubly."

**C.** The content of this "comfort"—as discussed above (in the *maamar*)—is that the ultimate intent is that the *neshamah* and the body should not be separate and distinct from one another, for then the *neshamah* does not belong to the body, such that the *neshamah* can be aroused with *hispaalus* toward *Elokus*, "un tzum guf hot dos kein shichus nit"... Rather, one must connect and unite the *avodah* of the *neshamah* with the body, so that the body, too, will be elevated with the *neshamah*, for the ultimate intent is in the body specifically.

—The Rebbe [Rayatz] told a story about the train travel in Italy—in those years when it earned a "reputation" for disorder ("a gevaldiker bilti seder")—that the passengers would wait in their places in the train cars, and everything was ready for the journey, the "engine" ("der paravoz," "lokomotiv") with the coals, the water, and the other necessary things, "es hot gebrent a feier, es hobn geflien funken, es hot zich getumlt"... But there was one problem: the "engine" was not connected to the cars, so the engine whistled and traveled on its way, while the passengers who sat in the train cars did not move from their place!... The analogy is that one must connect and unite ("tzutshepen") the *avodah* of the *neshamah* with the body, so that the body, too, will "travel" and be elevated with the *neshamah* ("der guf zol oich mitforn").

The point of the matter is that, in truth, there is no division between *neshamah* and body, spirituality and *gashmius*, because the existence of *gashmius* is also *Elokus*. Consequently, even at the time that a person is occupied with *gashmius* things, he is connected with the *Atzmus* and essence of *Ein Sof*, *Baruch Hu*, just as when he is occupied with spiritual matters.

**D.** This matter is also emphasized in the order of *tefillah*:

By way of introduction—the start of the day is with the recitation of "Modeh Ani" immediately upon waking from sleep, which is a general acknowledgement that encompasses the head and the foot equally, yet it does not belong to *hasagah* and internal *kochot*.

Afterwards, the order of *tefillah* begins—"Hodu LaHashem," *Pesukei D'zimra*,

#### 147

*Brachos* of *Krias Shema*, *Krias Shema*, *Shemoneh Esrei*—this is the order of *avodah* that belongs to the internal *kochot*.

However, this *avodah* is only by way of *hasagah*, and the *bitul* is only *bitul hayesh* alone.

The ultimate intent is that after a person goes through ("er geit durch") the entire order of *tefillah* with all his internal *kochot*, he should arrive at a level of *self-bitul* that encompasses the head and the foot equally—this is the concept of *nefilas apayim* (after *Shemoneh Esrei*), which is *self-bitul*, similar to the *bitul* of "Modeh Ani." However, the *bitul* of "Modeh Ani" is *before* the person belongs to the level of *pnimiyus*, whereas the *bitul* of *nefilas apayim* is *after* the general *avodah* in the internal *kochot*, which effects within them *self-bitul*, encompassing the head and the foot equally (similar to the *bitul* of "Modeh Ani").

And when a person goes through and accomplishes ("er tut-durch") the entire order of *tefillah* with all his internal *kochot*, and afterwards arrives at *self-bitul* that encompasses the head and the foot equally—then there no longer exists for him a difference between spirituality and *gashmius*, because he sees that the existence of *gashmius* is also *Elokus*.

**E.** One should add and explain the lesson and the test for this—that even when occupied with *gashmius* things, he is connected with *Elokus*:

The Rebbe [Rayatz] said in the name of the early *Chassidim* that the difference between *gashmius* and spirituality is only regarding *one's own* *gashmius* ("aigene gashmius"), where the involvement in *gashmius* is for the sake of the *body's* pleasure, whereas the involvement in spirituality is for the sake of the *neshamah*. But regarding the *gashmius* of *others* ("yenem's gashmius")—where there is no pleasure for his body—there is no difference between *gashmius* and spirituality.

From this it is understood that when a Jew is occupied with the *gashmius* of *others* with vitality, and with that same vitality ("mit dem zelbn chiyus") with which he is occupied with *his own* *gashmius* (or spirituality)—this is proof that his *gashmius* is also *Elokus*. That is, there is no difference for him between *gashmius* and spirituality, because his *gashmius* is also not for the sake of the body's pleasure, just like the *gashmius* of others, which is certainly not for the sake of the body's pleasure (for his body does not derive pleasure from the *gashmius* of others), because he feels that *gashmius* is also *Elokus*.

#### 148

**F.** This is the reason for the conduct we find among our *Rebbeim Nesi’im* in general (and in recent times in particular) that they strove to do good for a Jew *in gashmius*, and demanded from those connected and belonging to them that they occupy themselves with the *gashmius* of others with the same vitality that they occupy themselves with their own *gashmius*—because:

As time goes on ("altz vos veiter")—we are drawing closer to the coming of *Moshiach*, when there will be no difference between *gashmius* and spirituality, because it will be seen openly that the existence of *gashmius* is also *Elokus*, as it is written, "And the glory of Hashem shall be revealed, and all flesh shall see together," meaning, that even the physical flesh will see *Elokus*.

Therefore, as a *preparation* for the days of *Moshiach*, our *Rebbeim Nesi’im* strove in the matter of occupying oneself with the *gashmius* of others, as mentioned, for in this matter is expressed the negation of the difference between *gashmius* and spirituality.

***

**G.** The *parsha* of the week that we read in the Torah on *Shabbos* has a connection to the time in which it is read, and as is known, the saying of our *Rebbe* in the name of the Alter Rebbe (the author of *Tanya* and *Shulchan Aruch*): one must live "according to the time," meaning, according to the content of the *parsha* of the week.

Even though, seemingly, the *parsha* of the week belongs to the days *preceding* the *Shabbos*, as we begin reading it at the *Mincha* *tefillah* beforehand. But in truth, the *parsha* of the week also has a connection to the days *after* the *Shabbos*, as the influence of *Shabbos* continues into the days of the week that follow it.

#### 149

The connection that *Shabbos* has with the days that follow it, specifically, is with the three days after *Shabbos*, meaning until Wednesday, which is why we make *Havdalah* over a cup until Wednesday if one forgot and did not make *Havdalah* earlier. And in general, *Shabbos* has a connection *with all the days of the week* that follow it.

In addition to this, *Shabbos Mevarchim HaChodesh* brings blessing and influence *to the entire month*. And so it is regarding the reading of the Torah on this *Shabbos*—that it has a connection to the future, to the entire month.

**H.** There is a connection that is only partial, there is a connection that is greater, and there is a connection that is—total.

In the reading of the Torah on this *Shabbos* of this year, the *parsha* of *Shemitah* that we read today has an extra connection to our time.

Every year, on *Shabbos Mevarchim HaChodesh Elul* (or *Rosh Chodesh Elul*), we read *Parshas Re’eh*, and consequently also the *parsha* of *Shemitah* within it. But this year, this *parsha* has an *extra connection*, because this year is the year of *Shemitah*, according to the halachic ruling.

**I.** It has already been discussed, based on what is stated in the *Shelah*, that the Torah speaks in the upper realms and hints in the lower realms. That is to say, every matter in the Torah is as it is in spirituality and in spiritual matters, and by way of *hishtalshelus* it descended and evolved also into *gashmius*, in the *nigleh* of Torah as it is literally.

As it is with all matters in the Torah, so it is also regarding the laws of *Shemitah*. These laws exist as they are *in spirituality*, meaning in the upper worlds, and also below in the *avodah* in the soul of man, and by way of *hishtalshelus* they descended and evolved into *gashmius* literally.

In order to understand the laws of *Shemitah* as they are in spirituality, and primarily in matters

#### 150

that concern us in particular, in the *avodah* in the soul of man—if we had merited, we would know the matter as it is in spirituality, and from it we would learn how it is in *gashmius*. But since we have not merited, one must learn the matter as it is in *gashmius*, and from it we will learn how it is in spirituality.

**J.** Regarding the laws of *Shemitah*, their general content is that the year of *Shemitah* releases all debts. And there are two main laws in this:

1) The time of *Shemitah*—that the release of money is at the end of the year, at the very last moment of the year, and consequently it releases even the debts borrowed during the year of *Shemitah* itself.

2) One who hands over his promissory notes to the *Beis Din*, the *Shemitah* does not apply to them. This is the foundation of the *Prozbul* enactment: when the Sages saw that people were refraining from lending to one another for fear of losing the debt in the year of *Shemitah*, they enacted the *Prozbul*, whereby the *Shemitah* does not apply, because the *Prozbul* is the handing over of his notes to the *Beis Din*.

And as it is in *gashmius*, meaning that the year of *Shemitah* releases debts, according to the two main details mentioned above, so too do all these matters exist in spirituality and in the *avodah* of man. And this will be explained below.

**K.** It is stated in the *Mishnah Avos*: "The shop is open, the shopkeeper gives credit, the ledger is open, the hand writes, and whoever wishes to borrow, let him come and borrow."

The Creator, *Yisbarach*, gives every person in Yisrael strength and possibilities for children, life, and abundant sustenance—all as a *loan*, "whoever wishes to borrow, let him come and borrow." And *Hashem* collects this loan, as our Sages say, "He collects His own," for "the ledger is open," and the shopkeeper is trusted regarding his ledger.

—There are those who, based on a *heter iska*, collect *more than they lent*, as the Rebbe [Rayatz] explains in his *maamar* "The taste of her merchandise is good," 5709. —

#### 151

That is to say, *Hashem* bestows upon Yisrael children, life, and abundant sustenance from His open hand, and this influence is called a *loan*—and a loan has a twofold meaning:

1) A loan is given for spending, unlike a gift. For in a gift, the recipient must return the gift in kind, whereas a loan is given for spending, meaning that all the matters that *Hashem* bestows are given *to be spent in practice*.

2) A loan requires repayment, unlike a gift. Because Yisrael are the only children of the *Ribono shel Olam*, He did not want His influence to be by way of "bread of shame," but as a loan.

And the repayment of the debt must be like the nature and content of the influence, each influence according to its nature: When *Hashem* bestows children—the repayment is that one educates them in the way of Torah and *mitzvos*, according to the will of the Creator, *Yisbarach*. When *Hashem* bestows long life and healthy and complete life—one must remember that "days are formed, and not one of them is missing"; not one day should pass in vain; every single day must be full and packed with content appropriate for it according to *Shulchan Aruch*. When *Hashem* bestows abundant sustenance—one must give a *maaser* or *chomesh* to *tzedakah*. "He who lends to Hashem is gracious to the poor." And because of this, *Hashem* bestows blessing in the other four parts, so that the fifth part will grow even more.

**L.** But, it is revealed and known to *Hashem* the nature of the borrower, that He must lend to him and continue to lend to him, and in the end, the borrower will not always be able to settle his debt.

Therefore, He (the *Ribono shel Olam*) gave Yisrael the year of *Shemitah*, which *releases* all debts.

The content of *Shemitah* is as it is said, "And the land shall rest, a *Shabbos* to Hashem," meaning, a cessation and pause from worldliness and materiality in order to cleave to *Hashem*, similar to the future—"a day that is entirely *Shabbos* and rest for eternal life."

#### 152

A cessation from his previous essence and becoming a different person—this is what releases the previous debts.

**M.** A merchant has fixed times to do his accounts, in order to know the state of his business. If he were to occupy himself with accounts constantly, he would never engage in trade. Rather—at the end of the day he makes a total of his trade for that day; at the end of the week, at the end of the month—he makes an account of his business for that time. And at the end of the year—a general account of his trade throughout the entire year.

So it is also in spirituality. Every day one must make a *cheshbon hanefesh* during *Krias Shema* before retiring, regarding the deeds of the day; on *Erev Shabbos*—regarding the week; on *Erev Rosh Chodesh*—regarding the month. All of this is a private account. The month of *Elul* is the month of accounting, in which one makes a general account of his entire standing and state throughout the whole year.

—The account of the month of *Elul* itself is, as the Rebbe [Rayatz] said: the twelve days from *Chai Elul* until *Rosh Hashanah* are days in which one must make an account of the past year, one day for each month, and on *Erev Rosh Hashanah* is the day of the account for the month of *Elul*—

When one makes the general account and sees that he borrowed and borrowed, and even in the year of *Shemitah*, not only did he not settle his debts from the previous years, but he even added debts in this year—he falls into despair and gives up hope, G-d forbid.

Therefore, the Torah comes and rules: *Shemitah* releases at its end, at its very last moment. And he still has time to strive to release his debts from the previous years and from the year of *Shemitah* itself.

However, our Sages desire, and every person must strive to do so—*to settle his debts*, and not rely on *Shemitah* to release them, as the *Mishnah* says: "He who repays a debt during the *Shemitah* year, the Sages are pleased with him." But, at the very least, one must strive, if he has nothing with which to pay, that *Shemitah* *will release* his debts.

#### 153

**N.** In order for *Shemitah* to release the debts, one must remove them from the *Beis Din* of Above, because, as mentioned, one who hands over his notes to the *Beis Din*, *Shemitah* does not release them. He must strive that his matters come before the "Unique One"—the Creator, *Yisbarach*, the Unique One of the world.

He must strive that he not be judged from Above according to the law, but rather to exit the limitations of the attribute of *Din*, where then he has no lawsuit with the *Beis Din* of Above, but with the Creator, *Yisbarach*, Himself, and consequently all his debts will be released.

**A.** The Rebbe, my father-in-law, said (in one of his sichos[1]) in the name of the elder Chassidim: "Chai Elul is the day that brought and brings vitality into Elul."

This requires understanding:

In the month of Elul, there are various matters and different forms of avodah: matters of joy, and also matters that are the opposite of joy—such as the matter of "cheshbon hanefesh" (soul-searching) that takes place in Elul.

—As is known, the month of Elul is the month of accounting for the entire year, and especially from Chai Elul onwards, as the Rebbe, my father-in-law, stated[2] that the specific accounting for all twelve months of the year takes place during the twelve days from Chai Elul until Rosh Hashanah, one day for each month—

"Cheshbon hanefesh" is associated with a movement that is the opposite of joy—and this is for two reasons: (a) Due to the general nature of the accounting, which must be performed with focused attention, without hispaalus (which is the essence of joy). (b) Due to the specifics of the accounting—when one performs a soul-searching review of all his thoughts, speech, and actions throughout the year, knowing that "Hashem is exacting (even) with those around Him (the tzaddikim) to a hair's breadth,"[3] and also "He places praise in His angels,"[4] and even "the heavens (the general Seder Hishtalshelus) are not pure in His eyes,"[5] and certainly *he himself*, who has had thoughts, speech, and actions that were not as they should be [for example: the dust of lashon hara, regarding which the Gemara states[6] that "*everyone* is involved in the dust of lashon hara"], through which he has distanced himself from Hashem—he certainly arrives at a feeling of *great bitterness*.

From the language of the Rebbe, my father-in-law, mentioned above ("Chai Elul is the day that brought and brings vitality into Elul"), it is implied that Chai Elul brings vitality into *all* the matters of the month of

## 159

Elul—meaning, not only into the matters of *joy* in the month of Elul, but also into the matters of *bitterness* in this month.

Seemingly, this is difficult to understand: What is the meaning of vitality within bitterness?

**B.** And a greater innovation is stated by the Rebbe, my father-in-law (in the aforementioned sicha1)—"Chai Elul is a day designated for joy and blessing":

We must first note the precision of the language: "joy" and then "blessing"—that "joy" serves as a preface to "blessing." According to the well-known principle[7] that "blessing" comes from the word *himshech* (drawing down), it follows that joy is the preface to all matters whatsoever ("blessing"), for the beginning of all matters is through joy.

Thus, it turns out that not only must all the matters of the month of Elul (including the matters of bitterness within it) be performed *with vitality and joy* (which is the content of the saying that Chai Elul brings vitality into Elul), but furthermore, joy is the *preface* to all the matters of the month of Elul, and without the preface of joy, no matter can exist.

Seemingly, this is difficult to understand—what is the necessity of the preface of joy for all matters, and especially for the matters of *bitterness* in the month of Elul?

**C.** The explanation for this is as follows:

The feeling of bitterness in cheshbon hanefesh must stem from the fact that one’s thoughts, speech, and actions—whether for good or otherwise—touch upon the *Atzmus and Mahus (Essence and Being) of Hashem Himself*.

For when the cause of bitterness is that, as a result of improper thoughts, speech, and actions, one lacks children, life, or sustenance—then the "place" that the bitterness occupies is only to the extent that his own *existence* occupies a place.

And even if the cause of the bitterness is that, as a result of improper thoughts, speech, and actions, one has not refined his portion in the world—even then, the "place" that the bitterness occupies is only to the extent that the general matter of *Olam Hazeh* (this world) occupies a place.

Therefore, the cause of the bitterness must be that the person’s affairs touch (as it were) upon the Atzmus and Mahus of Hashem Himself—for then the bitterness is *with great intensity* *and in truth*.

The point is that one must first contemplate that "avodah is a need of the Higher One,"[8]

## 160

meaning, that his avodah touches Above, and even to the highest heights, above which there is nothing higher ("higher, higher, and even higher, than which there is nothing higher")—to the Atzmus and Mahus of Hashem Himself. For even though the general Seder Hishtalshelus is at a distance from the Atzmus, as "the *heavens* are not pure in His eyes," and certainly *he himself* (as mentioned in section 1), nevertheless, since the souls of Yisrael are "children to the Makom," "You are children to Hashem your G-d,"[9] therefore, the avodah of Yisrael—the avodah of a *child*—touches the Atzmus of Hashem Himself. And when he understands and feels in what "place" and "level" his avodah touches—then his bitterness over the *lack* of his avodah will be a true and intense bitterness.

**D.** And in order for there to be the feeling that a person's avodah touches Above—the preface of *joy* is specifically required:

Without joy—when he knows his standing, his situation, and the value of his *lowliness* ("zain niederekeit") in general, and especially due to his improper thoughts, speech, and actions, because of which he is at a *total* distance from the Atzmus (a fortiori from the fact that "the *heavens* are not pure in His eyes," as mentioned above)—he cannot in any way generate within himself the feeling that his avodah touches the *Atzmus* of Hashem Himself.

The remedy for this is the matter of *joy*. Since "joy breaks through barriers,"[10] it breaks through, regardless of his distance from the Atzmus (as mentioned above), and elevates the person, placing him in his true standing and situation (the standing and situation of all Bnei Yisrael)—"*children* to the Makom," and even—as he is at his root *in the Atzmus* of Hashem Himself. Consequently, he feels how much his avodah touches Above.

**E.** From this it is understood that the matter of joy is the preface to all matters of avodah—for all matters of avodah must be from within the knowledge and feeling that the matter of avodah touches Above.

As is emphasized in the order of avodah of every single day—"that they ordained to give praise and thanks to His Name every morning and to say, 'Happy are we, how good is our portion, etc., and how beautiful is our inheritance,' meaning, just as a person rejoices and is happy with an inheritance that fell to him, a vast fortune for which he did not toil, so too, and even infinitely more, we must rejoice over our inheritance that our forefathers bequeathed to us, etc."[11]

In this, the connection to every single Jew is also emphasized—for, just as the matter of inheritance does not depend at all on the toil of the heir, nor on the intellect of the heir (whether he is wise

or the opposite), but in any case he is an heir, so too regarding the inheritance of "how beautiful is our inheritance," there is no difference between the greatest of the great and the least of the least. Even the least of the least and the lightest of the light rejoices in the inheritance of "how beautiful is our inheritance."

And in the language of the verse at the beginning of the parsha of the week[12]: "You are standing today, all of you, before Hashem your G-d," and it adds and specifies, "your heads, your tribes, etc., from your wood-chopper to your water-drawer"—that even the least among them, who were appointed as "your wood-chopper" and "your water-drawer" because they were not worthy to enter the congregation,[13] are nevertheless also "standing... before Hashem your G-d."

And this is also the explanation of the words of the Rebbe, my father-in-law, that Chai Elul brings *vitality* into all matters of Elul, including the matters of *bitterness* in Elul—because a true and intense bitterness is awakened as a result of the person's feeling of how much his avodah touches Above, and this feeling comes specifically through vitality and joy.

**H.** The Rambam wrote[20]: "The joy with which a person should rejoice in the performance of a mitzvah and in the love of the G-d who commanded them, is a great avodah, and anyone who prevents himself from this joy is worthy of being punished, as it is said,[21] 'Because you did not serve Hashem your G-d with joy and goodness of heart.'"

And the implication of the Rambam's language is that the obligation of joy is in *every* mitzvah *and in every* matter of the avodah of Hashem, meaning, every matter—whatever it may be—through which he serves Hashem, must be done with joy.[22]

From this it is understood that even the matter of bitterness must be with joy—for since bitterness belongs to the *mitzvah and avodah of teshuvah*, it is like all matters of mitzvos and avodah of Hashem that must be performed specifically with joy.

And this matter is understood and emphasized more according to what is explained in the Torah of Chassidus[23]—that the essence of the mitzvos is (not so much the kavanah and the specific virtue in every mitzvah, but) *the fulfillment of the will of Hashem*, and this matter is in all mitzvos *equally*. Consequently, regardless of the differences that exist due to the details of the mitzvos, for example, a mitzvah that belongs to the line of Chesed or a mitzvah that belongs to the line of Gevurah, like bitterness, one must fulfill

**I.** And another matter is emphasized and explained in the Torah of Chassidus—how can bitterness be from within joy:

By way of introduction—until now it was only explained *the necessity* for bitterness to be from within joy (since it is also a mitzvah), and also *the reason and cause* of the joy (fulfillment of the will and command of Hashem), but it was not yet explained *how* bitterness can be from within joy, for since the movement of bitterness and the movement of joy are two *opposite* movements—how is it possible that these two movements should be in a person *at the same time*?!

The remedy for this—as it is in the Zohar[24], "Crying is lodged in my heart on one side, and joy is lodged in my heart on the other side": From the perspective of the feeling of distance from Elokus—which is from the perspective of his *animal soul*—he is in bitterness, and from the perspective that he is fulfilling a mitzvah (the will of Hashem)—which is from the perspective of his *divine soul*—he is in joy. And the distance that is from the animal soul does not disturb the joy that is from the divine soul, as is explained in Tanya[25] that "he should hold his soul dear to rejoice in its joy more than the despised body, so as not to mix and confuse the joy of the soul with the sadness of the body."

And another matter in this—for the aforementioned explanation is not yet sufficient, because, even though the bitterness and the joy come from different places (the bitterness from the animal soul, and the joy from the divine soul), nevertheless, they are found *in one place*, in the physical heart, and how is it possible that two opposite movements should be at the same time in one place—based on the words of the Sifri[26] regarding love and fear (which are also two opposite movements): "There is no love in the place of fear and fear in the place of love, except in the attribute of Hashem alone." And similarly regarding bitterness and joy—that in the avodah of Hashem, both can be together.

**J.** And another matter regarding the joy in bitterness, which is also emphasized in the Torah of Chassidus:

In addition to what was stated above (section H) that the cause of the joy in the avodah of bitterness is from the fulfillment of His will, which is from the *general* point in the avodah of bitterness as in all mitzvos, there is an additional reason from the *specific* nature of the avodah of *bitterness*

By way of introduction to the explanation of the precision of the language of the verse[27] "And I will surely hide My face on that day," "Hester Astir" (doubled)—that the concealment itself is in concealment, meaning, that it is not felt that this is a concealment.[28] The difference between them is—that when one knows and feels the concealment, then the feeling of concealment does not give rest... there is no appetite ("men hot nit kein appetite")... one cannot sleep peacefully... And this is the cause of the joy in bitterness—that from the very fact that *he feels* that he is in concealment, *and he is embittered* in his soul over the concealment, a feeling of joy is created within him for the fact that *he has exited from the double concealment* ("Hester Astir"), and consequently, he will eventually exit also from the first concealment (over which he is embittered).

For "I have set Hashem (and I find and reveal Him even in a matter of) before me (and consequently I have set Him) always."[30]

"You are standing today, all of you, before Hashem your G-d: your heads, your tribes, your elders, and your officers, all the men of Israel, your young children, your wives, and your convert who is within your camp, from the hewer of your wood to the drawer of your water." The subsequent verses explain that the intent of "you are standing" is to enter into the covenant of Hashem your G-d and His oath, and the verse adds, "in order to establish you today as a people to Him, and He will be a G-d to you." Now, "today" refers to Rosh Hashanah, as it is stated in *Likutei Torah* that "today" is Rosh Hashanah—"This day is the beginning of Your works." On this day of Rosh Hashanah, "you are standing, all of you," in order to "enter into the covenant... in order to establish you today as a people to Him, and He will be a G-d to you."

We must understand: what is the novelty of "today," Rosh Hashanah, that at that time there exists the matter of Hashem establishing Israel as a people to Him and He being a G-d to them? Surely this matter exists throughout the entire year, for throughout the year Israel is obligated in Torah and mitzvos, and consequently, they are a people to Him and He is a G-d to them. If so, what is the novelty of Rosh Hashanah?

Furthermore, we must understand the matter of the covenant on Rosh Hashanah—"to enter into the covenant of Hashem your G-d"—and likewise, in the Rosh Hashanah *davening*, we say "He remembers the covenant." Is the matter of a covenant specific to Rosh Hashanah? It exists always in a person's *avodah*. As mentioned in the very verses of the Rosh Hashanah *davening*, there must be a "remembrance of the covenant," as we say, "And also Noach, You remembered with love," as it is written, "And G-d remembered Noach," which refers to the covenant made with Noach. Likewise, there is the covenant that Hashem made with Avraham, as it is written, "On that day Hashem made a covenant with Avram." In addition to this, there is the covenant of Moshe, of which it is written, "Behold, I am making a covenant."

These three types of covenants correspond to three matters. The covenant of Noach is for the existence of the worlds, as the simple meaning of the verses indicates: "All the days of the earth... day and night shall not cease." The covenant of Avraham is for the giving of the land, and in a spiritual sense, it refers to the Torah, as understood from the words of our Sages: "Why is it called *Eretz* (land)? Because it desired (*ratzah*) to do the will of its Creator," for the Torah is the revelation of the Supreme Will. Furthermore, *Eretz* denotes *bitul*, that one nullifies oneself like the dust and earth of the ground, and through this *bitul*, one arrives at the opening of the heart in Torah, as it is written, "And let my soul be as dust to all; open my heart in Your Torah, and my soul will pursue Your mitzvos." And the covenant of Moshe is for the matter of *teshuvah*—that even one who has sinned, blemished, and gone astray, *teshuvah* will still avail him, as it is written, "Forgiving iniquity, transgression, and sin, and cleansing," and concerning this it is written, "Behold, I am making a covenant."

It is understood from all this that the matter of a covenant pertains to several things in the world and in the *avodah* of man. If so, why is it said that the matter of "entering into the covenant" is specifically on Rosh Hashanah? What is the connection between the covenant and Rosh Hashanah?

#### 176

Now, regarding the three types of covenants mentioned above, we find a difference between them. The covenant of Noach was in a manner that had a physical sign, which is the rainbow. Similarly, the covenant of Avraham was through grasping a physical object, as he cut the calf, and "a smoking furnace and a flaming torch passed between these pieces," and Avraham saw the *Shechinah* passing between the pieces. This is not the case with the covenant of Moshe, which had no connection to a physical object.

The reason for this is that the covenant of Noach effected a general refinement in the world. Before the Flood, the world was not as it should be, and the Flood effected purification in the world, as our Sages said regarding the verse, "A land not purified, nor rained upon," from which it is understood that the Flood effected purification in the world. For this reason, the Flood lasted forty days, corresponding to the forty *se'ah* of a *mikveh*, in order to purify the world. Therefore, the rainbow was a sign of the covenant of Noach. Seemingly, this is difficult to understand: the rainbow is a natural phenomenon—when the sun's rays strike the clouds, it causes a rainbow. Since it is a natural thing, how is it a sign of the covenant?

The matter is that before the Flood, the clouds and mists were coarser and thicker, and consequently, even when the light of the sun struck the clouds, it did not produce light and illumination. But after the Flood, the clouds became more refined, and therefore, through the striking of the sun's rays upon the clouds, a rainbow is created. The reason for this is that before the Flood, the world was not as it should be, and therefore the clouds were in a state of coarseness and thickness, and the sun's light was absorbed into them in such a way that no reflected light emerged. Through the Flood effecting refinement in the world, this caused refinement in the clouds as well, so that they became more refined, and therefore there is reflected light from the clouds, which is the rainbow. Thus, there is a physical sign for the covenant of Noach, because since the covenant of Noach is that the world should exist in such a way that "day and night shall not cease," which comes from the side of the general refinement of the world effected by the Flood, the sign for this is from a physical thing in the world—namely, that the rainbow contains a remembrance of the general refinement of the world that was made by the Flood, that by virtue of this refinement, even at a time when the generation is not righteous, there is still existence for the world, and "day and night shall not cease."

#### 177

Similarly, one can understand the matter of the covenant of Avraham, which was in a manner that effected refinement in the physicality of the world. The covenant of Avraham is for the giving of the land, which in a spiritual sense is the matter of Torah and mitzvos (as mentioned above). The fulfillment of mitzvos in general relates to the land—meaning even mitzvos that are not dependent on the land—as it is written, "And this commandment... which Hashem your G-d commanded... to do in the land," which refers there to all the mitzvos and attributes them specifically to the land, because all mitzvos relate to the land, as the Ramban writes. The matter of Torah and mitzvos is to be the connection of the created being with the Creator. The created being, from its own perspective, is a created being and has no connection with the Creator, as it is written, "The heavens are heavens to Hashem, but the earth He has given to the children of man." Through Torah and mitzvos, one effects a connection of the created beings with the Creator. At the time of the Giving of the Torah, there was the revelation of "Hashem descended upon Mount Sinai," and to Moshe He said, "Ascend to Hashem."

The beginning of this matter was with the Patriarchs, as the saying goes, "The deeds of the Patriarchs are a sign for the children." This means that through the deeds of the Patriarchs, it serves as a sign for the children—that the deeds of the Patriarchs give strength to the children so that they can fulfill them. This is what is meant by the fact that the beginning of the two thousand years of Torah is from Avraham Avinu—that the beginning of the connection of the created beings with the Creator was with Avraham Avinu. This is what is meant by "Avraham was one"—that Avraham, through his *avodah*, acted in the world, as it is written, "And he called there in the name of Hashem, the G-d of the world (*E-l Olam*)." The precision here is well-known: not "G-d of the world" (*E-l ha-Olam*), which would imply that G-dliness and the world are two separate things, but "G-d-world" (*E-l Olam*), that G-dliness and the world become one. This is the matter of Torah and mitzvos, through which the connection of the created being with the Creator is made. This is what is meant by "And let my soul be as dust to all; open my heart in Your Torah"—Your Torah, Hashem's Torah—"and in Your mitzvos"—Hashem's mitzvos—"my soul will pursue." Through the fulfillment of the mitzvos, the heart and the physical flesh, as well as the physical things with which one fulfills the mitzvos, become holy and vessels for G-dliness. And since the covenant of Avraham is about the matter of Torah, whose purpose is to effect refinement in the world, therefore the covenant of Avraham also had a grasp on the physicality of the world (as mentioned above).

#### 178

However, the covenant of Moshe, which is about the matter of *teshuvah*, as it is written, "G-d, slow to anger"—He is slow to anger not only for the righteous but also for the wicked—and concerning this was the covenant of Moshe, for in the continuation of this verse of "G-d, slow to anger," it is written, "Behold, I am making a covenant." The matter of *teshuvah* is above the world, for *teshuvah* is from the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy that are above the boundaries of the world, and also the matter of *teshuvah* is above time and space, as it is known that *teshuvah* is in one hour, in one day, and in one moment—above time, and likewise above space and above the vessels, as our Sages said regarding *teshuvah*: "There is one who acquires his world in one hour." That which usually requires the making of vessels, through *teshuvah* he acquires this world in one hour, because it is above the vessels. Therefore, the covenant of Moshe was in a manner that had no grasp on the physicality of the world, since the covenant is about the matter of *teshuvah* which is above the world.

The explanation of this matter in the *avodah* of man: the *avodah* that is analogous to the covenant of Noach is the *avodah* of *Modeh Ani*. Immediately upon waking from his sleep, he says, "I offer thanks before You... great is Your faithfulness." At the time of sleep, the soul ascends on high and draws life for itself. Due to the removal of the soul on high, what remains below is only a spark of vitality along with the body and the animal soul. Nevertheless, immediately upon waking from his sleep, even though he is before *netilas yadayim* and the morning blessings, and only a soul is in his nostrils, he says, "I offer thanks before You." This is because it is the body of a Jew, who is from the seed of Avraham, Yitzchak, and Yaakov; the body of a man of Israel is different from the body of a non-Jew, for from the time of the Giving of the Torah, their filth ceased. Therefore, even as he is before *netilas yadayim* and the morning blessings, he can say "I offer thanks." It is only that due to sleep, there is a remnant of impurity on the tips of his fingers, from which it is possible that there could be a grasp and nourishment, etc., but nevertheless, he can say then, "I offer thanks before You." Although it is only a general thanksgiving, it is a general thanksgiving that encompasses him entirely, and through *Modeh Ani*, he comes to "great is Your faithfulness," which effects an increase in faith and draws it into the world. This matter is analogous to the covenant of Noach, which effected a general refinement in the existence of the world. And after the *bitul* of *Modeh Ani* comes the *avodah* of *tefillah*. The *avodah* of *tefillah* is specifically at the beginning of the day, as Abba Binyamin said, "May my prayer be adjacent to my bed," and the content of the *avodah* of *tefillah* is, as is known, that *tefillah* is "a ladder set on the ground and its top reaches the heavens." This *avodah* is analogous to the covenant of Avraham, which is about the matter of Torah.

By virtue of this choice, every man of Israel, whatever kind of Jew he may be, can say *Modeh Ani*. This is not the case with the *avodah* of *tefillah*—"a ladder set on the ground and its top reaches the heavens"—this comes specifically through *avodah* and toil. This matter is analogous to the covenant of Avraham, which is about Torah (as mentioned above), because Torah comes specifically through *avodah* and toil. Meaning that the contemplation that brought the arousal, and the arousal itself, is in the category of limitation. And all the more so, it is a category of limitation relative to above. Therefore, from the side of the *avodah* of *teshuvah* itself, it is impossible that it should be truly without limit, and that his "without limit" should call forth the "without limit" from above; rather, the drawing down is from the side of the covenant alone.

However, all this is throughout the year, but on Rosh Hashanah, all things return to their source, for the removal is above *Atzilus* and above the *Tzimtzum*. This is what is meant by what we say, "You remember the works of the world and visit all the creatures of old," for the "works of the world" are the matters up to *Atzilus*, and "creatures of old" are above *Atzilus*. Meaning that the removal of Rosh Hashanah is not only from the specific will of the worlds but also from the general will. Therefore, although there are the three matters of the covenant mentioned above, nevertheless, from the side of the removal of Rosh Hashanah, the matter of the covenant is also removed, and therefore there must be a covenant anew, and that is the covenant of Rosh Hashanah. This is what is meant by saying before

#### 183

the Kingship, etc., that "You should make Me King over you," that there is no "people" at all, but that one must make Him, blessed be He, King, and since there is no King without a people, consequently there will be a people, and the making of Him King is through the souls of Israel, as our Sages said regarding the verse, "When the heads of the people were gathered, the tribes of Israel," and regarding the verse, "With the King in His work, they dwelt there"—with whom did He take counsel? With the souls of the righteous, and "Your people are all righteous." Meaning that the souls of Israel are those who draw down the level of Kingship from above the absolute will to the general will and to the specific will. And they effect "in order to establish you today as a people to Him, and He will be a G-d to you." Now, even when there is already a will for Kingship, nevertheless, one must remind Him, blessed be He, and this is the matter of the *Zichronos* (Remembrances), for since this is a thing that does not take up space, therefore one must remind Him of it. For a small thing, one must remind, and it is possible that it might be in concealment. Just as below, although every Jew has the essential love for Hashem, nevertheless it is a hidden love and one must reveal it from concealment to revelation. So too it is above, that there must be the matter of the *Zichronos* so that it will be in revelation. And with what? With the *shofar*, for all the drawings down of Rosh Hashanah are through the *shofar*, for the sound of the *shofar* is a simple sound, and furthermore, it is a *shofar* of an animal, and it turns out that the sound of the *shofar* is the sound of an animal. There is an "inner voice that is heard" and an "inner voice that is not heard," and the sound of the *shofar* is the "inner voice that is not heard," and furthermore, it is the sound of an animal, meaning that it is in total *bitul*, that he is literally equal with an animal, as it is written, "Man and animal You save, Hashem." Since on Rosh Hashanah all things return to their source, there is nothing at all, and consequently he is in total *bitul*, that he is literally equal with the animal. This *bitul* is above even the *avodah* of *teshuvah* which is above reason and intellect, because the *avodah* of *teshuvah* is that he feels the limitation of reason and intellect, and because of this his *avodah* is above reason and intellect. But the *bitul* of the *shofar* is that he has nothing at all, for since everything returns to its source, he has nothing at all, and he is poor and destitute, and through the *bitul* of the *shofar*, which is absolute *bitul*, through this he draws down the level of Kingship.

**This is** "You are standing today, all of you, before Hashem your G-d: your heads, your tribes... from the hewer of your wood to the drawer of your water." Since "today" is Rosh Hashanah, when everything returns to its source, on this day "you are standing, all of you, before Hashem," where there, your heads,

#### 184

etc., are literally equal with the hewer of your wood and the drawer of your water, who are the subjects who are not worthy to enter the congregation. And since everything returns to its source, where everything is literally equal, for this reason there must be the drawing down of the level of Kingship, and this is through "you are standing, all of you," etc.

The explanation of the matter in the *avodah* of man: just as there are ten types from your heads to the drawer of your water in the totality of Israel, so too it is in each one individually, that he has ten types, and there must be "you are standing, all of you"—both your heads, which is the *avodah* of reason and intellect, and higher than that, the *avodah* that is above reason and intellect, for even the skull is included in the head—and it must be equal with the hewer of your wood and the drawer of your water, which simply means the *avodah* of saying the letters of the Torah. Therefore, the Rebbe Rayatz warned to cherish every moment of Rosh Hashanah and to increase the letters of the Torah and the letters of the *tefillah*, and especially the letters of *Tehillim*. And something similar must be done in the days of preparation for Rosh Hashanah. Now, how will a person effect in himself that he reaches this level where everything is equal for him? This is through contemplating that on Rosh Hashanah he stands before Hashem, meaning above even the Name *Havayah* from above. For in the Name *Havayah* from above, it is written, "And Hashem called out, Hashem," for "called out" is an expression of drawing down. But on Rosh Hashanah, he is at the level of "before Hashem," where there everything is equal, as it is written, "You have made them all with wisdom," for the wisdom of *Adam Kadmon* and physical action are literally equal. And since there everything is equal, he sees that he is poor and destitute, that he has nothing at all, which is the content of the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah. As we say in *Selichos*, "Not by merit and not by deeds do we come before You... Yours, Hashem, is the righteousness, and ours is the shame of face." Because of this, that ours is the shame of face, for this reason one must rely on "Yours, Hashem, is the righteousness." Through this, that he reminds himself that everything returns to its source and he is poor and destitute—which is not by way of contemplation but by way of remembrance alone—through this he effects the matter of the *Zichronos* above and the drawing down of the Kingship.

**This is** the explanation of the expression "you are standing" specifically, that even at a time when the whole world is collapsing, you are standing. For on the day of Rosh Hashanah, the whole world is collapsing, for everything

returns to its source, and nevertheless, "you are standing," for the souls of Israel, who are rooted in His Essence, blessed be He, are confident in the drawing down of the Kingship. And these are the two matters of Rosh Hashanah: although Rosh Hashanah is the day of the birth of the world, and the world trembles, nevertheless, we wear white, etc., for we are confident that we will be victorious in judgment. For although "you are standing today"—that one must present himself on "today," Rosh Hashanah, before Hashem—and the Satan is also among them and he demands his portion, for although it is a statute for Israel, Jews are coming, nevertheless he claims, "Judgment to the G-d of Yaakov," what is with the G-dliness of Yaakov? And nevertheless, we wear white, for the souls of Israel are confident, because in their *avodah* they draw down the level of Kingship, and they are rooted in His Essence, therefore they are confident that there will be a good writing and sealing, in the good that is seen and revealed.
