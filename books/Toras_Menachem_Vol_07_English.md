## 5713. Second Day of Rosh Hashanah

"Today is the birthday of the world; today He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment." We must understand: Rosh Hashanah is the day Adam HaRishon was created. The world was created on the 25th of Elul, and Rosh Hashanah—the sixth day of the six days of creation—is the day Adam HaRishon was created. If so, it is difficult to understand the phrase "Today" (referring to Rosh Hashanah) "He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment." What is the connection between the creation of Adam HaRishon and all the creatures of the worlds, such that on the day Adam was created, all creatures of the worlds are judged? This is also the essence of the question raised in Chassidic discourses regarding the statement, "This day (Rosh Hashanah) is the beginning of Your works." After all, the world was created on the 25th of Elul, and Rosh Hashanah is the day of Adam’s creation; how can we say of Rosh Hashanah, "This day is the beginning of Your works"? Even if one could answer that we call the day of man’s creation "the beginning of Your works" because man is the purpose of creation—as the purpose of creation is for the sake of man —it is still not understood why it says "This day is the *beginning* of Your works." Seemingly, it should say, "This day is the *essence* of Your works."

The matter is that from the statement "This day is the beginning of Your works," we understand that besides the aspect of "the beginning of Your works," there is also an aspect of "the end of the work," as in the expression, "The end of the work is in the initial thought." This is the meaning of "This day is the beginning of Your works": although the world was created on the 25th of Elul, that is merely the aspect of "the end of the work." It is specifically today, Rosh Hashanah—the day of man’s creation—that is the "beginning of Your works."

2) This can be understood by first explaining the concept of "the end of the work is in the initial thought." The meaning is (not the simple interpretation, but) that the "end of the work" of the higher level comes into the "initial thought" of the lower level. This is as explained in the commentaries on *Sefer Yetzirah* regarding the statement, "Their end is embedded in their beginning"—the end of the higher level is embedded in the beginning of the lower level. The explanation of this: "Thought" refers to the world of Atzilus, for thought is the aspect of Chochma, as the saying goes: "Thought and *Yuvla* (Binah) are never separated." Since *Abba Ila'ah* (Chochma) nests in Atzilus, and Chochma is thought, Atzilus is called "thought"—especially the aspect of Chochma within it, which is the beginning and essence of Atzilus, and thus the beginning of thought. The point is that thought is not merely a garment, but a power of the soul—that is, the power of expression of the soul. When there is to be an expression from the soul, it occurs through the power of thought, which is the power of expression; through this power, it is revealed in thought (and subsequently in speech and action). Nevertheless, thought is not the soul itself, but rather a garment for the soul. Thus, thought has both an advantage and a deficiency: regarding the levels below it (speech and action), thought is not merely a separate garment but a unified garment; furthermore, thought is the power of the soul—the power of expression. However, regarding what is above it, thought is only a garment; that is, in thought, one does not find what is above it.

It is the same in Atzilus, which is called "thought." The world of Atzilus is not a new creation, but merely the revelation of the hidden from what is above Atzilus and above the Tzimtzum. Even though there is the limitation of the ten Sefiros in Atzilus—"ten and not nine, ten and not eleven" —they are nonetheless "ten Sefiros of *Bli Mah* (nothingness)," and it is merely a revelation of the hidden. This applies not only to the lights of Atzilus, which are a revelation of the hidden, but also to the vessels, which are in a state of measurement and limitation; they, too, are a revelation of the hidden, revealing the hiddenness that existed before the Tzimtzum. For from the perspective of the potential estimation before the Tzimtzum, or from the perspective of the point of the *Reshimu*, even when [the light] returned and shone, the vessels were formed in measurement and limitation. This is what is meant by the vessels being a revelation of the hidden: they reveal the hiddenness that existed before the Tzimtzum. The general nature of Atzilus is one of "closeness," for *Atzilus* comes from the word *etzlo* (with Him) and *samuch* (near). Not only are the lights in a state of closeness, but the vessels are as well, for "He and His life are one; He and His vessels are one." The aspect of "He" shines in "His life," and "He" shines in "His vessels." Because "His life" senses "He," and "His vessels" sense "He," they are "one"—this is why Atzilus is related to *etzlo* and *samuch*. But on the other hand, *Atzilus* also comes from the word *atzlah* (emanation) and *hafrashah* (separation), meaning it exists as a "thing." Atzilus is also a "world," and "world" (*olam*) comes from the word *he'elem* (hiddenness) and *hester* (concealment). This means that even Atzilus is in a state of existence, but the existence of Atzilus is in a state of total *bitul*—*bitul b'metzius* (nullification in existence). Above Atzilus, one cannot say that existence is in a state of *bitul*, because there is no existence there at all. But Atzilus is in a state of existence, yet as soon as it comes into being, it is nullified to be in a state of *bitul b'metzius*.

This is also true of the *Kav* (the line). The beginning of the revelation of the *Kav* is in Chochma, as it is written, "Hashem acquired me as the beginning of His way," "The beginning of Chochma." This refers to the Chochma of *Adam Kadmon*. The general revelation of the *Kav* is a revelation of the hidden—that which returned and drew forth from the *Ohr Ein Sof* that was before the Tzimtzum. From this perspective, it is a revelation of the hidden. However, because it came through the interruption of the Tzimtzum, it is in the state of a "short line." Regarding this, they said, "The end of the work is in the initial thought"—that which is drawn down and revealed in the *Seder Hishtalshelus*, even at the beginning of the *Hishtalshelus* (in the initial thought), is only the very last aspect. This is the concept of "the end of the work," for "work" is the lowest aspect, and within that itself there is an "end of the work," and this aspect is clothed and revealed in the beginning of the *Hishtalshelus*, in the initial thought.

The explanation of this: It is known from the interpretation of our Sages that before the world was created (and it is stated in the books of Kabbalah and Chassidus that this is also true before Atzilus was emanated), "He and His Name were alone." "He" and "His Name" are both in the state of the light (for from the very fact that there are different levels called by specific names, it is understood that all of this is only in the state of the light). That is, "He" also refers to the aspect of the light, but "He" is the essence of the light, and "His Name" is the expansion of the light. Within "His Name" (the expansion of the light) itself, there are also two levels: revelation to Himself and revelation to another. Even though the general light before the Tzimtzum is above the aspect of "another," there are still two levels within it: the aspect of revelation to Himself, which gives no place to "another" at all (even while being in the state of the expansion of light, it is a revelation to Himself that gives no place to "another"), and the aspect of revelation to "another"—even though it is above the aspect of "another," it itself gives place to "another" and has a connection to "another." The proof of this is that after the Tzimtzum, "another" is created from it, which is because even before the Tzimtzum, it had a connection to "another," to the point that it estimated within itself, in potential, everything that would eventually be in actuality. This level of "revelation to another" within the expansion of the light is the concept of "the end of the work." The general category of "work" is the lowest level, for the vitality in "work" is incomparable to the vitality in speech and thought. Within this itself, "the end of the work" points to a lower level; it is the concept of the *mazalos*, which are like the drawing down of hair through the interruption of the skull—a separate drawing down, as stated in *Etz Chaim* as if to say, the *Malchus* of the *Malchus* of *Ein Sof*. This aspect of "the end of the work" is drawn down and revealed in the initial thought, in the aspect of Chochma—this is the concept of "Abba nursing from the *Mazal*." It is the same with the *Kav*: "This precious holy thread... is called *Mazal*," which is like the drawing down of hair through the interruption of the Tzimtzum. This means that even in the highest level of "initial thought," only the lowest aspect of "the end of the work" is drawn down.

3) However, the purpose of creation is that there be a revelation of the *Atzmus* (the Essence) specifically, not merely the aspect of "the end of the work." For the purpose of creation is what the Holy One, Blessed be He, desired: to have a dwelling place in the lower worlds. The concept of a "dwelling place" is like a person’s dwelling below, where the essence and being of the person dwells in the home—"The glory of man is to dwell in a house," meaning the essence of the person. Likewise, the purpose of a "dwelling place in the lower worlds" is for there to be a revelation of the *Atzmus* specifically. This matter is drawn down through the *avodah* of the souls of Israel specifically, for "Israel arose in the thought," meaning in the highest level of thought, which is the inwardness of thought. Therefore, through their *avodah*, the drawing down of the *Atzmus* is accomplished.

This is the meaning of "This day is the beginning of Your works." Although the world was created on the 25th of Elul, within the creation itself, only the aspect of "the end of the work" is revealed. The innovation of today, Rosh Hashanah—the day of Adam’s creation—is that through the *avodah* of the souls in general, and specifically in the month of Elul and especially during the Ten Days of Repentance, when the *avodah* is in a state of inwardness and reaches the inwardness of Above (as it is written, "Seek My face"), the drawing down is accomplished (not only from the aspect of "the end of the work," but also) from the aspect of "the beginning of Your works." This is also the meaning of "Today He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment"—through the *avodah* of Israel, the drawing down of "the beginning of Your works" is accomplished in all created beings, all the creatures of the worlds.

4) One must still understand what the innovation of Rosh Hashanah is specifically, for the *avodah* of the souls of Israel exists throughout the entire year—"Every day one performs [His] work," and every day there is the *avodah* of "to work it and to guard it." "To work it" is the 248 positive commandments, and "to guard it" is the 365 negative commandments. What is the innovation of Rosh Hashanah specifically? The matter is that the *avodah* throughout the year is *avodah* based on reason and intellect (*ta'am v'da'as*). Because the *avodah* is based on *ta'am v'da'as*, it only reaches Above to the levels that are in a state of measurement and limitation. Even the *avodah* of prayer, about which it is said, "A ladder set on the ground and its top reaches the heavens"—even though it reaches the level of "the heavens," because it is an *avodah* based on *ta'am v'da'as*, it only reaches Above to levels that are in measurement and limitation. Even the *avodah* of *Krias Shema*, which is an *avodah* above *ta'am v'da'as*—for the concept of *Krias Shema* is to surrender one's soul in "One," a surrender of the soul that is above *ta'am v'da'as*, "with all your soul and with all your might" —nevertheless, because even the *avodah* of *Krias Shema* comes through *ta'am v'da'as* (for through contemplation in general, and specifically in *Pesukei D'Zimra* and the blessings of *Krias Shema*, one feels that "Hashem our God, Hashem is One" —meaning that when one contemplates with *ta'am v'da'as*, one then grasps with *ta'am v'da'as* that one must go beyond *ta'am v'da'as*), his surrender of the soul is also according to *ta'am v'da'as*. That is, his surrender of the soul is a feeling of the intellect, and therefore it is only a surrender of the soul in potential, not in actuality. Therefore, even through the *avodah* of *Krias Shema*, one only reaches Above to levels that are in measurement and limitation. Although *Krias Shema* is the concept of "to all who call upon Him," "to Him and not to His attributes," and there are two interpretations of this (the interpretation of the *Pardes* that "to Him" means the aspect of the light clothed in the vessels, and the interpretation of the Baal Shem Tov that this is the aspect of Divinity within the vessels themselves; and higher still, "to Him" refers to a level above both, which encompasses both), nevertheless, all of this is only in measurement and limitation, because it is an *avodah* based on *ta'am v'da'as*. This is not the case with the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah, which is an *avodah* from the inwardness of the heart and the inwardness of the soul, as it is written, "Seek My face," and "As water reflects the face to the face..." Through this, one draws down the inwardness of Above. Therefore, Rosh Hashanah specifically is "the beginning of Your works."

5) The explanation of this in *avodah*: It is known that there are two modes of *avodah*, the *avodah* of *iskafya* (subjugation) of the *sitra achra*, and the *avodah* of *is'hapcha* (transformation) of darkness into light. Within the *avodah* of *is'hapcha* itself, there are several aspects. The matter is that there are two opinions regarding the nature of darkness: the first opinion is that darkness is merely the absence of light, and the second opinion is that darkness is a creation in its own right. According to the first opinion, that darkness is the absence of light, it is not possible to transform the darkness itself into light, for there is no existence of darkness at all. The meaning of "transforming darkness into light" is only regarding the place where there was darkness, that there should no longer be darkness there, and no trace of darkness should remain, but rather there should be light there. This is the intention of our Sages that "willful sins are transformed into merits"—that in the person who had willful sins, no trace of the sins should remain, and there should be only merits. But according to this interpretation, the term "transforming darkness into light" is not entirely clear. Therefore, it can be interpreted that the concept of "transforming darkness into light" refers to the sparks of holiness that fell and were separated from their source until their light was extinguished (Heaven forbid), and for this reason they are called "darkness"—the sparks of holiness exist in reality, but one must transform them into light. This is the concept of "transforming darkness into light." All this is according to the first opinion, that darkness is the absence of light. But the second opinion is that darkness is an existence, and this is the truth. The proof for this is from what is written, "And darkness was upon the face of the deep." Since this was before the creation of light through the command "Let there be light," it is impossible to say that darkness is the absence of light. When the existence of light is present, one can say and negate that darkness is the absence of light; but when there is no existence of light, and there is not even a premise of the existence of light, it is not possible to negate the existence of light. Since the concept of darkness exists before the creation of light, one must conclude that darkness is a creation in its own right. This is also proven by the statement, "In the beginning darkness, and then light," and also from the statement that "light and darkness serve in confusion," that darkness is not only the absence of light, but a creation in its own right. According to this opinion, the term "transforming darkness into light" is more appropriate—that is, to transform the darkness itself into light. This is also the concept of "willful sins are transformed into merits"—the willful sins themselves are transformed into merits. This is the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur: to transform willful sins into merits, which is the concept of "transforming darkness into light." Through this *avodah*, the drawing down of "the beginning of Your works" is accomplished.

6) To understand the reason why it is specifically through the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light" that one draws down the aspect of "the beginning of Your works": the matter in short is that, seemingly, it is not understood how it is possible to transform darkness into light. It is understandable that the *avodah* of *iskafya* is because this concept already existed for worlds—before the Tzimtzum, the infinite light shone, and the power of limitation was in concealment; this is the root of the *avodah* of *iskafya*, to subjugate the darkness from the perspective of the light. But the *avodah* of *is'hapcha*, to transform the darkness itself into light—one must understand how it is possible to transform darkness into light, for light and darkness are two created beings, and likewise in their root they are two distinct things: the power of limitation and the power of the infinite. Just as the *Ohr Ein Sof* has the power of the infinite, so too it has the power of limitation. That is, they are two concepts: infinite and finite, and in other terms, concealment and revelation. How is it possible to transform darkness into light? The matter is that the power for the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light" comes from a level above the infinite and the finite, where there is no "finite" and "infinite," but only His power in the finite and His power in the infinite. In truth, one cannot use the term "power" for this, but only "ability"—His ability in the finite and His ability in the infinite, His ability to conceal and His ability to reveal. There are not two concepts here—His ability to conceal and His ability to reveal—but rather one concept: the concept of "ability," that His ability is in everything, to conceal and to reveal. This means not only that the two concepts are equal, but that it is one concept: His ability (blessed be He) is in everything, for He is higher than high, to no end, and lower than low, to no limit. From the perspective of the concept of "ability," which is above revelation and concealment, light and darkness, the transformation of darkness into light can occur. It is the same in *avodah*: from the perspective of the essence of the soul, which is above the powers, the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light" can occur. This is why through the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light," one draws down the aspect of "the beginning of Your works"—because the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light" is from the perspective of the essence of the soul, it reaches the aspect of "ability" that is above concealment and revelation, and through this, the aspect of "the beginning of Your works" is drawn down.

7) This is the meaning of "This day is the beginning of Your works." Rosh Hashanah is the day of the creation of Adam HaRishon, who said, "Come, let us bow down and kneel, let us bend the knee before Hashem our Maker," and through this, he drew down the aspect of Kingship, as it is written, "Hashem is King, He has donned majesty." It is the same every year: through the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah, where the souls of Israel accept upon themselves the yoke of His Kingship (blessed be He), they draw down the aspect of Will and Delight in the matter of Kingship. This is the concept of "the beginning of Your works"—the beginning of the Will and Delight in the attribute of Kingship. This is what our Sages said: "Say before Him [the verses of] Kingship..." "Before Him" specifically, "before Hashem." The drawing down is through the *Shofar*, for the voice of the *Shofar* is (not only a voice that is heard, but) "an inner voice that is not heard," for the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah is from the perspective of the inwardness of the soul, as it is written, "Seek My face." The *avodah* that is from the perspective of the inwardness of the soul is the *avodah* of *is'hapcha*, through which willful sins are transformed into merits. Through the *avodah* of *iskafya*, willful sins are transformed into inadvertent sins, but through the *avodah* of *is'hapcha*, willful sins are transformed into merits. Through this, one draws down the aspect of "the beginning of Your works," and higher still than the aspect of "the beginning of Your works," so that there will be drawn down not only the aspect of "revelation to another," and not only the aspect of "revelation to Himself," but also the essence of the light, and even more than that, the drawing down of the *Atzmus* itself—which is the ultimate purpose of the "dwelling place in the lower worlds."

8) This is also the meaning of "Today He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment"—that through the drawing down of the aspect of "the beginning of Your works" in the souls of Israel, it is also drawn down into the *Seder Hishtalshelus*. Just as we see below, that a person’s thoughts, speech, and actions when he has no delight in the matter are not at all like his thoughts, speech, and actions when he has delight in the matter—meaning that through the drawing down of delight, the very act of thought, speech, and action is performed in a different manner—so too it is understood Above: when the inwardness of the light (the inner delight and the purpose of creation) is drawn down into the souls of Israel, then all created beings are in a more exalted state. As we say at the conclusion of the blessing of Kingship, "Hashem, the God of Israel, is King, and His Kingship rules over all." This is "Today He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment"—that through the drawing down of the inwardness of the light into the souls of Israel on the day of Rosh Hashanah, the inwardness of the light is also drawn down into all the creatures of the worlds. This is the innovation of Rosh Hashanah: that then the inwardness of the light is drawn down into the externality of the light, and this is the fulfillment of the ultimate purpose of the "dwelling place in the lower worlds," which is accomplished through the ultimate perfection of the *avodah* of "transforming darkness into light."

The matter is that although even before the Tzimtzum the inwardness of the light shone in the externality of the light, nevertheless, before the Tzimtzum, the externality of the light did not exist in reality; rather, the externality was included in the inwardness. This is not the ultimate purpose, for the ultimate purpose is a "dwelling place in the lower worlds"—that the externality of the light should exist in reality, and as it exists in its reality, the inwardness of the light should shine within it. Because of this purpose, the Tzimtzum had to be in the manner of total removal. Seemingly, it is not understood why the Tzimtzum had to be in the manner of total removal and why there had to be a subsequent drawing down of the *Kav*, and why the *Kav* did not remain from the beginning. The matter is that at first, when the light of the *Kav* was shining, it was not possible for the vessels to come into being. After the vessels were already formed, they could receive the light of the *Kav*, but at first (before their formation), when the *Kav* was shining, it was not possible for the vessels to come into being (meaning they were included in the light). Since the purpose is that there be an existence of the vessels, and that as they exist in reality they should receive the light of the *Kav* within them, therefore the Tzimtzum was in the manner of total removal, through which the vessels were formed, and afterwards the *Kav* was drawn into them. It is the same with the formation of the human body: the human body is different from the bodies of other creatures. At first, there was the creation of his body, as it is written, "And Hashem God formed man of dust from the ground," and afterwards, "He blew into his nostrils the soul of life." The reason for this is from his root Above: the vessels were formed first, and afterwards the *Kav* was drawn into them; therefore, the body was formed first, and afterwards the soul was drawn into it. The matter is that if the creation of the body and the soul were as one, then the body would not be in its reality, but would be included in the soul. This is not the ultimate purpose; rather, the body should be in its reality—an existence such that it was given the choice to be able to conceal and hide the light of the soul, and as it is in its reality, the light of the soul should shine within it. Because of this purpose, the formation of the body was first, so that the body, as it is in its reality, should have the light of the soul shine within it. This is the innovation of Rosh Hashanah: that through the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah, the purpose of the "dwelling place in the lower worlds" is completed. Although even before the Tzimtzum the inwardness of the light shone in the externality of the light, at that time the externality of the light did not exist in reality. But through the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah, "Today He causes all creatures of the worlds to stand in judgment"—in all the creatures of the worlds, as they are in their reality, the inwardness of the light is drawn down. This is why on Rosh Hashanah the good writing and sealing occurs, so that even the material influence should be from His full hand, etc., and in an expansive manner, as it is from the perspective of the "Essential Expansion," the true infinite. That is, through the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah, which is from the perspective of the inwardness of the soul—"Seek My face"—there will be a drawing down of the inwardness of Above even into material matters. May we all, within the entirety of Israel, merit a good writing and sealing in a revealed and manifest good.

## Sicha of the Second Day of Rosh Hashanah, 5713

**A.** [The Rebbe washed his hands for the meal, and subsequently said:]
Since this entire meal (at the conclusion of the second day of Rosh Hashanah) is a novelty, we can add to it another novelty—that the entire gathering should say "L'chaim," request a blessing, and so on. Through this unification ("az m'vet zich kolel zein") with the collective—all those present here, and indeed throughout the entire world—they will bring this about.
[After saying L'chaim, the Rebbe instructed that the "Beinoni" nigun and the Alter Rebbe’s nigun be played. Afterwards, the Rebbe recited the blessing over the cup, and following Birchas Hamazon, he distributed from the "Kos Shel Bracha" to all those present.]

***

**B.** The Rebbe delivered a maamar on the theme of "Hayom Haras Olam."

***

**C.** Since Bnei Yisrael request from Hashem—and through their request, they bring it about—that there be an *hemshech* (drawing down) from the level of "the beginning of Your works," meaning not only from a level whose purpose is revelation to another, nor even only from a level of revelation to oneself, but rather from the very essence of the light, and even higher, from the level of the *Atzmus* (Essence) itself, and from there it should be drawn down below into this physical world—meaning that the material influence should be "from His full, open, holy, and expansive hand," stemming from the *bli gevul* (limitlessness) of the level of *Atzmus* (as discussed earlier in the maamar)—

The vessel for this is the action we take regarding the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah (through which the *hemshech* from the level of "the beginning of Your works" is accomplished, as mentioned in the maamar). This means that the *hisorerus* (awakening) of Rosh Hashanah and the good resolutions made on Rosh Hashanah should be drawn down in practice throughout the entire year. In other words, the *avodah* of keeping the *mitzvos* throughout the entire year should be as it is when driven by the *hisorerus* of Rosh Hashanah.

When the *avodah* is such that the *hisorerus* of Rosh Hashanah is felt throughout the entire year, we effect the same above—that the *hemshech* of "the beginning of Your works" from Rosh Hashanah is drawn down into the reality of the world below, even in material terms.

**D.** And since "a person is judged only according to his deeds at *that moment*" —when one resolves on Rosh Hashanah to extend the *hisorerus* of Rosh Hashanah throughout the entire year, one is then judged according to "that moment," resulting in material *hemshech*—in children, life, and ample sustenance—in a manner of *bli gevul*, as it is from the *bli gevul* of the level of *Atzmus*.

The concept here is, as is known, that for Bnei Yisrael, materiality comes from spirituality, as it is written, "For it is a statute for Yisrael, a judgment for the G-d of Yaakov." This means that the matter of "a statute for Yisrael"—from the expression "provide me with my allotted bread", which refers to material matters —is drawn from the "judgment for the G-d of Yaakov," the G-dliness of Yaakov, which refers to spiritual matters.

Therefore, when there is the matter of "the beginning of Your works" in spirituality—which is the resolution that one’s conduct throughout the entire year will be in accordance with the *hisorerus* of Rosh Hashanah, and Hashem combines a good thought with action —then it is accomplished in materiality as well, such that the material influence is in a manner of *bli gevul*.

May we be inscribed and sealed immediately in the Book of the Righteous ; a *kesivah v'chasimah tovah* for a good and sweet year, among all of Klal Yisrael.
[The Rebbe instructed to play "Nie Zhuritzi Chloptzi."]

***

**E.** [After Maariv and Havdalah, the Rebbe said:]
Although the matter of Havdalah, "He who separates between holy and profane," is a concealment of the holiness of Shabbos and Yom Tov, nevertheless, the intent of this concealment is so that there should be a *search* for the holiness of Shabbos and Yom Tov, like the parable of a father who hides himself from his son so that the son will search for him.

This matter is easier immediately after Rosh Hashanah, since the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah provides the strength so that throughout the entire year one can search for and find the matters of Rosh Hashanah.

(The Rebbe concluded:) May Hashem grant that there be this matter of searching, and in the words of the verse : "Seek Hashem," and then there will be "when He is found"; that there be "Call upon Him," and then—"while He is near."
*Kesivah v'chasimah tovah* for a good and sweet year.

**1.** First and foremost, I wish to express my gratitude for the effort you made to come here.
In accordance with the custom among Bnei Yisrael to wish one another *Kesivah Vachasimah Tovah* throughout the months of Elul and Tishrei, I wish you a *Chasimah* and *Gemar Chasimah Tovah*, in a manner of revealed and manifest good.
"Good"—in the eyes of the Ribono shel Olam—can sometimes mean that the result will be good; that is, the *purpose* of the matter is good. However, since for human beings every day is considered a distinct entity, I wish for you that in every single day you will see the good *of that day*. That is to say, not only good from the *calculation* that it will lead to a positive result, but rather, revealed and manifest good.

**2.** Bnei Yisrael hope and are confident that on Rosh Hashanah, the Ribono shel Olam granted them and their families a good and sweet year in every detail—children, life, and ample sustenance—in a manner of revealed and manifest good.
However, since there is no limit to good ("*Tzu gut iz doch kein shiur nito*"), the Ribono shel Olam gave us the days between Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur—the Aseres Yemei Teshuvah—so that through our avodah during these days, we can merit that on Yom Kippur, the Ribono shel Olam will give even more, "from His full, open, holy, and expansive hand", in accordance with the capacity of the Ribono shel Olam.
What is the avodah required during these days? Regarding this, we say in the Machzor: "Teshuvah, Tefillah, and Tzedakah," for through these three things, one influences the Ribono shel Olam to give from His full and expansive hand.
As a preface: at first glance, it appears that these three things exist also among the nations of the world (lehavdil), for they too have the concepts of "repentance" (Teshuvah), "prayer" (Tefillah), and "charity" (Tzedakah). In truth, however, there is a vast and essential difference in these three things as they exist among Bnei Yisrael, compared—lehavdil—to how they exist among the nations. This difference is expressed even in the very words themselves: *Teshuvah, Tefillah, and Tzedakah*.

**3.** Teshuvah:
The precise translation of the word "repentance" in Lashon HaKodesh is *charatah* (regret). But among Bnei Yisrael, it is not called *charatah*, but rather *Teshuvah*.
The difference between *charatah* and *Teshuvah* is diametrically opposed:
*Charatah* emphasizes something new. A person is sorry for having done something not good, or for not having done something good, and wishes to conduct himself in a new way.
*Teshuvah* emphasizes returning. A Yid is good in essence, and his desire is to do good. It is only that due to various circumstances—not his fault at all, or only partially his fault—he did something not good. But in essence, he is good.
This is the content of *Teshuvah*: returning to one’s root and source, to one’s inner "I" ("*tzu zein ineveyniksten ich*"), and revealing that inner "I" in such a way that he becomes the "master" of his life.
Therefore, the concept of *Teshuvah* exists for every single Yid. Even for a tzaddik, *Teshuvah* is relevant, meaning that he always strives to reach his inner "I" and reveal it. And for a rasha, the avodah of *Teshuvah* is also relevant, for no matter how low his state may be, it is always within his power and possibility to do *Teshuvah*, since he does not need to create something new, but only to return to his inner "I."

**4.** Tefillah:
The precise translation of the word "prayer" in Lashon HaKodesh is *bakashah* (request). But among Bnei Yisrael, it is not called *bakashah*, but rather *Tefillah*.
The difference between *bakashah* and *Tefillah* is also diametrically opposed: *Bakashah* means petition, while *Tefillah* means connection. *Bakashah* emphasizes asking the Ribono shel Olam to give, from above to below, what one lacks. When one lacks nothing, or has no desire for anything, *bakashah* is not relevant. *Tefillah* emphasizes connection with the Ribono shel Olam, from below to above. This concept is relevant for everyone at all times.
Every Yid has a neshamah that is connected to the Ribono shel Olam. However, because the neshamah has descended into a body and become attached to it, it must engage with physical matters, such as eating, drinking, and the like, and during that time, its connection with the Ribono shel Olam weakens. Therefore, specific times of the day were established for *Tefillah*, to renew and strengthen the connection with the Ribono shel Olam.
Thus, the concept of *Tefillah* exists—in full force—even for those who lack nothing, because *Tefillah* is not merely petition and request; the primary essence of *Tefillah* is the renewal and refreshing ("*der opfrish*") of a person’s connection and *dveikus* with the Ribono shel Olam.

**5.** Tzedakah:
The precise translation of the word "charity" in Lashon HaKodesh is *chesed* (kindness). But among Bnei Yisrael, it is not called *chesed*, but rather *Tzedakah*.
The difference between *chesed* and *Tzedakah* is also diametrically opposed:
*Chesed* emphasizes that he is a "good person." That is: the other party does not deserve it, and he is not obligated to give, but out of his own *goodness*, he gives.
In contrast, *Tzedakah* emphasizes the justice ("*gerechtigkeit*") of the matter. This is in two ways:
a) He is *obligated* to give to the other. He knows that he is not giving his own; the Ribono shel Olam gave him these funds as a deposit so that he would give them to others.
b) Since he needs the Ribono shel Olam to give to him—and the Ribono shel Olam is certainly not obligated to him—he must and is *obligated* to act with the principle of "measure for measure", to give to others even when he is not obligated to them. Through this, he awakens the upper realms so that the Ribono shel Olam will give him more than he is entitled to, since he, too, gave to others more than his own capacity.
Especially *Tzedakah* that concerns the many, and particularly *Tzedakah* for Torah institutions, where every student being educated in these institutions constitutes a foundation for the future building of a home in Yisrael and a future guide in his environment. When one gives *Tzedakah* for such institutions, one can come before the Ribono shel Olam and say: "I have given more than my own strength for Your 'business,' so to speak; You, too, give more than I am entitled to for *my* needs."

**6.** This is the content of the three things—*Teshuvah, Tefillah, and Tzedakah*—through which one merits a *Chasimah* and *Gemar Chasimah Tovah*:
When one returns to the inner "I" (*Teshuvah*), connects to the Ribono shel Olam (*Tefillah*), and gives *Tzedakah* in a manner of "justice" (*Tzedakah*)—then, in addition to the good that the Ribono shel Olam gave on Rosh Hashanah, He gives even more on Yom Kippur: a good and sweet year in a manner of revealed and manifest good.

**7.** What was said above (section 6)—that the way to influence the Ribono shel Olam to give and bestow upon a person more than he is entitled to is through giving *Tzedakah* in general, and specifically *Tzedakah* for Torah institutions that produce students who illuminate the entire world with the light of Torah—is emphasized even more when it comes to giving *Tzedakah* for the institutions of Tomchei Temimim Lubavitch:
The founders of Yeshivas Tomchei Temimim—the Rebbe (Rashab) and the Rebbe (Rayatz)—established that the students of the yeshivah should study the revealed Torah together with the study of Chassidus, as this study (the study of Chassidus) infuses vitality and joy into the entirety of Torah. Furthermore, the founders of the yeshivah ingrained ("*haben eingevurzelt*") in the students to disseminate their methods and perspectives in their own environments as well, so that through this, they illuminate the entire world.
Therefore, when one enables Yeshivas Tomchei Temimim to educate its students in the appropriate manner, including bringing new students into the yeshivah—where each of these students will be a shining candle in an entire community of Yidden—one is thereby illuminating many communities of Bnei Yisrael through "the mitzvah candle and the Torah light", and the light within the Torah, which is the study of Chassidus. And since this is certainly part of the Ribono shel Olam’s "business," through this, the Ribono shel Olam will give him for his own affairs "from His full... and expansive hand."

**8.** [After Rabbi Shmarya Gurary told the Rebbe that the *baalei-batim* wanted to hear something regarding the "dinner", the Rebbe said:]
Chazal said: "All of a person’s sustenance is determined for him from Rosh Hashanah until Yom Kippur, except for the expenses of Shabbos, the expenses of Yom Tov, and the expenses of sending his children to Talmud Torah... if he adds, they add for him," and through this, it is added also to the person’s own private account.
Consequently, when one decides during these days to increase in the giving of *Tzedakah*—and especially those who have the merit of being "collectors of *Tzedakah*," which is greater than the giving itself—then the Ribono shel Olam adds even more to their own private account.
And since regarding the Aseres Yemei Teshuvah it is said, "Seek Hashem *when He is found*, call upon Him *when He is near*"—meaning that during these days the Ribono shel Olam is close to every single Yid—this is the appropriate time ("the season") for everyone to awaken themselves to increase in the giving of *Tzedakah*, and also to awaken their peers to do so, through which the Ribono shel Olam will add even more to their private account.
(And the Rebbe concluded:)
Blessings for success, and a good and sweet year.

## With the help of Hashem. Sicha of Tuesday, 18 Tishrei, the second day of Chol Hamoed Sukkos, 5713.

### – Simchas Beis Hashoeivah –
*Unedited*

**1.** A Sukkah must specifically be a temporary dwelling (*diras arai*), not a permanent one. This is why "a Sukkah that is higher than twenty amos is invalid," because "above twenty amos, a person does not make his dwelling a temporary one, but a permanent one." Yet, this temporary dwelling must be made *permanent*—"You shall dwell (*tashvu*) as you live (*tadur)*; from here they said: all seven days, a person makes his Sukkah permanent and his house temporary."

Thus, the mitzvah of Sukkah is composed of two opposites: on one hand, it must specifically be a temporary dwelling, and on the other hand, one must make it permanent—that is, to make the temporary into the permanent—and through this, the mitzvah is fulfilled.

In the Yerushalmi it is stated: "It is written, 'In Sukkos you shall dwell (*tashvu*),' and *tashvu* means nothing other than *tadur* (to live/dwell), as it is said, 'And you shall inherit it and dwell (*yashavtem*) in it'" (meaning the intention is not merely sitting, but dwelling).

It should be noted that even in the matter of "And you shall inherit it and dwell in it," the connection between *arai* (temporary) and *keva* (permanent) is emphasized:
Dwelling in the Land is in a manner of *keva*, as our Sages said that had they been worthy, the entry into the Land (immediately after the Exodus from Egypt, as stated in the Song of the Sea, "You will bring them and plant them in the mountain of Your inheritance, etc.") would have been in a manner of true and complete Redemption, after which there is no exile, and therefore it is spoken of as an inheritance—which "has no interruption."

However, the path to coming to Eretz Yisrael was through the journey in the desert, where the Bnei Yisrael were in a state and situation of *arai*, which is the concept of "I made the Bnei Yisrael dwell in Sukkos when I brought them out of the land of Egypt."

**2.** The concept of this in the *avodah* of man:
The totality of the world is analogous to a Sukkah, and it contains the connection and participation of *arai* and *keva*, because:
On one hand, the reality of the world is a matter of *arai*, for in and of itself it has no reality at all, since it was created from "nothing," not a thing. And even after it was created, its entire existence is only from the word of Hashem that brings it into being and sustains it every moment; in and of itself, it is "existing and ceasing," etc.

On the other hand, our Sages said, "Its legs are the world, which the Holy One, Blessed be He, longed to create," "The Holy One, Blessed be He, desired to have a *dwelling* for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms." It is explained in Chassidus that just as a person’s dwelling below is in such a way that the essence and being of the person dwells in the dwelling, so too is it with the dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms: that there is a revelation of the Essence below, which is the true reality—and this is a matter of *keva* in the most exalted manner.

That is to say: the matter of *arai* in the reality of the world is in the *lowest* manner, lower even than the *arai* of the Sukkah—for even a temporary thing has some existence at that time, whereas the reality of the world, even at the time it exists, is not a reality at all in and of itself. Conversely, the matter of *keva* that is made in the reality of the world through the *avodah* of Yisrael to make a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms, is in the *most exalted* manner—to be a dwelling for His Essence, the true reality, whose truth is not like the truth of all other beings.

**3.** And just as the mitzvah of Sukkah is in such a way that the Sukkah is a temporary dwelling and the person must make it permanent—so too in the totality of *avodah* in the world, "to work it and to guard it," to make a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms, there must be these two aforementioned matters:
When one considers the world a permanent thing, it is impossible to make it a dwelling for Him, just as a Sukkah higher than twenty amos (which a person makes a permanent dwelling) is invalid. Therefore, one must consider the world a *temporary* thing—that is, all material things, the affairs of the world, must be secondary and null to him, and his engagement with them must be as one does something in a temporary way.

On the other hand—the material things that are in a manner of *arai*, one must make them *permanent* through his *avodah* to Hashem within the material things themselves. That is, in addition to the *avodah* of Hashem in spiritual matters, Torah and prayer, there must also be *avodah* in the material things themselves, such as in eating and drinking, by making a blessing before and after, and likewise through the totality of *avodah* in the manner of "Let all your deeds be for the sake of Heaven" and "In all your ways know Him."

**4.** The explanation of this:
When the reality of the world and its material affairs occupy an important place for a person, then the materiality causes a disconnection from spirituality, as they cannot dwell together, as is brought in Chassidus from what is stated in *Chovos Halevavos* that just as fire and water cannot dwell in one vessel, so too the love of Hashem and the pleasures of this world cannot dwell in one heart.

Beyond this, even if the materiality did not cause a disconnection from spirituality, and they could dwell together—since the intention is that the Holy One, Blessed be He, desired to have a dwelling *in the lower realms*, one must draw down and reveal Elokus *in a thing that has no reality or value at all*. Consequently, when one draws down Elokus into a material thing that has a sense of place and importance, the supreme intention is not fulfilled.

Therefore, it must be felt by the person that material things are a temporary thing, and to the extreme of *arai* (in the lowest manner)—that they are not a reality at all; and this temporary thing must be made permanent, and to the extreme of *keva* (in the most exalted manner)—a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, by making the material things themselves a "vessel" for Elokus.

**5.** And this matter is emphasized in the manner of fulfilling the mitzvah of Sukkah:
Regarding the *sechach* of the Sukkah—"The verse says, 'When you gather from your threshing floor and your winepress,' the verse speaks of the waste of the threshing floor (stalks) and the winepress (branches and empty clusters)," and not the "threshing floor itself and the winepress itself" (the stalks with the grain and clusters with the grapes). Therefore, we cover with the *waste* of the threshing floor and winepress specifically. In this is hinted the lack of importance of the affairs of the world (*arai*)—that they are considered only as "*waste*."

And through this—the fulfillment of the mitzvah of Sukkah is done in the manner of "You shall dwell as you live," that "he eats and drinks and strolls in the Sukkah and reviews in the Sukkah." That is, not only the study of Torah, but even the affairs that are throughout the entire year, which are only matters of *permission*—eating, drinking, and strolling—their performance in the Sukkah is a *mitzvah*, and one makes a special blessing over them, etc.

The point of the matter—as stated—is that when it is felt by him that the affairs of the world are only *arai* (waste), then he has the ability to make the affairs of the world vessels for Elokus (mitzvah).

**6.** And in such a manner, the beginning of the *avodah* of the new year is made:
After Rosh Hashanah, the Ten Days of Repentance, and Yom Kippur, in which the rectification of the previous year is made—for on Yom Kippur the Holy One, Blessed be He, forgives and pardons all undesirable matters—on the first day of the festival of Sukkos—"the first for the counting of sins"—the *avodah* of the new year begins.

Therefore, at the beginning of the order of *avodah* of the new year, we emphasize immediately the goal of the *avodah*—in the mitzvah of Sukkah, a temporary dwelling that a person makes permanent—that it must be felt that the totality of the world is a matter of *arai*, the extreme of *arai*, and the *avodah* is to make it *keva*, the extreme of *keva*, a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed.

And even though the extreme of *arai* and the extreme of *keva* are opposites in the extreme—for this, there is the giving of strength from the *avodah* of Yom Kippur, whose conclusion and seal is the declaration "Hashem is the Elokim," the content of which declaration is—that the reality of nature (*Elokim* in *gematria* is *hateva*—nature) in its truth is above nature (*Havayah*), and with this strength one goes out to the *avodah* of making the Sukkah, to make from the extreme of *arai* the extreme of *keva*.

**7.** And this matter is learned from what is written, "In Sukkos you shall dwell, and *tashvu* means nothing other than *tadur*, as it is said, 'And you shall inherit it and dwell in it'":
The difference between inheritance ("And you shall inherit") and a gift or sale—in a sale, the buyer must pay from his assets for the thing sold, and even in a gift, where the recipient does not pay for it, "if he did not do a favor for himself, he would not have given him the gift"; whereas inheritance is not dependent at all on the status, situation, or action of the heir, but rather on his being a son, "a son is the leg of his father."

And this matter exists for every single one of Yisrael, the children of Avraham, Yitzchak, and Yaakov, as we say, "How beautiful is our inheritance"—that this matter is for all Bnei Yisrael *equally*, without regard to the differences in levels of their status and situation due to their *avodah* (for inheritance is not dependent on the status and situation of the heir). From this itself it is understood that this is a matter *most exalted* that cannot be reached through *avodah*, and therefore it comes specifically through inheritance, an inheritance that has no interruption, which is a matter above limitation, which cannot come through *avodah* that is connected with the limitation of the vessels.

And from the matter of the inheritance that is in Yisrael ("And you shall inherit it") comes "And you shall dwell in it"—in Eretz Yisrael: "Eretz" (Land)—"Why is it called Eretz? Because it wanted to do the will of its Creator," and "Yisrael"—named for "For you have struggled with Elokim and with men and have prevailed," that is, not to be impressed by men and even not by angels ("Elokim"), but to stand in dedication and surrender to Elokus, in the manner of "clinging and attached to You, the *yechidah* to unite You."

And from the connection to the Holy One, Blessed be He—there are no limitations, and consequently, one can make from *arai*, *keva*, from the extreme of below, a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed.

**8.** And through this, the drawing down is made for the entire year in such a way that even the material affairs (*arai*) are in permanence and expansion:
It is known that "the essence of the judgment on Rosh Hashanah is on the bodies... a person is judged on Rosh Hashanah only for the affairs of this world, etc.," and therefore, "even the mercies (the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy) that sweeten the judgments... are drawn down so far below until they are clothed in materiality."

And this matter is emphasized also on the festival of Sukkos—in which they are judged on the water, the matter of rains, which include all material affairs. And from the matter of the permanence in the mitzvah of Sukkah—all material affairs are drawn down in a manner of permanence,

And in simple terms—a good and sweet year in action and in the open in everything needed for each one and his household, with children, life, and ample sustenance, and in the manner of "And you shall rejoice in your festival," from within joy.

***

**9.** Regarding the creation of man—that he was created to complete the intention of making a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms, by "placing him in the Garden of Eden to work it and to guard it"—it is stated in the Midrash that "Adam and Chava were created as twenty-year-olds."

And since this is the order from the side of creation, that "the world was created in its fullness," and only after the sin of the Tree of Knowledge, when the world is not in its rectified state, is there also the advancement of years until they become "as twenty-year-olds," it is understood that even now, the essence and goal of the *avodah* is when one becomes twenty years old, for then the strength of man is filled.

In the language of the Mishnah—"Twenty years for the pursuit":
Even though the simple meaning of "pursuit" is "after his sustenance, after he has learned Scripture, Mishnah, and Gemara and married a wife and fathered children, he must return and seek sustenance,"

—And as the Alter Rebbe explains in the Laws of Torah Study, that "in their days, when they would learn with the youths from ten years old and up, five years Mishnah and five years Talmud," until the age of twenty, because, "even after the marriage (whose 'beginning time is from eighteen years old,' 'eighteen years for the chuppah'), he can learn two or three years [two years—the nineteenth year and the twentieth year, or three years—according to the opinion that 'eighteen years for the chuppah' means *at the beginning* of the eighteenth year] without such great distraction before he fathers many children"—

Since material sustenance is dependent on spiritual sustenance, as it is written, "If you walk in My statutes, etc., and I will give your rains in their time, etc.," that is, that the blessing of Hashem in material affairs is *on the condition* of fulfilling the Torah and mitzvos—it is understood that together with the pursuit after material sustenance, one must also pursue spiritual sustenance.

And it should be added to this, that after the order of "Five years for Scripture, ten years for Mishnah, etc., fifteen years for Gemara" (until the age of twenty), where the main engagement is *in the study of Torah*—then begins the order of *avodah* of "Twenty years for the pursuit" regarding the matter of *action* (fulfillment of the mitzvos, including the refinement of the affairs of the world), for "great is study that brings to action," and as is emphasized also regarding the mitzvah of *Hakhel*: "Read the Torah, etc., assemble the people, the men and the women and the children, etc., so that they may hear and so that they may learn, etc., and observe to do all the words of this Torah, etc., to fear Hashem your Elokim, etc."

From this it is understood regarding the students of the yeshivos (many of whom are present here now) who are around the age of twenty—the main time of *avodah*—that for them there must be the matter of "pursuit" ("*yogen zich*") regarding the study of Torah (for as long as they are within the walls of the yeshiva they have no worries of sustenance at all), to flourish ("*shteigen*") in the study of Torah with greater strength and greater power.

**10.** And this matter is also emphasized on the festival of Sukkos—in which the order of *avodah* of the entire year begins:
In addition to the mitzvah of Sukkah—there is also the mitzvah of taking the Four Species, as it is written, "And you shall take for yourselves on the first day, etc.," and the order of taking the Four Species—that the blessing is "on the taking of the *lulav*" specifically, because "in its species it is higher than all of them."

And the concept of this—as explained in the Midrash that the Four Species hint to four types among Bnei Yisrael, starting from those who have Torah and good deeds, taste and fragrance, to those who have neither taste nor fragrance, etc. (even though they have the merit that "they grow in brotherhood"), "The Holy One, Blessed be He, said: let them all be bound in one bundle," and as it is written, "And they all made one bundle to do Your will with a whole heart."

[It should be noted that the matter of "one bundle" is only "to do Your will with a whole heart," as we find what was said to Chizkiyahu regarding Shevna, "Do not call a conspiracy everything that this people calls a conspiracy," because "it is a conspiracy of the wicked, and a conspiracy of the wicked is not counted." Conversely, when the connection and the bundle is "to do Your will with a whole heart," then they bind and include even those who are in the category of *aravah* (willow) who have neither taste nor fragrance, and to such an extent, that without them the mitzvah is not fulfilled!]

Now, the *lulav* ("palm branches") which "has taste but no fragrance," points to those "who have Torah but no good deeds":
"Fragrance" points to mitzvos whose fulfillment is not from the side of taste, but because they are His will, may He be blessed, whereas "taste" points to Torah, according to the fact that the study of Torah must be with understanding and comprehension specifically, and to such an extent that "in the Oral Torah, if one does not understand the explanation, it is not considered study at all."

Therefore, the "*lulav*" which has taste and no fragrance, hints to Torah scholars, and first and foremost to the yeshiva students, who are found throughout the day within the four cubits of prayer and Torah, and their Torah is their profession, and they have no business with the materiality of the world, plowing and sowing, etc., and consequently, they have no possibility to fulfill the mitzvos dependent on the Land, and the like.

And this is why the blessing is on the taking of the *lulav* (which hints to those of Torah) specifically—similar to what is said in the Midrashim of our Sages "Who are the guardians of the city?... These are the scribes (teachers of Scripture) and the teachers of Mishnah... and so it says, 'If Hashem does not build the house, etc.'" (the end of the verse is "If Hashem does not guard the city," and this is in the study of Torah), and that is, that through them specifically the blessing is drawn down—"Blessed (*Baruch*—from the language of drawing down) are You, Hashem our Elokim, King of the world"—that the *Havayah* that is above nature be drawn down into nature (*Elokim*), through which there will be the building of the world to make it a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed.

**11.** And this matter is emphasized also regarding the *na'anu'im* (shaking)—that the measure of the *lulav* must be larger "in order to shake with it":
By way of introduction—that the shaking is a primary matter in the taking of the *lulav* (as is emphasized also in the language of the Mishnah "A minor who knows how to shake is obligated in *lulav*"), and it is included in the *shirai* (preparatory) mitzvah, but it is not indispensable, as is proven from that which "the first day of the festival that falls on Shabbos and one forgot and took the *lulav* out to the public domain, he is exempt, because he took it out with permission," and we say in the Gemara, "They only taught this where he did not go out with it, but if he went out with it, he is obligated," and it asks, "But from the moment he lifted it, he fulfilled it," and it answers, "When he turned it," and it does not answer that he did not shake. And we even find in the Yerushalmi that from this we learn also regarding the mitzvah of circumcision, that "even regarding the skin that is not indispensable in circumcision, (even) if he separated, he returns."

And the matter of the shaking is—"He moves it back and forth to stop evil winds, he raises and lowers to stop evil dews."

Now, even though the shaking is with all four species, nevertheless, the essence in this is the *lulav*. In this is hinted that the drawing down into the world is through the study of Torah (the matter of the *lulav*), that is, that it is in the power of those of Torah to stop undesirable matters (evil winds and evil dews) and to draw down all good even in materiality, children, life, and ample sustenance.

And all this is done through the study of Torah, and in a manner of *toil* in Torah specifically, as Rashi explains: "If you walk in My statutes," "That you should be *toiling* in Torah," and that is, that it is not enough (and it is not a "trick") to study in the order of the yeshiva, but even when he has fulfilled his obligation according to the clock, and he is already tired, and if he forces himself to add and study then the study will be without "taste" (*geshmak*)—he must add and *toil* in the study of Torah, toil specifically, without "taste," and through this they effect the drawing down of blessings from above, "And I will give your rains in their time," in the entire world.

**12.** It is stated in the Gemara "It is forbidden for a person to fill his mouth with laughter in this world, as it is said, 'Then our mouth will be filled with laughter.'" But, this matter is regarding a matter of joy and song in general, whereas joy and song that brings benefit in *avodah*—not only is it permitted, but furthermore, there is an obligation in the matter.

More specifically:
The matter of joy and song effects that the *avodah* will be easier, as the saying of the Rebbe Rayatz that when a soldier goes to war, he goes with a "march" (song of marching), and specifically through this he is victorious in the war. That is: not only does the "march" not distract from the war, but on the contrary, it causes the victory of the war. And likewise in *avodah*—not only does the matter of joy not distract from the *avodah*, but on the contrary, through this the *avodah* is done with sweetness ("*geshmak*"), and then it is in its perfection, fullness, and beauty.

And another matter in this:
Since the matter of joy and song is a permitted thing, if one does not utilize it for matters of holiness, then in addition to the lack of benefit in holiness, the matter is liable to break out in other matters, and not only in matters that are not holiness, but also in matters that are not even matters of permission.

That is: a thing permitted according to Torah—is needed by a person also from the side of nature (unlike a thing forbidden according to Torah, which also from the side of nature is not needed by the person), and ultimately the matter is expressed and breaks out in some way. And consequently, if one does not utilize it for holiness, the matter is liable to break out in undesirable matters.

And especially for those who are in the category of "Twenty years for the pursuit"—for, from the side of the increase of strength and energy ("*energie*") in these years, the nature of joy and song is liable to break out in other matters, and therefore, the need to utilize the nature of joy and song for matters of holiness is more emphasized, to prevent the breaking out in other matters. And all the more so since joy and song adds benefit in the *avodah* whose essence and goal is in the time of "Twenty years for the pursuit," as stated above.

And if these things are said for the entire year, all the more so on the festival of Sukkos, when one fulfills the mitzvah "And you shall take for yourselves on the first day the fruit of a beautiful tree, palm branches, etc.," and it is stated in the Midrash "By the fact that Yisrael go out from before the Holy One, Blessed be He, and their *lulavim* and *esrogim* in their hands, we know *that Yisrael are the victors*," then certainly one must go with a "march" of victory.

**14.** It was spoken above that in the mitzvah of Sukkah is hinted the intention of the totality of creation—the Holy One, Blessed be He, desired to have a dwelling for Him, may He be blessed, in the lower realms—that the world is a matter of *arai*, and one must make it a permanent dwelling for the Holy One, Blessed be He, through the fulfillment of the mitzvos.

## Sicha of Wednesday, 19 Tishrei, 3rd Day of Chol Hamoed Sukkos, 5713

**A.** In addition to the matter of Simchas Beis Hashoevah during the festival of Sukkos every year, this year’s Sukkos—the conclusion of the Shemitah year—is augmented by the matter of "Hakhel," as it is written: "At the end of seven years, at the appointed time of the year of Shemitah, on the festival of Sukkos... assemble the people, etc."

During the festival of Sukkos itself, the matter of "Hakhel" took place (not on the Yom Tov itself, but) on the "conclusion of the first day of the festival," which is the beginning of the days of Chol Hamoed—whether because they were unable to perform it on the Yom Tov itself, or because the obligation of Hakhel is inherently "during the festival" (specifically during Chol Hamoed).

Regarding this itself, it is not clear whether "Hakhel" had to be during the day, or whether it took place at night at the conclusion of the first day of the festival. Since this did not involve a matter of *avodah* (Temple service), but only the reading of sections of the Torah [especially since the reading did not take place in the location where the Altar stood, but in the Women’s Courtyard, and the reading was not performed by a Kohen, but by the King (and according to many opinions, if there is no King (as before the time of Shaul, who was the "first king"), the leader of the generation reads)], the principle that all matters of *avodah* in the Temple must be performed specifically during the day does not apply to it.

According to many opinions, even if for some reason they did not fulfill the mitzvah of Hakhel on the first day of Chol Hamoed, they could fulfill it on the subsequent days of Chol Hamoed.

**B.** In the mitzvah of Hakhel, there is a matter in which this mitzvah differs from all other mitzvos: All mitzvos are obligatory only for those who have understanding—a male thirteen years of age and a female twelve years of age.

Even the mitzvah of *chinuch* (education) associated with minors—although the obligation of *chinuch* rests upon the father (since the recipient of the education is a minor who is not obligated in mitzvos)—does not apply to a minor who has no understanding at all, but only to a minor who has reached the age of *chinuch*, five or six years old, who, even if he is not a person of full understanding, possesses enough understanding to receive the education provided to him.

The exception is the mitzvah of Hakhel, regarding which it is stated: "Assemble the people, the men, the women, *and the little ones*," for it is a mitzvah to bring the "little ones" to Hakhel as well. The reason for this is not because of the matter of *chinuch*—in which case there would be the limitation that they must be minors who have reached the age of *chinuch*—but as our Sages said: "The men come to learn, the women come to hear, why do the little ones come? *To provide reward for those who bring them*." Consequently, there is no limitation that they must be fit for *chinuch*, for the "reward *for those who bring them*" does not concern the *minor’s* education.

Therefore, the conclusion of many *Acharonim* is that regarding the "little ones" of "Hakhel," there is no age limitation; if it is a viable child, whose months have been completed, and who has passed the status of a *nefel* (after thirty days), he is included in the command "Assemble... the little ones."

Furthermore, since the bringing of the little ones is to provide reward for those who bring them (and not for the sake of *chinuch*), according to many opinions, this also includes a minor for whom an adult of similar status would be exempt from Hakhel (such as a deaf person, a blind person, etc.), even though regarding an adult, they said that anyone exempt from the pilgrimage (whether because he lacks land, or because he is an elder who cannot walk on his feet, or is outside the Land) is exempt from Hakhel—for since the obligation is upon the *bringer* (who must bring the *minor*), there are no restrictions regarding the minor himself, other than that he must be a viable child.

The manner of the obligation to bring the little ones is that they must be brought to the Beis Hamikdash to be present at the time of the reading of the Torah sections. Since the little ones come only "to provide reward for those who bring them," it would seemingly suffice to bring them to the Temple Mount, and it would not be necessary to bring them specifically to the place where the Torah sections are read. Nevertheless, the obligation of Hakhel is such that even the little ones must be present at the time of the reading of the Torah sections—for the verse includes everyone together: "The men, the women, and the little ones," and concludes, "in order that they may hear and in order that they may learn"—even though it speaks also of a small infant *who does not hear and does not understand*! From this it is proven that the matter of "Hakhel" touches upon a place (level) that is above limitations, which is why it is relevant even to a minor who has not reached the age of *chinuch*.

The general content of Hakhel is—in the words of the verse—"to fear Hashem your G-d, etc.," to awaken *yiras shamayim* (fear of Heaven), through the fact that the King or the leader of the generation reads in the ears of all the people, "the men, the women, and the little ones," "sections that urge them in mitzvos and strengthen their hands in the religion of truth": "From the beginning of *Eileh Devarim* until *Shema*, and *Shema* and *Vehayah Im Shamoa*, *Aser Te’aser*, etc."

Based on this, we can understand an additional precision in the mitzvah of Hakhel—that although according to the literal meaning of the verse, the obligation of the mitzvah should rest upon the one who has the power to assemble, upon the King or the *Beis Din*, who are obligated to assemble the men, women, and little ones [for in the preceding verses it is said, "And Moshe called to Yehoshua, etc.," "And Moshe wrote this Torah and gave it to the Kohanim, the sons of Levi, etc., and to all the elders of Yisrael," "And Moshe commanded them, saying, etc., Assemble the people, etc."], nevertheless, we find commentators who explain the details of the mitzvah, that this is a command resting upon each and every one of the men and women to come to the gathering of Hakhel.

**C.** We should add and explain the reason why the mitzvah of Hakhel is at the conclusion of the Shemitah year:

Regarding the Shemitah year, it is said, "The land shall rest a Sabbath to Hashem," meaning that throughout the entire year, the Bnei Yisrael did not engage in any of the labors of plowing, sowing, and reaping, etc., which connect a person with the physicality of the earth and materiality. Consequently, they were *disconnected* from physical and material matters to a degree far greater than the disconnection that exists in other years.

During the six years in which they engage in the labor of the land, the *person’s ownership* is felt: he is the one who plows, he is the one who sows, and he is the one who reaps, and consequently, he is the master of the produce he gathers from the field and vineyard—"And you shall gather your grain, and your wine, and your oil"—an ownership that also concerns the obligation of *terumos* and *maaseros*, since the grain and fruit belong to him. Whereas in the Shemitah year, there is a royal decree—"the King’s expropriation"—that the grain and fruit are ownerless to all, and there is nothing here but *the ownership of the Ribono shel Olam*.

Moreover: The growth of the grain during the six years in which they engage in human labor is—seemingly—the *way of the world*, which has continued for more than five thousand years, both among Bnei Yisrael, and—to distinguish—among the nations of the world. Whereas in the Shemitah year, in which they rest from the labor of the land according to the command of the Ribono shel Olam, the *blessing of the Ribono shel Olam* is seen openly in the growth of the grain and fruit, as it is written: "And if you say, 'What shall we eat in the seventh year? Behold, we will not sow, nor gather our produce,' then I will command My blessing for you in the sixth year, and it will produce the crop for the three years"—above nature.

Thus, in the Shemitah year, Bnei Yisrael had to awaken—and certainly did awaken—with greater intensity and strength to everything connected to the feeling of the Ribono shel Olam’s ownership over the entire land and His blessing in all physical matters.

Therefore, when they emerge from the Shemitah year to the following year, in which they will begin again to engage in all the matters of "seed and harvest, etc."—because that is the will of the Ribono shel Olam—there is a need for a preface and clarification, that nothing changes in the Ribono shel Olam’s ownership of the world, and it is in full force just as it was in the Shemitah year.

This is the content of the reading of the Torah sections at Hakhel at the conclusion of the Shemitah year—that the beginning of the year in which they engage in physical labors is: "Shema Yisrael Hashem Elokeinu Hashem Echad," "And it will be if you listen, you will listen, etc.," the content of which sections is the acceptance of the "yoke of the Kingdom of Heaven" and the "yoke of mitzvos,"

And in continuation of this—"And you shall gather your grain, and your wine, and your oil," meaning, that even the gathering of the grain and fruit (the totality of all physical matters) depends on "And it will be if you listen, you will listen, etc.,"

And up to "Aser Te’aser"—that a Jew performs within himself a matter of *mesiras nefesh* (self-sacrifice), to take something he labored for, for the sake of "the life of his soul," and give it to *tzedakah*, without any ulterior motive.

**D.** And at the conclusion of the Shemitah year itself—the time of Hakhel is specifically on the festival of Sukkos:

It is stated in the Midrash that the festival of Sukkos is called the "first day," because it is the "first for the counting of sins," for on Yom Kippur, the Ribono shel Olam forgives all sins, "and from Yom Kippur until the festival, all of Yisrael are engaged in mitzvos, this one is engaged in his Sukkah and this one with his Lulav," and the first possibility for a matter of sin, Heaven forbid, is—on the festival of Sukkos, the "first for the counting of sins," because in it they are more connected to the matters of this world.

Therefore, when they emerge from Yom Tov to Chol Hamoed, in which it is permitted to engage in labor, including the labor of the land, in a case of loss—this is the appropriate time to assemble the people and read in their ears the Torah sections that awaken them to the unity of Hashem, etc., as a preface to the order of *avodah* of the year following the Shemitah year, in which they engage in the labor of the land.

**E.** Based on this, the great importance and merit of the mitzvah of Hakhel is understood—that it is a "strong pillar and great honor in the religion."

This is also the reason for the reading of the Torah sections at Hakhel specifically by the King—for the matter of the King is "that he may lead them out and that he may lead them in," meaning, to lead Bnei Yisrael out and guide them in the war of this world against the not-good matters, and to return them in peace from this war, and therefore, he—and similarly the leader of the generation—must awaken all of Klal Yisrael, from the greatest to the smallest, "to fear Hashem... all the days."

And in this matter are included not only "the men and the women," but also "the little ones," and even little ones who have not reached the age of *chinuch*:

Although they are not yet fit to receive a matter of *chinuch*, nevertheless, they must also be brought to the gathering of many of Bnei Yisrael who are awakening to *yiras shamayim*,

For, in such a gathering, the *Shechinah* rests with greater intensity and strength, as our Sages said, "When ten are sitting, the *Shechinah* rests," and all the more so when the ten (all those gathering together) are engaged in matters of Torah and *yiras shamayim*—and as it is stated regarding this, the parable of a person who sees a *tzaddik* praying and pouring out his soul before Hashem, that the seeing itself, even if the viewer does not do anything, acts upon the person who sees, at the very least in a way of *makif* (encompassing)—

So, the effect of the gathering of "Hakhel" is a matter that is not connected with intellect and understanding, and therefore the effect is also upon the little ones who have not reached the age of *chinuch*—from the side of the *point of Yiddishkeit* that is in its completeness in each and every one of Yisrael.

**F.** And this matter constitutes a lesson and a moral for us:

As a preface—that although the Shemitah of lands was not practiced outside the Land even at the time the Beis Hamikdash stood, and all the more so at this time, nevertheless, even at this time and even outside the Land, the Shemitah of debts remains, and furthermore, even regarding the Shemitah of lands which is not practiced now, there is the spiritual matter in this mitzvah, and the moral lesson that we learn from it, that this matter is in its completeness in every place and at every time, and with greater intensity—when the special time arrives in which they would fulfill this mitzvah.

Therefore, at the conclusion of every Shemitah, at every time and in every place where Bnei Yisrael are, they demand of a Jew—and since they demand of him, it is a sign that he has the strength to accomplish this—that although tomorrow he goes out to the street to engage in physical and material matters, nevertheless, he should effect within himself, and also in the street and in physical matters, that when he engages with them and does them as they ought to be done, he will be able to remember at the time of the act that "Shema Yisrael Hashem Elokeinu Hashem Echad," and he will know that "And you shall gather your grain, and your wine, and your oil" depends on "And it will be if you listen, you will listen, etc."

And this is not a matter connected specifically with Eretz Yisrael, or with the existence of the Beis Hamikdash—for the sections of *Shema* and *Vehayah Im Shamoa* are recited in every place and at every time twice every day, except that at the time of "Hakhel," when there is a need for a special awakening, they add and read them in a most special way—by the King or the leader of the generation, and in the gathering of all Yisrael, the men, the women, and the little ones, as above.

And this is also the instruction regarding the effect upon others—"Assemble the people, the men, the women, and the little ones"—without considering the status and situation of the other, for the point of Yiddishkeit is in its completeness in each and every one of Yisrael, even the least of the least, and therefore, first and foremost, one must draw him into a gathering of Jews where they speak about *yiras shamayim*, and this will be a good beginning so that ultimately the matter will take effect in him until he comes to the status and situation of "to fear Hashem."

And in this itself, the effect can be in several ways, and the best way—fitting for the will of the Ribono shel Olam who is the essence of good and the nature of the good is to do good—is when it is done in a line of joy.

And this is the extra merit in a gathering of *yiras shamayim* that is done on the festival of Sukkos (which is then the time of the mitzvah of Hakhel), that in addition to it being one of the three festivals whose matter is "appointed times for joy," it has "extra joy"—Simchas Beis Hashoevah, regarding which they said, "Whoever has not seen the Simchas Beis Hashoevah has not seen joy in his days."

And the greatness of the joy on the festival of Sukkos constitutes an addition of strength for each and every one so that the engagement in the above will be easier and with more pleasantness, and consequently, the obligation resting upon each and every one to engage in this matter is greater, at the very least in the way of "open for Me an opening the size of a needle's eye," and as stated, without considering the status and situation of the other, even if it seems to him that so-and-so is in a status and situation of a minor who has not reached the age of *chinuch*, and even if they try to educate him, it will not help—for "Why should you concern yourself with the hidden things of the Merciful One?"; you must do everything that depends on you to bring him to a gathering of *yiras shamayim*, and all the rest the Ribono shel Olam will already do, and certainly ultimately the Ribono shel Olam will effect that "they shall all become one association to do Your will with a whole heart."

**G.** Regarding the matter of the King—who is the one who reads the Torah sections at Hakhel—about whom it is said, "You shall surely set a king over you"—the question is known: When Bnei Yisrael asked Shmuel the Prophet, "Give us a king," he was very displeased with this, and the Ribono shel Olam also said to him, "For they have rejected Me, etc." And seemingly, this is a mitzvah that the Ribono shel Olam commanded Yisrael? Especially according to the statement of our Sages, "Three mitzvos were Yisrael commanded upon their entry into the Land, to appoint for themselves a king, etc."—from which it is understood that this is a most exalted mitzvah, and even more than the mitzvos dependent on the Land.

And also, if their demand for a king at that time was an undesirable thing—why did the Ribono shel Olam command Shmuel the Prophet *then* to concede to Bnei Yisrael and appoint a king for them?

**H.** In Chassidus it is explained regarding this, that the intent in the appointment of a king is in two ways (one above the other):

1) A simple reason, for (in the words of the Mishnah) "Were it not for the fear (of the kingdom), one man would swallow his fellow alive"—that the King will lead and effect that the conduct of the people of the state will be as is proper. Even when the intellect understands that one must conduct oneself as is proper, it is not enough, since "the eye sees and the heart covets, etc."—and therefore there is a need for the fear of the King, which effects that they conduct themselves in the straight path.

2) When the situation is that the mind is in control of the heart constantly—there is no necessity for the appointment of a king for the matter mentioned; however, there are such matters that the people do not have the knowledge to understand how they should conduct themselves in them, but only the King—from the side of his merit that "from his shoulders and upward he was taller than all the people"—understands them, and gives orders how to conduct oneself, and the people of the state do so because of the command of the King.

And this is the matter of the appointment of a king (in its inwardness and its root) among the people of Yisrael—that their Master and King is the Ribono shel Olam, and the king of flesh and blood who is appointed, is the intermediary who reveals His Kingdom to Bnei Yisrael:

Bnei Yisrael from their own side—believe, understand, and feel that their vitality is drawn and comes from (the Kingdom of) the Ribono shel Olam, and this matter must awaken in them the *bitul* (nullification) to the Ribono shel Olam. But when they are in such a situation that this *bitul* is lacking in them—they are in need of a king of flesh and blood, who through the fear of his kingdom, etc., effects in them fear and *bitul* to the King of Kings, the Ribono shel Olam.

[And the matter in this:
The King—together with the greatness and strength of his existence, the *bitul* to the Ribono shel Olam is felt in him most intensely "like a servant before his master," to such an extent that he is not an existence for himself at all, as is emphasized in the words of the Gemara regarding the details of the distinctions in the manner of bowing in prayer, that "the King, once he bows, does not stand up again." And this matter is expressed also throughout the entire day—in the Torah scroll that "goes out and comes in with him" that "he makes it like an amulet and hangs it on his arm."

And this is the content of the command "You shall surely set a king over you," "that his fear shall be upon you"—that the fear of the King, meaning, his fear and *bitul* to the Ribono shel Olam, will be drawn to all Bnei Yisrael, that in them too there will be the matter of fear and *bitul* to the Ribono shel Olam.]

But when Bnei Yisrael are as they ought to be, and there is in them the matter of *the above* in *bitul* to *Elokus* from their own side—then the innovation of (the appointment of) the King is in a more exalted way: The levels in *Elokus* that Bnei Yisrael from their own side cannot reach, being *above* their grasp—the King (who from the side of his merit has a grasp also in them) draws and influences them to the people of Yisrael, and this matter effects in them that there will be in them also a more exalted level in fear and *bitul*—*bitul* that is above their understanding and grasp.

[**I.** And in more detail:
Regarding the matter of fear—there are (in general) two levels: (a) *yirah tata’ah* (lower fear), regarding which they said, "If there is no fear, there is no wisdom," (b) *yirah ila’ah* (higher fear), regarding which they said, "If there is no wisdom, there is no fear," meaning, that first there must be the matter of wisdom—the study of Torah, and up to the most exalted level in wisdom, and afterwards and through this they can come to *yirah ila’ah*.

And this matter is emphasized in the first King—Moshe Rabbeinu, about whom it is said, "And there was a king in Yeshurun," whose matter is—Torah, "Moshe received Torah from Sinai and transmitted it, etc." to all Bnei Yisrael, who, being a general soul that includes all the souls of Yisrael, he draws and influences to them the matters of Torah, including also the most exalted matters in Torah, which from their own side are above the grasp of Bnei Yisrael, and therefore there is a need for the action of Moshe Rabbeinu—from the side of the matter of kingship in him—to draw them to Bnei Yisrael, and through this is effected in them also the matter of fear that is above wisdom, *yirah ila’ah*.

And to add and note, that even when it speaks about the action of the King—Moshe Rabbeinu—in the drawing of *yirah ila’ah*, after the preface of wisdom, etc., the influence of the physicality of Bnei Yisrael is also drawn through him.

And the matter in this—that the Torah (the matter of Moshe Rabbeinu) is the "conduit" through which all influences are drawn in the world, as it is written, "If you walk in My statutes"—that you shall be toilers in Torah—"then I will give your rains in their time, etc."

And as we find in the generation of Moshe that all matters of abundance for Yisrael, including also the influence of meat, had to be through Moshe Rabbeinu specifically:

When Bnei Yisrael asked for meat, and Moshe Rabbeinu said, "From where shall I have meat," and as is explained in Chassidus that from the side of the greatness of the level of Moshe Rabbeinu he could not constrict himself in the influence of meat—the Ribono shel Olam said to him, "Gather to Me seventy men of the elders of Yisrael, etc., and I will take of the spirit that is upon you and put it upon them, etc.," meaning, that although the influence was through the seventy elders, nevertheless, it had to be from the spirit of Moshe specifically.

And seemingly it is not understood: since Moshe Rabbeinu stands in a status and situation that he cannot constrict himself in the influence of meat, and all the more so that "they asked for meat not properly"—why did the influence of the meat have to be from the spirit of Moshe specifically, and could it not have been only through the elders? But the matter is—because all influences are through the conduit of Torah, which is drawn by Moshe Rabbeinu, who is the *Nasi* and the King, "that he may lead them out and that he may lead them in" also regarding their physical matters.]

**J.** Based on this, the above questions will be answered:

Shmuel the Prophet wanted the situation of Bnei Yisrael to be in a way that they would stand in *bitul* to *Elokus* from their own side, without the need for a king, and the King would effect in them the most exalted level of *bitul* and fear (in general—*yirah ila’ah*);

But Bnei Yisrael asked, "a king to judge us like all the nations"—a king who would effect in them the negation of the matter of "one man swallowing his fellow alive"—which indicates that the level of *yiras shamayim* that should be in Bnei Yisrael from their own side was lacking in them. And therefore the Ribono shel Olam said, "For they have rejected Me, etc."—*yiras shamayim* is lacking in them.

And it is understood that nevertheless the Ribono shel Olam "conceded to them" and commanded to appoint a king—for, when in a Jew *yiras shamayim* is lacking, for whatever reason, although he must come to fear and *bitul* by himself, without the influence of the King, nevertheless, it is impossible to wait, and until then a Jew would be, Heaven forbid, without *yiras shamayim*; he must immediately appoint over himself a king who will effect in him *the above* matters, and afterwards in the course of time he will come to the level of *bitul* and fear from his own side, and up to also the levels of the second type—through a king, as above.

**K.** From every matter one must derive an instruction in the *avodah* of Hashem:

Although in the time of exile there is no king—our Sages said, "Who are the kings? The Rabbis." Just as there is a mitzvah of the appointment of a king, similarly a Jew has a command of the kings who are the Rabbis—"Make for yourself a Rav."

And in this there is an instruction from the above matter:

There are those who think that regarding low matters they themselves can understand and decide, and consequently they do not need to ask about them to a "Rav."

The Mishnah says, "Make for yourself a Rav," *every* Jew must have a Rav—whereas they think that the intent in this is regarding exalted matters, but when it speaks about simple matters, he believes in himself, and does not need the influence of a Rav. He has the ability to effect this alone.

And although time passes and he still stands in that same low situation—nevertheless he thinks that it is not worth going for this to a "Rav." He will wait "until a spirit is poured upon him from on high," until he awakens in fear as is proper, and will fix everything that needs to be fixed—by his own power.

And regarding this is the instruction of the appointment of a king in spirituality now: Even though ("upon *you*"—among Bnei Yisrael) the matter and action of a king is relevant mainly regarding exalted levels, when there is a situation of "they have rejected Me," Heaven forbid, or a fear of such a situation—one must immediately use the fear of the King for this matter.

**L.** Likewise there are those who say that they do not find for themselves a "Rav."

They must know that this is from the counsel and temptation of the *Yetzer*. For certainly Yisrael is not widowed, and such an existence is not relevant that among Bnei Yisrael there would not be one who has love of Hashem and fear of Hashem more than him, and consequently he can be for him a "Rav." But for this there must be "make" (also from the language of "they compel for *tzedakah*," forcing one's own *yeshus*) and *toil*—he must toil and search, until he finds a "Rav." For, upon himself he is forbidden to rely, and it is a necessity that he have a "Rav." And "you toiled *and found*"—if he will toil and search in truth, he will find a "Rav."

And the "Rav" will teach and review with him the sections that the King would read at Hakhel—*Shema* and *Vehayah Im Shamoa*, etc., the content of which is the acceptance of the yoke of the Kingdom of Heaven, etc. (as above, section 3), and after he awakens in him the lower level of *bitul* and acceptance of the yoke, he will draw upon him also the exalted level—*yirah ila’ah*.

And to the above there is a special relevance to the footsteps of Moshiach, on the eve of the coming of Moshiach Tzidkeinu—whose matter is: Rav (will teach Torah to all the people, even to the Patriarchs and Moshe Rabbeinu) and King—Malka Meshicha.

**M.** In the mitzvah of Hakhel there are two parts: the assembling of the people, and the reading of the Torah. And in the reading of the Torah itself—the reading of the Torah by the King, and the hearing of the Torah sections by the people (as the enumerators of the mitzvos explain).

That is to say: There is an obligation upon each and every one of Yisrael to hear the reading of the Torah sections, and there is an obligation upon the King or the leader of the generation to read the Torah sections, and on the contrary, the obligation of reading is *in greater force*, for the obligation of hearing is a private matter that is relevant to each individual separately, whereas the obligation of reading is a general matter that concerns Klal Yisrael.

**N.** And from this it is understood that so it is also regarding the content of the matter of "Hakhel" as it is relevant at this time—and as a preface:

The principle "Torah is eternal"—applies also to the *mitzvos* of the Torah, for they are a part of the Torah, and not only that, but they are the essence of the Torah, as our Sages said, "Great is study that leads to action."

Therefore, although in the time of exile it is impossible to fulfill most of the positive mitzvos that are dependent on the time that the Beis Hamikdash stands and Yisrael are on their land, nevertheless, even then the mitzvos are eternal just as the Torah is eternal, for the principle "Great is study that leads to action" is also at a time when it is impossible to fulfill most of the aforementioned mitzvos—for, in addition to the fulfillment of the mitzvos in actual deed in this physical world, which is the essence, and in the well-known language "action above," there is also the spiritual matter of these mitzvos in the *avodah* of man, and when it is impossible to fulfill the mitzvos literally, there must be at least their spiritual matter in the *avodah* of man.

And similarly regarding the content of the mitzvah of Hakhel, that its fulfillment by the King or (when there is no king) by the leader of the generation (as above, section 1)—this is relevant to "Who are the kings? The Rabbis," each and every one in his city and in his environment, upon whom rests the responsibility and the obligation, which is also a merit, to effect upon the men, women, and little ones in their environment that there will be an addition in them in *yiras shamayim*.

And just as the obligation of reading by the King is in greater force than the obligation of hearing by each of the men, women, and little ones (as above)—so too the obligation of the "kings who are the Rabbis" to effect in their environment is *in greater force* than the obligation resting upon the men, women, and little ones who must receive influence from them.

**O.** And this is not for the sake of homiletics, but regarding action in practice:

The students of the Yeshivos—"from twenty years old and upward, all who go out to the army in Yisrael"—have merited and are in the corner of light, in the four cubits of Torah, and have no business with the physicality of the world, so that they can devote themselves entirely to the study of Torah, and connect through it with the King of Kings, the Ribono shel Olam (similar to what is said regarding mitzvos, "Honor the mitzvos, for they are messengers"), which is the matter of "Who are the kings? The Rabbis."

Therefore, each one of them must know that there rests upon him the responsibility, obligation, and merit toward those who are in his environment, who, from the side of their excessive engagement in the matters of the world, are liable to forget, Heaven forbid, that "the earth is Hashem's"—to gather them from time to time and show them how Jews declare "Shema Yisrael," and awaken them to *yiras shamayim*, acceptance of the yoke of the Kingdom of Heaven and acceptance of the yoke of mitzvos, and the fulfillment of practical mitzvos in actual deed.

One must explain to them, for example, that it is true that they must engage in the matters of the world, as it is said, "And you shall gather your grain, etc.," and not only that, but the Torah says that so-and-so is the master of the house, etc., but nevertheless, one should not exaggerate in this beyond the measure, and one must know that the livelihood depends on the blessing of the Ribono shel Olam, and therefore one must conduct oneself according to the instruction of the Ribono shel Olam in His Torah, and similarly in simple matters.

And it is understood that one should not wait until they honor him and invite him to say words of moral instruction, etc., but on the contrary, *upon him* rests the responsibility to gather and assemble Jews and awaken them in matters of *yiras shamayim*, for he is a *general person* who has the power to influence and effect in his environment, similar to the action of the brain and heart upon all the limbs of the body.

**P.** And furthermore—that the action in the environment is relevant and touches also upon his *avodah* within himself, and therefore, there is no place for the claim of *bitul Torah* (neglect of Torah), etc., in addition to the fact that it is a clear thing that one mitzvah does not prevent its fellow, for both are the command of the Ribono shel Olam.

## Sicha of the night of Simchas Torah, 5713

**A.** It is known that all the matters of the month of Tishrei are general in nature; likewise, the joy of Shemini Atzeres and Simchas Torah is a general joy.

The concept here is as we find regarding the *kabbalas ol* (acceptance of the yoke) of Rosh Hashanah, which differs from the *kabbalas ol* of the entire year. The *kabbalas ol* of the rest of the year is a specific matter, whereas the *kabbalas ol* of Rosh Hashanah is the *kabbalas ol* of the *etzem hanefesh* (essence of the soul), which includes all the powers [of the soul]. The same applies to the joy of Simchas Torah: it is a general joy from the *etzem hanefesh* that includes all the powers as one.

Likewise regarding the aspect of time—this is not merely a joy belonging to this specific time, but a general joy for the entire year.

**B.** From this it is understood that the joy of Simchas Torah cannot be based on intellectual reason and understanding:

Since intellect is a specific power that is measured and limited, when joy is based on intellect, it is in accordance with the measurement and limitation of the intellect—both the measurement and limitation of the general concept of intellect, and the measurement and limitation of one’s own specific intellect, to the extent that *he* understands and *he* has a reason to rejoice. Consequently, this is not a general joy that stems from the *etzem hanefesh*, and it suffices only for this specific time, not for the entire year, for one only knows what is happening now, and it is impossible to know what will be throughout the entire year.

Therefore, the joy must be from the perspective of *kabbalas ol*—because Hashem commanded us to be in joy.

This is the reason that the joy of Simchas Torah is expressed through dancing—as explained in the *maamarim* of Chassidus that, seemingly, the joy of Simchas Torah should have been expressed through understanding and comprehension in the study of Torah. Nevertheless, the joy is expressed specifically through dancing, dancing with the feet, which is the lowest power and limb—because the joy of Simchas Torah must be specifically from *kabbalas ol*.

And although the concept of joy is an emotion of the heart, and if so, how can there be joy from *kabbalas ol* and a commandment—similar to the question regarding what is written, "And you shall love Hashem your G-d," how can there be a commandment regarding love, which is an emotion of the heart?—the matter is that when one contemplates that through this joy he is fulfilling the commandment of Hashem, *mitzvah* being from the root *tzavta* (connection) and *chibur* (union), that he is brought into *tzavta* and union with Hashem, from this itself he rejoices with great joy.

And when the joy is from the *tzavta* and union with Hashem, then the joy is without limit—for since Hashem is *Ein Sof*, the joy that comes from Him is also in a manner of without limit—which encompasses and includes all the powers and matters in the soul, and continues throughout the entire year.

**C.** Furthermore, not only can the joy not be from the intellect because it must be a general joy, a joy not connected to a specific power, but a joy without limit—but since "the mitzvah of the day is in joy", there is *no time* for intellectual contemplation.

The Rebbe Rayatz says that one must cherish the forty-eight hours of Shemini Atzeres and Simchas Torah and utilize them for joy and dancing. Consequently, it is a *waste of time* to dedicate it to intellectual contemplation, because in the meantime one is occupied with another matter that is *not* of the matter of joy. Furthermore, since the contemplation is in a manner of "weighing and arguing" whether one should be in joy or not (for in "weighing and arguing" there is room given also for the negative), it turns out that not only is he occupied in the meantime with a matter that is not of the matter of joy, but on the contrary, he is occupied with a matter that is the *opposite* of joy.

This is an additional explanation for why joy that is from intellectual contemplation is not true joy (in addition to what was stated above, that since the intellect is measured and limited, the joy is also in measurement and limitation)—for since in the matter of contemplation there are parts that build and parts that destroy, even when the conclusion of the contemplation is that one must be in joy, the room given to the negative argument remains and is felt, and consequently, this is not a complete joy.

**D.** And regarding the practical application:

One must put aside all matters of "weighing and arguing" and the calculations that a person knows in his own soul—everything has its time, and now is not the time to think about these matters—and even if from the perspective of the intellect there is no reason to be in joy, one must be in joy from the perspective of *kabbalas ol*.

And when the matter of "Let Yisrael rejoice in His Maker" will be fulfilled—then there will also be the matter of "Hashem will rejoice in His works".

And the Rebbe concluded: "Sing a happy *nigun*."

***

**E.** The order in matters of holiness is "we elevate in holiness", meaning that one must always add more, and so it is also regarding the matter of joy, that one must always add in the matter of joy.

However, there are those who are melancholic ("*mara-shechorah*-nikes") who fear that if the joy is without limit, they will not be able to add to the joy afterwards. It turns out that because of the nature of melancholy, they take the matter of joy and the obligation to add to the joy, and utilize it for the sake of a "calculation" that is the opposite of joy (limiting the joy).

Therefore:

First of all—it has already been discussed above that this is not the time to make calculations about what will be afterwards, but one must rejoice in a manner of without limit.

And furthermore—that even the aforementioned calculation is incorrect, for even in "without limit" there are differences in levels, and consequently, even when the joy is in a manner of without limit, they will always be able to add to it even more.

As is also understood from the story that the Alter Rebbe wanted to give a gift to the Tzemach Tzedek of matters of knowledge in Torah, and the Tzemach Tzedek refused to accept the gift, saying that his desire was to toil in Torah. Afterwards, he regretted that he did not take the gift, because even after receiving the gift he could have added and toiled on higher matters—even though it was certainly a most lofty gift, without limit, nevertheless, even after receiving it he could have added and toiled in higher matters, because even in "without limit" there are a multitude of levels without end.

**F.** The order of "we elevate in holiness" regarding the matter of joy is also according to *nigleh* (the revealed Torah):

First of all—the joy of the festival of Sukkos is from the commandment "And you shall rejoice in your festival", and especially according to what is written in the Yalkut, "You find three joys written regarding the festival," meaning that the joy of the festival of Sukkos is greater than the joy of the festival of Shavuos and the festival of Pesach. But nevertheless, there is a limitation in the joy of the festival, as the ruling that "the court is obligated to appoint officers during the festivals, etc."

In addition to this, there is also the joy of the second day of the festival of Sukkos, which, because it is from the words of the Scribes, there must be in it a greater joy than the first day of Sukkos, because the words of the Scribes are more beloved and dear than the words of Torah. But all of this is still within the limitation of the joy of the festival.

Higher than this is the *Simchas Beis HaShoeivah*, regarding which they said, "He who has not seen the *Simchas Beis HaShoeivah* has not seen joy in his days." However, even in this joy there were limitations, as the Rambam writes that "the common people and whoever wished did not perform it, but the great sages of Yisrael, etc., and the Chassidim and the elders and men of action were those who were dancing, etc., but all the people, etc., would come to see and hear."

And the most lofty joy is the joy of Shemini Atzeres and Simchas Torah, in which there are no distinctions between Chassidim and men of action and the rest of all the people, but all participate in the joy equally.

It should be noted that the innovation of Shemini Atzeres and Simchas Torah regarding the *Simchas Beis HaShoeivah* is not only regarding the rest of the people (who in the *Simchas Beis HaShoeivah* would only come to see and hear, and in Shemini Atzeres and Simchas Torah they were rejoicing and dancing themselves), but also regarding the Chassidim and men of action—as is seen by the senses that when there is a multitude of people who are rejoicing, then the joy is greater, because the multitude of people itself increases and multiplies the joy.

**G.** The reason for the difference in the manner of joy according to *nigleh* is in accordance with the difference from the perspective of the inwardness of the matters above—that as much as there is an addition in the quality of the *hemshechos* (drawings down) from above, so too there is an addition in the matter of joy.

By way of introduction, the difference between the time of Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur and the festival of Sukkos—that on Rosh Hashanah the matter of *aliyah* (ascent), etc., begins, and continues until after Yom Kippur, and therefore this is not a time of joy, but a time of *aliyah* and concealment. And on the festival of Sukkos the matter of *hemshech* (drawing down) begins, and therefore this is a time of *joy*, and regarding this it is written, "A wise son makes a father rejoice." And since on the festival of Sukkos itself, from time to time, there is an addition in the quality of the *hemshech*, therefore there is an addition also in the quality of the joy.

**H.** And in greater detail:

On the festival of Sukkos, the *bechinah* (aspect) of the *makif* (encompassing light) is drawn down, which is the concept of the *s'chach*. However, this is a *bechinah* of *makif* that belongs to the *p'nimi* (internal), which is why the mitzvah of Sukkah (*makif*) emphasizes the matter of knowledge (*p'nimi*), as it is written, "In order that your generations *know* that I caused the Bnei Yisrael to dwell in Sukkos," and this knowledge belongs to the fulfillment of the mitzvah itself (unlike other mitzvos where the reason for the mitzvah does not belong to the fulfillment of the mitzvah itself). For this reason they said, "A Sukkah that is higher than twenty cubits is invalid," "for the verse says 'in order that they know'... up to twenty cubits a person knows that he is dwelling in a Sukkah; above twenty cubits a person does not know that he is dwelling in a Sukkah because the eye does not dominate it".

And on the festival of Sukkos itself there are seven days, corresponding to the seven firmaments, which, although each day is connected with a higher firmament, nevertheless, in their general aspect, they are one.

After this comes Shemini Atzeres, which is the eighth to the festival of Sukkos, and together with this it is considered also a "festival in its own right". From this it is understood that its quality regarding the festival of Sukkos is not only like the quality of each day of the festival of Sukkos regarding the day before it, but there is also drawn into it a matter in its own right.

This is why on Shemini Atzeres we sit in the Sukkah, but it is not like the sitting in the Sukkah during the seven days of the festival, for which we make a blessing, and its obligation is from the Torah; whereas on Shemini Atzeres we do not make a blessing on the sitting in the Sukkah, and it is not from the Torah but from the words of the Scribes—in which is emphasized the connection of Shemini Atzeres to the festival of Sukkos, and together with this also the quality of Shemini Atzeres regarding the festival of Sukkos, which is from the perspective of the loftiness of "the words of the Scribes are more beloved and dear than the wine of Torah," and as is known regarding the matter of *sefer* (book), *sofer* (scribe), and *sippur* (story), that the matters of the Written Torah are from the *bechinah* of *sefer*, whereas the matters that are from the words of the Scribes are from the *bechinah* of *sofer*, which is higher than *sefer*.

And after this comes Simchas Torah, in which we do not sit in the Sukkah at all, meaning that it is a matter that is higher than the festival of Sukkos in a manner that is completely incomparable.

**I.** This matter should be explained according to the parable of the influence of intellect from a teacher to a student:

When the teacher influences intellect to the student—he cannot give him all the intellect as it is, but he must first *tzimtzum* (constrict) the intellect, and give him only what is according to his level. But nevertheless, in the influence of the intellect as it is according to the level of the student, the teacher also introduces the inwardness of the intellect, only that the inwardness of the intellect is found in the letters in concealment and in a *bechinah* of *makif*. Through this, the student can eventually reach also the inwardness of the intellect.

This is what our Sages said, "A person does not stand on the mind of his teacher until forty years," but after forty years "he stands on his mind"—this does not mean that after forty years the teacher must return and influence the intellect, but rather, that after he has heard from the teacher the externality of the intellect that belongs to him, and it has been received and absorbed into his inwardness through study and toil during forty years, "forty years for understanding"—then he can reach also the inwardness of the intellect (he stands on the mind of his teacher), because in the externality of the intellect, the inwardness of the intellect is found in concealment.

And in the inwardness of the intellect (which is higher than the student's comprehension and in a *bechinah* of *makif*) itself—there are two levels: (a) a lofty intellect that even though he cannot receive it in his inwardness, nevertheless, it is felt by him at least that there is here a higher matter, only that he cannot grasp it—this is the *makif* that is close; (b) an intellect that is most deep, to the extent that it is not felt by him at all—this is the *makif* that is far. The order of receiving the intellect by the student is—that first he receives the externality of the intellect and toils for forty years so that it will be absorbed into his inwardness, and during this time it is felt by him that there is a deeper matter that he does not absorb—the *makif* that is close. And after forty years—the *makif* that is close (which he felt) is drawn into his inwardness. And then he begins to feel that there is a higher *makif*—the *makif* that is far. And afterwards, the *makif* that is far is also drawn into his inwardness.

**J.** According to this, it is understood regarding the three times—Sukkos, Shemini Atzeres, and Simchas Torah:

On the festival of Sukkos—when the *hemshech* begins after the ascents of Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur—only the revelation that belongs to the created beings is drawn into the inwardness, but it is also felt that there is a higher revelation that is in the *bechinah* of the *makif* that is close, and one must toil so that this too will be drawn into the inwardness. This is the concept of "In Sukkos you shall dwell... in order that your generations know..."—that one must dwell under the *makif* that one knows about (the *makif* that is close) and toil so that it will be drawn into the inwardness—similar and analogous to the work of the student during forty years in the externality of the intellect, and in the letters in which it is felt that there is a deeper matter (the *makif* that is close) and therefore he toils to attain this. It is understood that in the work during the forty years itself there are many levels, and analogous to this is also the work during the seven days of the festival of Sukkos.

This is why we make a blessing on the mitzvah of Sukkah during the seven days of the festival of Sukkos—the concept of this blessing is in two: (a) regarding the drawing down of the *bechinah* of externality (the revelation that belongs to the created beings) into the inwardness, (b) regarding the drawing down of the *bechinah* of the *makif* that is close.

And after the work of the seven days of Sukkos—on Shemini Atzeres the *makif* that is close is drawn into the inwardness, and the *makif* that is far begins to be felt.

This is why on Shemini Atzeres we sit in the Sukkah but we do not make a blessing on the sitting in the Sukkah—because the drawing down of the *makif* that is close is done *automatically* through the work of the seven days of Sukkos, whereas a higher *makif* has not yet been drawn down.

And on Simchas Torah—the *bechinah* of the *makif* that is far is also drawn *into the inwardness*, and therefore we do not sit in the Sukkah at all.

**K.** And as stated above—from the perspective of the addition in the *hemshech* from above from time to time, there is an addition also in the matter of joy, until Simchas Torah, when the *hemshech* is the most lofty, and therefore the joy is also the greatest, without limit. ## B’s”D. Simchas Torah, 5713

(Transcript, unedited)

To understand the concept of Simchas Torah: beyond the fact that Yisroel rejoices with the Torah, there is another interpretation—that the Torah itself rejoices in the additional light drawn into it. We must understand what is added to the Torah during these days that causes the Torah itself to rejoice. Furthermore, we must understand why Simchas Torah was specifically established on Shemini Atzeres. 

Now, there are two interpretations of Shemini Atzeres: first, *atzeres* as an expression of *akavah* (retention/pausing); and second, *atzeres* as an expression of *meluchah* (royalty), as in "heir to the throne" (*yoresh atzer*), or "this shall be restrained (*yeiatzer*) among my people". We must understand the connection between *meluchah* and Shemini Atzeres. While the concept of *akavah* is clearly relevant to Shemini Atzeres—since the *hemshechos* (drawings of light) of Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, and Sukkos are absorbed into Shemini Atzeres in a manner of *pnimiyus* (inwardness)—the absorption occurs through *akavah*. Just as we see in a person below, that in order to grasp a concept of intellect so that it is internalized, one must pause and dwell upon it, so too above: the absorption of the *hemshechos* occurs through *akavah*. This is the connection of *akavah* to Shemini Atzeres. But we must still understand the connection of *meluchah* to Shemini Atzeres.

This can be understood based on what the Rebbe Rayatz said: that Shemini Atzeres and Rosh Hashanah are equivalent in many matters of *kavanos* and supernal *yichudim*, except that in Rosh Hashanah they are in the line of *yirah* and bitterness, while in Shemini Atzeres they are in the line of *simcha*. And indeed, even regarding Rosh Hashanah, it is not understood why it was established on the day of the creation of Adam HaRishon, the sixth day of creation. After all, the essence of Rosh Hashanah is "the beginning of Your works", so it would have been appropriate to establish it on the 25th of Elul, the beginning of creation. One cannot say the reason it was established on the day of Adam HaRishon’s creation is because the *avodah* of Rosh Hashanah is *kabbalas ol* (acceptance of the yoke), and *kabbalas ol* was initiated by Adam HaRishon, who said "Hashem is King, He has donned majesty", "Come, let us bow down and kneel". For the concept of *kabbalas ol* exists even among the angels, who stand in *yirah* and *kabbalas ol*, and the angels were created before Adam HaRishon—there are two opinions as to whether they were created on the second day or the fifth day, but all agree they were created before Adam. If so, why was Rosh Hashanah established specifically on the day of Adam’s creation, and not earlier?

2) To understand all this, we must first preface the well-known fact that in the general *seder hishtalshelus* there are two aspects: *sovev kol almin* and *memale kol almin*, or in other terms, *makifim* and *pnimiyim*—the matters of creation and the matters that encompass creation. The ultimate intent is that there be a *yichud* of these two, a *yichud* of *sovev* and *memale*, and in terms of the Names, the *yichud* of *Mah* and *Ban*. This is the concept of the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*, which is the preparation for the revelation of the essence of the *Ein Sof*. This is the ultimate intent, for the Holy One, blessed be He, desired to have a dwelling place in the lower worlds. 

Now, this *yichud*—the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*—is accomplished specifically through the *sefirah* of *Malchus*. It is distinct from all other *sefiros*, which were emanated to be from *ayin* to *yesh*—that is, they were emanated to be in a state of *hemshech* and revelation. This is the general concept of *Atzilus*, which is the intermediary between the *Ohr Ein Sof* of the Emanator and the created beings; through *Atzilus*, the process of *his-havus* (coming into being) occurs, and through *Atzilus*, the revelation of *Elokus* in the created beings occurs. All matters emanated in the world of *Atzilus* are for the sake of the lower realms; the emanation of the *sefirah* of *Chochma* of *Atzilus* is so that there might be knowledge and comprehension in the created beings, and so it is with the emanation of the other *sefiros*. Thus, the *sefiros* of *Atzilus* are revelations to the "other," and their purpose is to allow for the revelation of light below; therefore, through them, *his-havus* and the revelation of *Elokus* in the created beings occur. *Malchus*, however, does not effect revelation in the created beings; on the contrary, it effects a state of concealment. It is the one that effects the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*. The other *sefiros* are revelations to the "other"—they bring about *ayin* to *yesh*—but the *sefirah* of *Malchus* effects the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*.

3) The reason for the difference between the *sefirah* of *Malchus* and the other *sefiros* regarding their effect on the created beings lies in the difference within themselves. The manner of the emanation of *Malchus* is not like the emanation of the other *sefiros*. As is known, *Malchus* was emanated only as a single point, unlike the other *sefiros*; *Chochma* and *Binah* were emanated from the start as complete *eser sefiros*, and *Ze’er Anpin* was emanated in the aspect of *vav-katzavos* (six extremities), whereas *Malchus* was emanated only as a point. 

Although the *sefirah* of *Chochma* is also a point—the concept of the letter *yud* of the Name *Havayah*—the point of *Chochma* is not like the point of *Malchus*. The point of *Chochma* is a point that possesses *tziyur* (form/definition), while the point of *Malchus* is a point that possesses no *tziyur*. Now, the fact that *Chochma* is a point is due to two reasons, and in both cases, it is a point that possesses *tziyur*. The first reason is that *Chochma* comes as a point from the levels above *Chochma*; it is the lowest level of the revelations above it, and therefore it is a point of the matters above it. From this, it is understood that *Chochma* is a point that possesses *tziyur*, for the essence of this point is the end of the supernal levels above it—meaning it contains the matters above it, but in the manner of a point. Thus, it is a point that possesses *tziyur*. The second reason is that *Chochma* is the beginning of revelation, and all beginnings of revelation come specifically in the aspect of a point. From this, too, it is understood that *Chochma* is a point that possesses *tziyur*, for since it is the beginning of revelation, it contains the matters that will be revealed later, but in the manner of a point. 

However, the *sefirah* of *Malchus* is an essential point that possesses no *tziyur*; it is in a state of absence of existence and expansion, and in a state of absolute *bitul*. Even though *Malchus* is the source and beginning of *Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah*—and thus possesses *tziyur*—that is only in the aspect of *Malchus* of *Malchus*. But the essence of *Malchus* as it is in *Atzilus* is not a source for *Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah*, and it is in the aspect of a point that possesses no *tziyur*. The *sefirah* of *Malchus* is a point beneath *Yesod*, and it is in a state of ascent and *bitul* to the matters above it.

Although *Chochma* is sometimes also in the aspect of a point that possesses no *tziyur*, this is only at certain times, when it ascends to receive from the *Ohr Ein Sof* that is above *Chochma*. But in the regular course, *Chochma* is a point that possesses *tziyur*. This can be understood from what we see in a person below: when one needs to grasp a very deep intellectual concept that is not within his reach, or when a student learns from a teacher who is not within his reach, there must be a *bitul* of his intellect. When one learns an intellect that is within his reach, or a student learns from a teacher who is within his reach, he must indeed exert himself to receive the intellect, but through exertion he receives it, since it is within his reach. Not so when the intellect is not within his reach; exertion alone is not enough, but there must be a *bitul* of the intellect—both when one exerts himself on an intellect not within his reach, like "he was astonished for one hour", or "the teacher was silent", and in the case of a student learning from a teacher not within his reach, where "his lips drip with myrrh". 

This means that when the intellect one is engaged with, or the teacher one is learning from, is within his reach, he must exert himself, clear his mind of other matters, and connect his intellect to the subject at hand. But when the intellect is not within his reach, not only must he clear himself of other matters, but his intellect itself must be in a state of *bitul*, which is the concept of astonishment. Similarly, a student learning from a teacher not within his reach must have a *bitul* of his intellect (the *tziyur* of his intellect is nullified), which is like a point that possesses no *tziyur*. But this is only for an intellect not within his reach; when one needs to receive an intellect that is within his reach, even though he must exert himself, he remains in the *tziyur* of his intellect, and it is like a point that possesses *tziyur*. An analogy for this can be understood above: the fact that *Chochma* has the aspect of a point that possesses no *tziyur* is only at certain times, when it ascends to receive from the *Ohr Ein Sof* that is entirely above *Chochma*. But in the regular course, *Chochma* is a point that possesses *tziyur*. This is not the case for the *sefirah* of *Malchus*, which is always in the manner of a point that possesses no *tziyur*.

4) The matter of this is that a point that possesses no *tziyur* is in the *sefirah* of *Keter*. The *sefirah* of *Keter* is not a source for the *sefiros* that follow it, nor is it the lowest level of what is above it; rather, it is a point that possesses no *tziyur*, as the saying goes, "The supernal *Keter*... is dark, before the Cause of all causes," meaning it is in absolute *bitul*. The *bitul* of the *sefirah* of *Keter* is revealed specifically in the *sefirah* of *Malchus*, as is known, "The supernal *Keter* is the *Keter* of *Malchus*". Therefore, the *sefirah* of *Malchus* is also a point that possesses no *tziyur*, as it is in absolute *bitul*. The root of the concept of a point that possesses no *tziyur* is the point of the *reshimu* (impression) before the *Tzimtzum*. The point of the *reshimu* is not a source for *hishtalshelus*, nor is it what remains of the light before the *Tzimtzum*—for then it would be impossible for *his-havus* to come from it—but rather it is merely an impression from the *Ohr Ein Sof* before the *Tzimtzum*. Therefore, it is a point that possesses no *tziyur*, and it is in a state of *bitul* and ascent to the matters above it. This is the general difference between the *sefirah* of *Malchus* and the other *sefiros* in the manner of their emanation, and because of this, there is also a difference in their effect on the created beings. All the *sefiros* that possess *tziyur* are revelations to the "other"—they bring about *ayin* to *yesh* and reveal *Elokus* in the created beings. But the *sefirah* of *Malchus* does not effect revelation in the created beings; on the contrary, it effects concealment in them, and effects the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*. Since the *sefirah* of *Malchus* is in *bitul* and ascent to the matters above it, therefore, even *Malchus* of *Malchus* as it comes into *Beriah, Yetzirah, and Asiyah* effects concealment in the created beings, and effects in them the concept of *bitul* and ascent to the essential concealment.

5) With this, we can understand the reason why Rosh Hashanah, "this day is the beginning of Your works," was established specifically on the day of the creation of Adam HaRishon. Because the ultimate intent of creation is that which the Holy One, blessed be He, desired—to have a dwelling place in the lower worlds. The preparation for this is the *bitul* of *yesh* to *ayin*, which is accomplished through the *sefirah* of *Malchus*. Therefore, Rosh Hashanah was established on the day of the creation of Adam HaRishon, because Adam HaRishon revealed the *bitul* of the *sefirah* of *Malchus* by saying, "Hashem is King, He has donned majesty; come, let us bow down and kneel." Although the *bitul* of *kabbalas ol* also exists in the angels, the angels are in a state of "existence." Since the angels are the messengers of influence below, they are in a state of existence. Even though they stand in *bitul* with love and fear in a manner of *ratzo v'shov*, this is due to their *avodah*, but in their own essence, they are in a state of existence. Not so with *neshamos*, which are in *bitul* in their essence. As we say on Rosh Hashanah, "Whether as children or as servants"—what we say "as servants" is not a degradation (as the simple interpretation suggests), but a superiority. The *bitul* of servants is *bitul* in essence, and the concept of *meluchah* applies specifically to servants; even if one has many children, the concept of *meluchah* does not apply to them, but only to servants. Since the *bitul* of servants that effects the concept of *meluchah* exists specifically in the *neshamos* of Yisroel, therefore Rosh Hashanah was established specifically on the day of the creation of Adam HaRishon.

Now, the order of the *hemshech* of Rosh Hashanah is through *Malchiyos, Zichronos, and Shofaros*, as the saying goes: "Recite before Me *Malchiyos* so that you may crown Me over you; *Zichronos* so that your remembrance may ascend before Me for good; and with what? With the *shofar*." The matter of this is: *Malchiyos* is the *bitul* from the side of the receiver, the *bitul* of servants. *Zichronos* is the *hemshech* from the side of the Giver—meaning, in order for there to be a *hemshech* from a most exalted level where the *avodah* of the created beings does not occupy any space at all, one must effect the concept of *zichron* (remembrance), "so that your remembrance may ascend before Me." For at lower levels, even though the *avodah* of the created beings is at a lower level than them, there is no need to "mention" it. The concept of *zichronos* is specifically at a level where the matter of the created beings does not occupy any space at all. In the matter of *Shofaros*, both aspects—from the side of the Giver and from the side of the receiver—are included. In the *shofar*, there are the sounds: *tekiah, shevarim, teruah, tekiah*. The three sounds *tashrat* are the *bitul* from the side of the receiver, which is the concept of the *tekiah*—a simple cry, the cry of the heart—and *shevarim-teruah* are "groaning and wailing", which is the *bitul* from the side of the receiver. The final *tekiah* is from the side of the Giver, which is what is written, "He shall blow with a great *shofar*", and "The L-rd *Havayah* shall blow with the *shofar*", which is the concept of drawing down the simplicity of the Essence, etc. 

This is also the concept of the request for the arousal of great mercy on Rosh Hashanah, that there be a *hemshech* of the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy, of which it is written, "And *Havayah* passed before his face." Now, "faces" (*panim*) are the names that are garments for the lights, and a garment is a state of concealment over the light. Although this is not like the concealment of the *kelim*, it is still a concealment. For example, the face of a person: although there is a revelation of the light of the *nefesh* in it—unlike other limbs that conceal the light of the *nefesh*—the skin of the face still conceals the inwardness of the light of the *nefesh*. Proof of this is the flushing of the face during joy and pleasure, as "the face of Rabbi Eliezer flushed when he found a new *Tosefta*". But without the element of joy, it is not so, because the skin of the face conceals the inwardness of the light of the *nefesh*. Therefore it is written, "And *Havayah* passed before his face"—that the Thirteen Attributes of Mercy from the aspect of the inwardness of the *Ohr Ein Sof* should shine in such a way that the garments of the "face" will not conceal at all. This *hemshech* is through the blowing of the *shofar*, for the face of the blower must become red, which is the concept of the revelation of the inwardness of the vitality without the concealment of the "face."

6) With this, we can also understand the connection of the concept of *meluchah* to Shemini Atzeres. On Shemini Atzeres, the *hemshech* of Rosh Hashanah comes in *pnimiyus*. This is the concept of "on the eighth day"—*shemini* from the word *shemen* (oil). The month of Tishrei in general is the seventh month, which is "satiated with everything," blessings within it, etc., and Shemini Atzeres is the "oil" (the essence/richness) of the entire seventh month. This means that on Shemini Atzeres, all matters are drawn down in *pnimiyus*. As mentioned above, Shemini Atzeres is the same matter as Rosh Hashanah, meaning the *hemshech* of Rosh Hashanah comes on Shemini Atzeres in *pnimiyus*, which is *atzeres* from the word *akavah* and absorption. And since the *hemshech* of Rosh Hashanah is the concept of *Malchus*, as mentioned above, therefore the concept of *meluchah* is relevant to Shemini Atzeres—meaning that on Shemini Atzeres, the concept of *meluchah* is drawn down in *pnimiyus*. 

This is the connection between the two interpretations of Shemini Atzeres: *atzeres* as *akavah* and *atzeres* as *meluchah*. The concept of *meluchah* is in the *akavah* and absorption, meaning the *hemshech* of *meluchah* is in *pnimiyus*. This is also the reason why Simchas Torah was established specifically on Shemini Atzeres. Since on Shemini Atzeres the *hemshech* of the *sefirah* of *Malchus* occurs in *pnimiyus*, and the concept of *Malchus* reaches higher than *Chochma* (as mentioned above), through this, one effects additions even in the Torah. The Torah, in its own right, is the aspect of *Chochma*, for "the Torah emanates from *Chochma*". But on Shemini Atzeres, one effects in the Torah an addition of light from the aspect of the essence of the *Ein Sof*, to connect the Torah with the Holy One, blessed be He, as the saying goes, "David would connect the Torah of above with the Holy One, blessed be He." This power exists in all of Yisroel, for "all of Yisroel are children of kings"; since the *neshamos* are rooted in the Essence above the Torah, they have the power to connect the Torah with the Holy One, blessed be He. This *hemshech* is on Shemini Atzeres, because then is the *hemshech* of *Malchus* in *pnimiyus*, which reaches higher than *Chochma*. This is also the concept of Simchas Torah—that the Torah itself rejoices. For "the Torah emanates from *Chochma*," but on Shemini Atzeres, one effects in it the *yichud* with the Holy One, blessed be He: "The Torah and the Holy One, blessed be He, are entirely one".

## Sicha of Simchas Torah, 5713

**A**. The Rebbe washed his hands for the meal. Afterward, he instructed that a *nigun* be played and delivered a *maamar* on the topic of understanding the concept of Simchas Torah.

***

**B**. Shemini Atzeres is distinct from the seven days of Sukkos in several respects, summarized by the mnemonic "Pazar Kashev".

It is explained in Chassidus that the content of this mnemonic is that through "Pazar" — the scattering and breaking down of *yeshus* (selfhood) — one achieves "Kashev," the ability to listen and absorb the matters of *kedushah*. This is the essence of Shemini Atzeres: "It shall be an *atzeres* (gathering) for you", the concept of absorption.

**C**. Regarding the interpretation of the mnemonic "Pazar Kashev," the letter *Reish* stands for "*regel* (a festival) unto itself"—which Rashi explains as meaning "that one does not sit in the Sukkah."

One must understand: the fact that one does not sit in the Sukkah merely indicates that the festival of Sukkos has concluded. Seemingly, this does not point to the unique essence of the festival of Shemini Atzeres, for throughout the entire year one does not sit in the Sukkah. If so, how can it be said that the uniqueness of Shemini Atzeres as a "festival unto itself" is expressed by the fact that "one does not sit in the Sukkah"?

The explanation for this is understood through Chassidus—as discussed previously, the reason one does not sit in the Sukkah on Shemini Atzeres is that the *makif* (encompassing light) of the Sukkah is then drawn into the *pnimiyus* (internal dimension). Based on this, it is understood that "not sitting in the Sukkah" indeed points to the specific essence of the festival of Shemini Atzeres.

—This is further proof of the necessity of studying *pnimiyus haTorah* (though in truth, no proofs or evidence are needed for this). Beyond the immense value of *pnimiyus haTorah* in its own right, it is also the inner dimension and vitality of the *nigleh* (revealed) Torah, to the extent that one sees tangibly that there are many matters in the *nigleh* of Torah that cannot be understood without *pnimiyus haTorah*.

**D**. The explanation of the concept of drawing the *makif* into the *pnimiyus* (the essence of Shemini Atzeres, where one does not sit in the Sukkah) in the context of man’s *avodah*:

Our Sages said that "this world is like a corridor before the World to Come", for "one hour of spiritual pleasure in the World to Come (where they 'enjoy the radiance of the *Shechinah*') is better than all the life of this world". Thus, the revelations of the World to Come are in the category of *makif*. Based on this, the drawing of the *makif* into the *pnimiyus* is the concept of "you shall see your world in your lifetime".

Although this level pertains to the level of the Patriarchs, as our Sages said, "The Holy One, Blessed be He, gave three people a taste of the World to Come in this world: Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob"—nevertheless, our Sages said that "every single Jew is *obligated* to say: 'When will my deeds reach the deeds of my ancestors, Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob?'" Consequently, every Jew can attain this.

This is achieved through *avodah* in a manner where "all your deeds are for the sake of Heaven" and "in all your ways, know Him". For when all of one's physical affairs in this world become vessels for *Elokus*, to the point that the concept of "know Him" is achieved through them, this is the essence of the "World to Come"—"enjoying the radiance of the *Shechinah*" as it is drawn into the *pnimiyus*, which is the concept of *Da'as* ("know Him").

**E**. In order to attain this ("*m'zol haltn ba dem*"), when each person is by himself, *yeda inish b'nafshi* (a person knows his own soul, etc.). Therefore, the advice for this is to include oneself with all of *Bnei Yisrael*, for the collective is good, and through this, every individual can also reach the level of "you shall see your world in your lifetime."

This concept (the virtue of inclusion) is emphasized in the mitzvah of taking the Four Species: when the Four Species are separate, even the *esrog* (the most superior of them) is not a mitzvah, whereas when the Four Species are bound together, even the *aravah* (the lowest of them) becomes a mitzvah.

This is the order of the festivals: first comes the festival of Sukkos, and afterward Shemini Atzeres. For after fulfilling the mitzvah of the Four Species on Sukkos, the essence of which is the binding and unity of all *Bnei Yisrael* together, one can then attain the concept of Shemini Atzeres—the drawing of the *makif* into the *pnimiyus*, which is the concept of "you shall see your world in your lifetime."

***

**F**. We are still standing in the *Yom Tov* of Simchas Torah—by virtue of the concept of adding from the profane to the holy.

It should be noted that there is a special virtue in adding from the profane to the holy, not only regarding Shabbos, which is "sanctified and established", but also regarding *Yom Tov*, which depends on the sanctification of the month by the *Beis Din*, as it is written, "These are the festivals of Hashem which you shall proclaim"—it is written *atem* (you), "you, even if you err, you, even if you do so intentionally, etc.". For the *Beis Din's* determination regarding *Yom Tov* is only that Rosh Chodesh is on a certain day, but the *holiness of the Yom Tov* does not depend on them; rather, when the fifteenth day of the month arrives, it becomes *Yom Tov*. In contrast, the matter of adding from the profane to the holy is *entirely given to Bnei Yisrael*. When *Bnei Yisrael* come and say that they wish to add time from the profane to make it holy, it becomes holy—meaning, the Holy One, Blessed be He, and the Heavenly entourage decree that this time is holy. To such an extent that regarding Yom Kippur, everyone agrees "that the addition to Yom Kippur is *d'oraisa* (Torah law)".

Thus, we are in Simchas Torah, where there must be the concept of joy and dancing, and not making calculations regarding the past or the future (as discussed above). Nevertheless, the ultimate goal is *avodah* in thought, speech, and action as is fitting in daily life. On Simchas Torah itself, the order of *avodah* is through dancing with one's feet, not through Torah study, but after Simchas Torah, one must sit down to study *nigleh* and Chassidus in depth.

Accordingly, I wish to urge now—at the *conclusion* of Simchas Torah—regarding the study of a specific part of Torah that, to our sorrow, has been almost entirely neglected: the study of the *Orach Chaim* section of the *Shulchan Aruch*, as follows.

**G**. It is known what the Mitteler Rebbe wrote in his introduction to the *Shulchan Aruch* regarding the obligation upon everyone to study *Shulchan Aruch* to know the deeds they must perform, for this is the essence of study (since regarding *pilpul* and debate, one cannot know to what extent it is aligned with the truth). Especially regarding the *Orach Chaim* section, which is most relevant in practice, for often a simple question arises in *Orach Chaim*, and one does not know how to conduct oneself in practice.

Therefore, everyone must establish a fixed time every day, for at least a few minutes, to study *Shulchan Aruch Orach Chaim*, specifically the laws needed for practical application, such as the laws of interruption during *tefillah* for *Kedushah*, etc., the laws of *brachos* (blessings) on food, and the like.

The main intent here is not necessarily to study the original sources of the laws in the *Tur* and *Beis Yosef*, etc. (which someone who has time for it can do, and may his strength be fortified), but to study the *Shulchan Aruch* itself with the *Be'er Heitev* and other books that have been accepted throughout the Jewish world. From the fact that they have been accepted by *Bnei Yisrael*, it is proven that they have been accepted Above as well.

The main thing is that there should be a fixed time to study these laws every single day, for then the study of the laws one learned today will help one remember the laws learned yesterday, since all the laws of the Torah are connected and related to one another, similar to the saying of the Rebbe Rayatz in the name of the Rogatchover, that the entire Torah is one long word, beginning with the *Beis* of "*B*ereishis" and ending with the *Lamed* of "l'einei kol Yisrae*l*."

**H**. There is an additional virtue in this: that everyone together should have a fixed time to study *one subject in Torah*, the *Shulchan Aruch* section of *Orach Chaim*.

It is also worth noting what "they said about Rabbi Yehuda bar Rabbi Ilai, that six students would cover themselves with *one tallis* and engage in Torah". This is a great virtue, even compared to the generation of Moses (the generation of knowledge) and the generation of Hezekiah (which was almost the generation of Moshiach)—as our Sages expounded : "'Grace is false'—this is the generation of Moses and Joshua; 'and beauty is vain'—this is the generation of Hezekiah; 'a woman who fears Hashem, she shall be praised'—this is the generation of Rabbi Yehuda bar Rabbi Ilai." This is due to the greatness of the virtue of "one *tallis*."

The concept here :
*Tallis* (garment)—refers to the Torah, which is the garment of the Holy One, Blessed be He, as it is written, "Who wraps Himself in light as a garment."

Just as one must use a garment (*tallis*) consistently, and if one does not use it, it spoils—so too must one study Torah consistently, every day. It is obvious that this is not like a physical garment, which wears out through excessive use, for this concept does not apply to the *nimshal* (the subject matter), the garment of Torah. From this, it is understood that when everyone together has a fixed time to study one subject in Torah—in this case, the laws needed for practice (which is the essence of study) in the *Shulchan Aruch Orach Chaim*—there is in this the elevation of "*one tallis*."

**J**. When the Rebbe Rayatz began to publish and distribute *sichos* and letters in the spoken language, Yiddish, he wrote in his letter to one of the Chassidim in Eretz Yisrael that one of the reasons for this is so that the women and girls of Chabad could also read the *sichos* and letters, which contain stories of the righteous, etc. Through them, vitality will be added to the overall *avodah* of the women and girls—both regarding the study of the laws they need to know and regarding the overall matter of education that is placed in the hands of the women —as discussed above (section G), that *pnimiyus haTorah* gives vitality even to the *nigleh* of Torah.

To strengthen this, there is a need to establish an "Agudas Nashi U'Bnos Chabad", so that they may study together the laws they need, speak together about the matter of education, and study together the *sichos*, stories of the righteous, etc., through which vitality will be added both to the study of the laws and to the matter of education.

—The study of the laws, etc., is a matter that pertains to all Jewish women, both those of the Chassidic circle ("Chassidishe froyen") and those of other circles of *Bnei Yisrael* ("Olam'she"), but the *sichos*, stories of the righteous, etc., will add light and vitality to this.

**K**. The virtue of the "Agudah" is great—it strengthens all the women who belong to this organization. Furthermore, through this, they will inspire additional women to join the Agudah:

A thing done by an individual—aside from the fact that since it lacks publicity, others may not know about it—even when they do know about it, because it is done by an individual, it does not have the power to inspire others as much. In contrast, a thing done in an "Agudah" has publicity, and it has the power to inspire others, as it is the nature of people that doing something in public and in an organization inspires others to participate as well.

Especially according to what is explained in Chassidus that it is the nature of a spark to be drawn to a large torch; certainly, the virtue of the organization is great in drawing everyone to it.

**L**. The words of the righteous endure forever. Consequently, since this matter is the desire of the Rebbe Rayatz, the matter will certainly come to fruition. However, one must strive for it to come to fruition *as soon as possible*.

In our Holy Land, the Agudah was already established in practice during Chol Hamoed Sukkos (following the preparations that began even before then), and here, one should strive to establish the Agudah at least by Shabbos Bereishis, which follows and comes in the wake of Simchas Torah.

**M**. It should be added that even if at first there are only a few members in the Agudah—one should not be discouraged by this.

The truth is that the establishment of the Agudah is worthwhile even for a few members. Furthermore, when they proceed with strength and are not discouraged by the fact that there are few members, in time the Agudah will expand more, in the words of the verse, "Though your beginning was small, your end will be very great."

It is worth noting what is explained in Chassidus regarding the interpretation of the verse, "From the straits I called to Hashem; He answered me with expansiveness," in connection to the blowing of the *shofar*—that it is precisely through the call "from the straits" that "He answered me with expansiveness" is achieved.

**N**. We are now at the time when the concept of "and Jacob went on his way" begins.

The concept here:
A Jew can make a calculation and ask himself: It is true that there were all the revelations of Rosh Hashanah, Yom Kippur, Sukkos, Shemini Atzeres, and Simchas Torah, but nevertheless, what does he have—being in a standing and situation that is not as it should be—from all of this, and what does he take with him on his way?!

Similarly, when he makes a calculation—what does he take with him on his way now, after the Rebbe has passed away...

—But to this, there is instruction and empowerment from the reading of Simchas Torah in *Parshas V'zos HaBerachah*, where we conclude the Torah with the words "l'einei kol Yisrael" (in the sight of all Israel), and Rashi explains, "that his heart prompted him to break the Tablets before their eyes, etc., and the opinion of the Holy One, Blessed be He, agreed with his opinion, as it is said, 'which you broke'—may your strength be fortified for having broken them." Seemingly, it is not understood: what is the virtue of breaking the Tablets? It is explained in Chassidus that through the breaking of the Tablets, "double the wisdom" was added in the second Tablets, and the virtue of exertion in Torah was added, etc. From this, it is also understood regarding the matter of passing away, "the death of the righteous is as difficult as the breaking of the Tablets" ("as the burning of the House of our G-d", "more than the destruction of the Beis HaMikdash"), that the intent in this is for the sake of an elevation, for the Holy One, Blessed be He, does not strike and afflict to cause pain, G-d forbid, but for a benefit.

Returning to our subject—that regardless of his standing and situation, he should not fall in spirit, and he must know that he has enough strength to go on his way.

This is "*and Jacob* went on his way," specifically Jacob:
It is true that one cannot call him by the name "Israel," based on "for you have contended with G-d and with men and have prevailed", since he is not in a standing and situation where he has become the master over the nature of the world ("contended with G-d"), and he is not indifferent to people who mock ("with men").

But nevertheless, he has the virtue of "Jacob," *Yud-Ekev*, meaning, that even in the heel (*ekev*), the point of Jewishness shines. As the words of the Rebbe Rayatz in the name of our Rebbe (the Alter Rebbe), that every Jew does not want and cannot be separated from *Elokus*, to the extent that even regarding the body of a Jew it is said, "and You have chosen us". As is known the story that the Rebbe (Rashab) rolled up his sleeve and said—pointing to his arm—how precious it (the body) is, for which so much Torah and mitzvos were poured out.

With this power—the power of "Jacob," *Yud-Ekev*, the point of Jewishness that shines even in the heel—he can go on his way.

This is also why the concept of "and Jacob went on his way" comes after the joy and dancing of Simchas Torah—for in the joy and dancing of Simchas Torah, there are no distinctions between Chassidim and men of action and the rest of the people; rather, everyone is equal (as discussed above). Therefore, it is connected to and awakens the essence of the soul and the point of Jewishness in all of Israel equally. Through this power, the concept of "and Jacob went on his way" is achieved.

## B’s”D. Shabbos Parshas Bereishis, Mevarchim HaChodesh and Erev Rosh Chodesh Marcheshvan, 5713

(Unedited transcript)

*Bereishis bara Elokim es hashamayim v’es ha’aretz*. It is stated in the Targum (and similarly in the Zohar), *Bereishis bara*—"with *Chochmah* He created." For although the process of *hishtalshelus* (bringing into existence) occurs through all the *Sefiros*—which is the meaning of *Bereishis* (referring to *Keter* and *Chochmah*), *bara Elokim* (referring to *Binah*), and *es hashamayim v’es ha’aretz* (referring to *Ze’er Anpin* and *Malchus*, where *shamayim* is *Ze’er Anpin* and *aretz* is *Malchus*)—meaning that the creation is brought about by all the *Sefiros*, nevertheless, it is written *Bereishis bara*, "with *Chochmah* He created," because *Chochmah* is the beginning of the revelation, and the inception of the process of *hishtalshelus* begins from *Chochmah*.

Just as this is true regarding the creation of the world in particular—that its inception begins from *Chochmah*—so too is it true regarding the entirety of *Seder Hishtalshelus*, that its beginning is from the *Sefirah* of *Chochmah*. This is the meaning of the verse *Bereishis... es hashamayim v’es ha’aretz*, for *shamayim* and *aretz* represent the entirety of *Seder Hishtalshelus*. As the Rebbe, our master and teacher, stated in the *Hemshech* of Rosh Hashanah 5703, every world is in the category of *shamayim* relative to the world below it, and in the category of *aretz* relative to what is above it; thus, *shamayim* and *aretz* encompass the entirety of *Seder Hishtalshelus*. Regarding this it is written *Bereishis bara*, "with *Chochmah* He created," for the beginning of the creation of all the worlds is from *Chochmah*.

The verse continues, *v’ha’aretz haysa tohu vavohu*, which refers to *Olam HaTohu*. The word *haysa* ("was") implies that it already existed, referring to *Olam HaTohu*. The verse continues, *v’ruach Elokim merachefes* ("and the spirit of G-d hovered"). It is stated in the writings of the Arizal that *merachefes* refers to *rephach meis* (288 dead). *Rephach* refers to the 288 sparks of *Tohu*, and *meis* ("dead") refers to the fall of the sparks, which is the concept of the opposite of *Kedushah* (*sitra achra*).

One must understand the connection and sequence between *Bereishis bara* and what is written afterward, *v’ha’aretz haysa* etc. Furthermore, one must understand the connection between *rephach* and *meis*: *rephach* refers to the 288 sparks of *Tohu*, which are sparks of *Kedushah*, while *meis* refers to the opposite of *Kedushah*. What is their connection? In the *Midrash* it is stated that "the spirit of G-d" refers to the spirit of *Melech HaMashiach*. Accordingly, it turns out that the spirit of *Mashiach* hovers over the category of *rephach meis*. We must understand the meaning of this. We must also understand the connection of the spirit of *Mashiach* to *Bereishis bara*, that right at the beginning of creation, the text speaks of the spirit of *Mashiach*.

2) To understand all this, we must first introduce the well-known concept that the entirety of *Seder Hishtalshelus* is divided into the three categories of *Olam* (world), *Shanah* (year/time), and *Nefesh* (soul), upon which *Sefer Yetzirah* is founded. These three matters are also the three concepts of *nekudah* (point), *kav* (line), and *shetach* (surface/area). However, the order of *nekudah, kav, shetach* is from above to below, while the order of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh* is from below to above: *Olam* is the category of *shetach*, *Nefesh* is the category of *nekudah*, and *Shanah* is the category of *kav*, which connects the *nekudah* so that it may be drawn into the *shetach*.

The matter is as follows: the beginning of creation is from the *nekudah*, which is a point that possesses a "design" (*tziyur*). Proof of this is that the beginning of a *kav* is a *nekudah*; therefore, it is understood that the *nekudah* contains a design, and from it is the beginning of creation. This is the meaning of *Bereishis bara, b’chochmasa bara*—that the beginning of creation is from *Chochmah*, for *Chochmah* is a *nekudah* that possesses a design, as *Chochmah* is the beginning of revelation (meaning, it is not prior to revelation, but is the beginning of revelation). Thus, it is a *nekudah* that possesses a design, and afterward, it is drawn from it via the *kav* into the category of *shetach*—a *shetach* that bestows and a *shetach* that receives. This is why creation is from the Name *Havayah*, for the Name *Havayah* has three letters *Yud-Heh-Vav-Heh* (the *Yud-Heh* being the *Chochmah-Binah*): the *Yud* is the category of a *nekudah* possessing a design (for the form of the letter *Yud* is a point with a thorn above and a thorn below, and a point in the middle, which is a *nekudah* possessing a design); the *Vav* is the *kav* through which it is drawn into the *Heh*, which is the category of *shetach*. This means that through the *kav*, it is drawn first into the bestowing *shetach* and then into the receiving *shetach*.

3) All this can be understood from what we see in the "small world," which is man, where the connection of the *Nefesh* with the body involves the three concepts of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh*. The category of *Nefesh* is the vitality as it stands ready to animate the body (before it is enclothed in the limbs to animate them). For the *Nefesh* itself is not at all in the same category as the body to animate it; rather, it possesses a category of vitality that stands ready to animate the body. This vitality is not the same as the vitality as it is drawn into the body to become "fleshy" life; rather, it is spiritual vitality, and it is the entirety of the vitality in the category of *chai b’etzem* (life in essence).

The category of *Olam* is the vitality that is drawn into the body in its specific limbs and animates it in the category of "fleshy" life; this vitality is merely an illumination (*ha’arah*) from the *Nefesh*. The connection of the vitality with the body is the category of *Shanah*. The matter is as follows: the vitality of the *Nefesh* unites with the body in such a way that the body becomes a special garment for the *Nefesh*, meaning that it is impossible to separate between the body and the vitality. The vitality with which the *Nefesh* animates the body is such that the body itself becomes alive. This is not in a way where the body remains in its existence as it was, and the *Nefesh* merely moves it (like a puppet), but rather the body itself becomes alive (as explained in the *Maamar* of the Rebbe, our master and teacher).

From this it is understood that although the category of vitality enclothed in the body is merely an illumination, it is nevertheless in *dveikus* (attachment) with the entirety of the vitality, which is the category of *chai b’etzem*. For the power to animate the body so that the body itself becomes alive comes from the category of *chai b’etzem*; because it is *chai b’etzem*, it is alive to animate so that the body itself becomes alive. Since the vitality enclothed in the body animates the body in its interiority so that the body itself becomes alive, it is understood that even though the vitality is merely an illumination from the *Nefesh*, it is nevertheless in *dveikus* with the category of *chai b’etzem*.

However, this *dveikus* is an imperceptible *dveikus*, for the vitality is "carried" (*zugetragen*) to the body to animate it in all its limbs—meaning the vitality is not in *dveikus* to the *Nefesh*, but is "carried" to the body to animate it. Thus, the *dveikus* of the vitality enclothed in the body—the category of *Olam*—with the category of *chai b’etzem*—the category of *Nefesh*—is in the manner of *ratzo v’shov* (running and returning). The power to animate the body in its interiority is from its *dveikus* in the category of *chai b’etzem*—this is the category of *ratzo*. But at the same time, the vitality is in the category of *shov*, for it animates the body, meaning it does not transform the body so that it ceases to be a body, but rather it animates the body as it is in its existence; thus, it is in the category of *shov*.

Now, just as the manner of vitality is specifically in the category of *ratzo v’shov*, so too is it regarding the essence of the vitality; the very connection of body and *Nefesh* is in the category of *ratzo v’shov*. For through their connection, the *Nefesh* and the body do not change from their essence; the body remains physical and the *Nefesh* remains spiritual. The connection is that the *Nefesh*, as it is spiritual, is connected with the body, which is physical. Since physicality and spirituality are opposites, it is understood that the connection is specifically through *ratzo v’shov*. Thus, both from the perspective of the essence of the vitality and from the perspective of the manner of the vitality, it is in the category of *ratzo v’shov*, and this is the concept of *Shanah*, as it is known that the root of time is the category of *ratzo v’shov*.

Just as it is in this "small world," man, so too is it in the "great world," which also contains the three categories of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh*. The created being is in the category of *Olam*, from the word *he'elem* (concealment). Similarly, the Divine vitality enclothed in the created being is also called *Olam*, from the word *he'elem*, because it is constantly "striving" in the *yesh* (the created entity) to bring it into existence, and its *dveikus* in its source is not apparent within it. The process of *hishtalshelus* is through the power of the *Atzmus* (Essence), whose existence is from His Essence, and He alone has the power to bring *yesh* from *ayin*. The power of creation that exists in the Divine *Ayin* to create the *yesh* is from its *dveikus* in its source, but nevertheless, the *dveikus* in its source is not apparent within it. On the contrary, it is "carried" (*zugetragen*) to create the *yesh*, constantly striving to bring it into existence, as it is written, "He who renews in His goodness every day, constantly, the work of creation." Therefore, it is called *Olam*, from the word *he'elem*, because it conceals its source.

Like the vitality of the *Nefesh* that animates the body—that although the fact that it animates the body is from its *dveikus* in its source, which is *chai b’etzem* (as mentioned above), nevertheless the *dveikus* is not felt within it, for it is enclothed in the body—so too is it with the Divine vitality that creates the created being. Although the fact that it creates the created being is specifically from its source, nevertheless its source is not felt within it, and it conceals its source. It is specifically through this concealment of its source that it creates the *yesh*; therefore, it is called *Olam* (concealment). This is the category of *Olam*, which is the category of *shetach*, encompassing the created beings and the Divine *Ayin* that creates them. *Nefesh* is the category of *nekudah*, which is the point of *Chochmah*, from which is the root of creation, for in *Chochmah* there is the power of the *Atzmus*. The connection of the category of *Olam* with the category of *Nefesh* is through *ratzo v’shov*, which is the category of *Shanah*, for the power that exists in the *Ayin* to create the *yesh* is specifically from its *dveikus* in its source, but the *dveikus* is not apparent. This means the *dveikus* is in the category of *ratzo v’shov*, which is the concept of *Shanah*, for the creation of time is from *ratzo v’shov*.

4) Since the intent of creation is because the Holy One, blessed be He, desired to have a dwelling place below, and this dwelling place is created through the *avodah* (service) of the souls of Israel, it must be that in the manner of the *avodah* of the souls of Israel, there are also the three concepts of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh*. The matter is as follows: the purpose of *avodah* is the study of Torah and the performance of *mitzvos* in practice, which is the category of *ma'aseh* (action). However, in order for there to be vitality in the performance of *mitzvos*, this is through the *kavanah* (intent) of the *mitzvos*, which is the category of *machshavah* (thought), as the saying goes, "A *mitzvah* without *kavanah* is like a body without a soul."

The *kavanah* here does not refer to the specific *kavanos* of each *mitzvah*, but to the general *kavanah* in all *mitzvos*, which is the concept of *ahavah* (love) and *yirah* (fear/awe). Through the arousal of *ahavah* and *yirah*, the performance of the *mitzvah* is with will, desire, and great vitality. Then, a person is meticulous to perform every *mitzvah* in all its details and nuances, and also *mehadar* (beautifies) the *mitzvah*. For when one performs the *mitzvah* without vitality, the performance is such that one is not meticulous or *mehadar* in it; but when one has vitality in the *mitzvah*, one performs the *mitzvah* in all its details and nuances and *mehadar* in it.

This is the precision of the language written by our Rebbe in *Tanya* : "And he who performs them in truth is he who loves the Name *Havayah* and desires to cleave to Him, etc." This means that the performance of *mitzvos* can also be from *kabbalas ol* (acceptance of the yoke) without *ahavah* and *yirah*, but for it to be a true performance—meaning that he has vitality in it and that the performance is with meticulousness and *hiddur*—this is specifically through *ahavah* and *yirah*.

However, although *ahavah* and *yirah* are the vitality of the *mitzvos*, there must also be a connection of the *ahavah* and *yirah* (the vitality of the *mitzvos*) with the *mitzvos* in practice. That is, to "carry" (*zugetragen*) the *ahavah* and *yirah* to the performance of the *mitzvos*. For it is possible that even if one has *ahavah* and *yirah* during *davening*, after *davening* the performance of the *mitzvos* will not be as it should be. The reason for this is that there was no connection of the *ahavah* and *yirah* with the performance of the *mitzvos* in practice—meaning the *ahavah* and *yirah* were from the *Neshamah* and did not have a connection and bond with the *Nefesh HaBahamis*. Since during *davening* the *ahavah* and *yirah* were not connected with the *Nefesh HaBahamis*, therefore, after *davening*, when he occupies himself with the body and the *Nefesh HaBahamis*, no impression of the *ahavah* and *yirah* remains.

For the *Neshamah* above perceives *Elokus* (Divinity) and stands in a movement of *ahavah* and *yirah*. Standing above in *ahavah* and *yirah* is from the perception of *Elokus* (for even the angels above perceive *Elokus* and stand in *ahavah* and *yirah*, and all the more so the souls). Nevertheless, when the soul descends below, the body and the *Nefesh HaBahamis* conceal and hide the *ahavah* and *yirah* of the *Neshamah*. This is also true in *avodah*: when the *ahavah* and *yirah* are from the *Neshamah*, after *davening* it passes and fades. Therefore, there must be a connection of the *ahavah* and *yirah* with the *Nefesh HaBahamis*, and this connection is during *davening*.

This is the concept of *nekudah, kav, shetach* in *avodah*. *Nekudah* is the *kavanah* of the *mitzvos*, *ahavah* and *yirah*, which is *machshavah*. *Shetach* is the performance of the *mitzvos* in practice, which is *ma'aseh*. And the *kav* is the connection through the *avodah* of *davening*, which is *dibbur* (speech). However, the connection is by way of *ratzo v’shov*, for during the performance of the *mitzvah*, a person must be "carried" (*zugetragen*) to the performance of the *mitzvah* and not to the *kavanah*, as explained in *Tanya* regarding the matter of *kavanah* of *lishmah* in Torah study, which must be at the time of beginning the study and at the beginning of every hour when the combinations change, but afterward (during the study) one must be "carried" to the study. So too is it in the performance of *mitzvos*: during the performance of the *mitzvah*, one must be "carried" to the performance of the *mitzvah*, but nevertheless, even then the *kavanah* must be felt, at least in the way of a *makif* (encompassing light). For specifically through the feeling of the *kavanah* is it possible for the performance of the *mitzvah* to be in truth, and this is why the connection is specifically through *ratzo v’shov*.

Just as it is in the general *avodah* of the performance of *mitzvos*, so too is it in the *kavanah* of the *mitzvos* (which is the vitality of the *mitzvos*) itself, which also contains the three concepts of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh*. That is, in the love of Hashem, which is the essence of *avodah*—for "there is no service like the service of love" —there are the three aforementioned concepts. When one contemplates *Elokus* and arrives at the realization that *Elokus* is good for him, then he is aroused in love for *Havayah*. This love is called *Olam*, for *Olam* is the category of *metzius* (existence/reality), and this love is also in the category of *metzius*, because it comes from the feeling that *Elokus* is good for him.

The category of *Nefesh* in love is when the love is not in the category of *metzius*, and it is from the category of the source of the intellect, which is above the intellect that relates to the *Midos* (emotions). This means that not only is his love for *Havayah* not because it is good for him (where his *metzius* would be felt), but furthermore, the love itself is not in feeling; he does not feel at all that he loves, meaning he is in a state of complete *bitul* (nullification). This is one of the explanations of what Rabbi Yochanan ben Zakkai said, "I do not know on which path they are leading me". Although he possessed all the virtues that were said about him, nevertheless, because he was in a state of complete *bitul*—not only did he not feel his own existence, but he did not even feel the love—therefore he said, "I do not know on which path they are leading me."

However, because the intent is that there be love in revelation (for specifically then can the love be in a way that it gives vitality to the *mitzvos*), therefore there must be a connection so that it is drawn from the category of the source of the intellect to be love in revelation. The connection is through the category of intellectual *hispaalus* (arousal), which is the intermediary between the two levels. Since the *hispaalus* is over the goodness of *Elokus* in essence and not over the fact that it is good for him, this relates to the category of the source of the intellect (*Nefesh*). But because this is the concept of *hispaalus*, it relates to the category of love in revelation (*Olam*). This is the category of *Shanah*, through which is the connection that it be drawn from the source of the intellect to be love in revelation.

Through the drawing from the category of the source of the intellect to the category of love in revelation, the love in revelation also becomes in a way of *bitul*. For when the love is in feeling, even though it is in *Kedushah*, nevertheless, through the multiplicity of *hishtalshelus*, it is possible for there to be "nursing" for the *chitzonim* (external forces) from it. As it is in *avodah*, so too is it in the entirety of *Seder Hishtalshelus*. Therefore, even though the celestial spheres recite song and are in *bitul*, as it is written, "And the host of heaven bows to You" (which refers to their rotation toward the west, because the *Shechinah* is in the west), and the *bitul* is from their perception of *Elokus*, as the Rambam writes that they recognize their Creator—nevertheless, there are people who bow to the sun, etc. This is because the *bitul* of the spheres is in feeling, and therefore, through the multiplicity of *hishtalshelus*, it is possible for there to be an entirely opposite matter from it, which is idolatry.

An analogy for this: when a servant stands before the king and is in a state of complete *bitul*, he cannot bear to be honored. But when he can bear to be honored, this indicates that he is not in a state of complete *bitul*. So too in the analogy of the spheres: although they are nullified to *Elokus*, nevertheless, because they are not in a state of complete *bitul*, through the multiplicity of *hishtalshelus*, it is possible for there to be an entirely opposite matter from it.

5) This is *Bereishis bara*, etc. The word *Olam* has three meanings, and it can be said that they are the three concepts of *Olam, Shanah, Nefesh*. The meaning of *Olam* common in the words of our Sages is "place." The meaning of *Olam* common in the *Tanach* is "time," as it is written, *E-l Olam* (G-d of Time/Eternity). The third meaning of *Olam* is as our Sages said, "I was a youth and I have also aged" —the Prince of the World says this. This is the category of *Nefesh*, meaning the vitality of the worlds, which contains the category of "youth," meaning the vitality is in *tzimtzum* (contraction), and there is the category of "aged," which is in greater revelation.

This is the difference between *chol* (weekday) and *Shabbos*. On *chol*, the drawing of vitality is from the category of "youth," which is in the category of measure and boundary. On *Shabbos*, when there is an ascent of the worlds, it is said "I have also aged", for the category of *mochin d'gadlus* (mature intellect) is drawn into it from the category of *Ein Sof, Baruch Hu* (though the ascent on *Shabbos*, i.e., the receiving of *mochin d'gadlus*, is through the *avodah* during the six days of *chol*, as the saying goes, "He who toils on Erev Shabbos will eat on Shabbos"). This is why Yonasan and Dovid, when they went out to the field, took a youth with them. Yonasan is the category of *Ze’er Anpin*, and Dovid is the category of *Malchus*. When they went out to the field—to arrange the *hishtalshelus*—they took a youth with them, who is the intermediary that contracts the vitality so that it can be drawn into the worlds.

Since the drawing is through *tzimtzum*, through the multiplicity of *hishtalshelus*, it is possible for there to be "nursing" for the *chitzonim* from it. This is the connection of what is written, *v’ha’aretz haysa* etc., *v’ruach Elokim merachefes*, to what is written before it, *Bereishis bara* etc. Right at the beginning of creation, at the end of the levels of *Kedushah*, it is possible that through the multiplicity of *hishtalshelus*, there will be "nursing" for the *chitzonim* from it, for since it is only in the category of *bitul* of the *yesh*, it is possible for there to be a matter of the opposite of *Kedushah* from it. This is also the connection of *rephach* to *meis*; although they are 288 sparks of *Kedushah*, nevertheless, it is possible for a matter of death to emerge from it, which is the concept of the opposite of *Kedushah*.

Regarding this it is written, *v’ruach Elokim* etc., and our Sages said this is the spirit of *Mashiach*. The spirit of *Mashiach* is the category of the interiority of *Atik*, and it is the concept of drawing the *He'elem Atzmi* (Essential Concealment) into all the levels, where it is impossible for there to be "nursing" for the *chitzonim* from it. Just as in *avodah* there must be the connection of the category of love in revelation with the category of the source of the intellect—meaning the drawing of *bitul* into the love as it comes in revelation (as mentioned above in section 4)—so too is it in *hishtalshelus*: there must be the drawing of the *He'elem Atzmi* into all the levels, where it is impossible for there to be any "nursing," and through this, the intent is completed—that the Holy One, blessed be He, desired to have a dwelling place below.

## Sicha of Shabbos Bereishis, Mevarchim Chodesh Mar-Cheshvan, 5713

**A.** Maamar on the verse "In the beginning G-d created..."

**B.** It is a well-known adage that the Rebbe Rayatz related in the name of the Tzemach Tzedek: "As one stands on Shabbos Bereishis, so it continues for the entire year."

Since this has been told and revealed to us, it applies to us as well.
Therefore: The choice is ours to draw down joy for the entire year through the joy we experience now, on Shabbos Bereishis.
– The essence of Shabbos is indeed *taanug* (delight), as is known regarding the distinction between Shabbos and Yom Tov; the essence of Yom Tov is *simcha* (joy) ("festivals for joy"), while the essence of Shabbos is *taanug*. Nevertheless, even on Shabbos there is an element of *simcha*, except that the *simcha* is included within the *taanug*.
(The Rebbe concluded:) May Hashem help that the *simcha* of Shabbos Bereishis should illuminate all the Shabbosos of the year, and from these Shabbosos—from which all the days are blessed—may *simcha* be drawn down into the weekdays of the entire year.
The Rebbe instructed that a *nigun* of *simcha* be played (saying: "Sing a joyous *nigun*").

**C.** It is known that the *avodah* of the entire year in the manner of "And Yaakov went on his way" begins on Shabbos Bereishis, when we bless the month of Mar-Cheshvan, standing at the conclusion of the month of Tishrei, regarding which it is said in the Midrash, "Tishrei, you shall leave and you shall atone..."
– (The Rebbe smiled and said:) At the beginning of the Tzemach Tzedek’s leadership, when the Chassidim were not yet accustomed to the style of his *maamarim*, which frequently cited Chazal (unlike the style of the Mitteler Rebbe’s *maamarim* which they were used to hearing previously), which made the *chazara* (review) of the *maamarim* difficult for them—the Tzemach Tzedek once mentioned the aforementioned phrase, "Tishrei, you shall leave..." and during the *chazara*, the *chozrim* (those reviewing) debated the interpretation and source of this phrase.
The Rebbe Maharash was then a small child of about five or six years old, and was already well-versed in the Midrashim of Chazal. When he heard their discussion, he responded that this is the language of the Midrash. When they asked him where this Midrash was—after they agreed to give him what he requested for it, which was a certain toy...—he showed them the words of the Midrash.
(The Rebbe added:) By telling this story, I intend to mention in the meantime the names of our Rebbes, the Nesi'im: the Mitteler Rebbe, the Tzemach Tzedek, and the Rebbe Maharash!
From this it is understood that in Parshas Bereishis, which we read when we begin the *avodah* of "And Yaakov went on his way," there is a directive connected and relevant to the beginning of the *avodah*, as follows.

**D.** At the beginning of the Parsha it is said: "And the earth was *tohu* and *bohu*, and darkness was upon the face of the deep, and the spirit of G-d hovered upon the face of the waters."
Regarding these four terms—*tohu*, *bohu*, darkness, and the deep—it is stated in the *sefarim* that they correspond to the four *kelipos*: the three completely impure *kelipos*, and *kelipas nogah*.
In contrast—regarding "the spirit of G-d hovered upon the face of the waters," Chazal said, "This is the spirit of Melech HaMashiach," which is an extremely lofty revelation, a revelation of the *Atzmus*.
Seemingly, this is very puzzling: How is it possible that when speaking about *kelipos*, one mentions in the same breath the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach"?!
The explanation for this is that the intent of the verse is to emphasize that regardless of the magnitude of the darkness from the *kelipos* in general, and specifically from the three completely impure *kelipos*, one must know that the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach" already exists.
– Similar to what we find in the Midrashim of Chazal regarding the destruction of the Beis HaMikdash, that immediately the redeemer of Israel was born—
It is just that we must *reveal* it.
This matter is also emphasized in the continuation of the verses: "And G-d said, 'Let there be light,' and there was light," where "light" denotes the matter of revelation, and specifically—the revelation of the light that was created on the first day, which the Holy One, Blessed be He, hid away for the time to come, which is the content of the revelation of the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach."
This revelation is accomplished through Torah—as is understood from the words of the Midrash, "'Let there be light'—this is Avraham," with whom the "two thousand years of *Torah*" begin, and through him (i.e., through the essence of Avraham, which is Torah), the revelation of the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach" is brought about.

**E.** The directive from this for the *avodah* of man:
There are those who fear the magnitude of the darkness and argue that in a state where darkness is increasing, it is impossible to begin with a great light, but only with a limited light, proportional to the time and place, etc.
To this comes the directive from the beginning of the reading of Parshas Bereishis—that even though one is at the *beginning* of the *avodah* (the month of Tishrei has just ended and the *avodah* of the entire year is beginning), and furthermore, *tohu, bohu, darkness, and the deep* prevail, nevertheless, we begin immediately with the "*spirit of Melech HaMashiach*"—the most lofty light, the inwardness of the *Shechinah* which is above and distinct from the worlds, which the worlds cannot receive even as an *makif* (encompassing light)—the parallel to which in the human soul is the inwardness of the heart.
And immediately we continue with the declaration "*Let there be light*," meaning, without taking into account the status and condition of the world, whether it is worthy of this or not, we declare, "'Let there be light'—this is Avraham," and we begin to illuminate the world with Divine light, just as Avraham publicized the Divinity of the Almighty in the world even to the Arabs, without considering whether they were worthy of it or not.
And then—"*and there was light*," that it actually happens, "it becomes light in actuality," reaching the revelation of the greatest light—the light that shone on the first day.

**F.** Based on what was said above (section D) that the revelation of the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach" is accomplished through the "two thousand years of Torah," which begin with Avraham, of whom it is said, "'Let there be light'—this is Avraham"—we must explain the aforementioned directive in greater detail regarding *Torah*:
In Torah there is *nigleh d'Torah* (the revealed Torah) and *pnimiyus haTorah* (the inner Torah): *nigleh d'Torah* is clothed in human intellect and speaks of physical matters whose substance we grasp; *pnimiyus haTorah*, however, speaks of matters whose existence we only grasp, and its totality is above human intellect.
When one goes out to illuminate the world with the light of Torah, there are those who argue that one should not begin immediately with *pnimiyus haTorah*, since one must proceed in an orderly and gradual fashion.
To this comes the directive from the beginning of Parshas Bereishis—that at the beginning of the *avodah*, and in a state of the greatest darkness, one begins immediately with the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach," whose content in Torah is *pnimiyus haTorah*.
This is also the essence of Avraham—"'Let there be light'—this is Avraham"—*Av Ram* (Exalted Father), that which is hidden from all thought, meaning that in Torah itself, this is above human intellect—*pnimiyus haTorah*. Furthermore: "Avraham" is written with a *Mem Stuma* (closed Mem), which relates to the "closed *maamar*" (discourse), which is the matter of *pnimiyus haTorah*, the *satim* (hidden) of Torah.
And when one is not affected by the darkness of the world, but declares "Let there be light," and illuminates the world with the light of *pnimiyus haTorah*—then "and there was light," that it happens in actuality, that the light of *pnimiyus haTorah* illuminates the entire world.
And through this, one merits the revelation of the "spirit of Melech HaMashiach" with the coming of Mashiach *in actual reality*, because, "when your springs spread outward," this is the Master, Melech HaMashiach, which is the perfection of the intent of creation: for the Almighty to have a dwelling place in the lower worlds.

**G.** In the recent period, the founding of *Es-Ha* (Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos—Association of Yeshiva Students) has come to fruition.
This is the place to explain the content of the name of the association—"*Es-Ha*"—a name given at the time by the Rebbe Rayatz.
By way of introduction—we see that in determining the names for his holy institutions, the Rebbe Rayatz was precise that even the *acronym* of the institution's name should have content and meaning corresponding to the nature of that institution.
For example: in the institutions "Karni Hod Torah" and "Merkaz L'Inyanei Chinuch," whose acronyms are "KeHaT" and "MaLaCh."
Furthermore—not only in names in the Holy Tongue, but even in names in the language of the country, the Rebbe Rayatz was precise that the acronym should have content and meaning in the Holy Tongue.
For example—in the institution whose name in the language of the country is such that its acronym is "*Kos*" (cup):
"*Kos*"—even though its meaning is concealment (from the word *kisui*—covering), nevertheless, it is said of it, "I will lift the cup of salvations", and as is known the story-adage that the cup of King David—the Book of Psalms—lifts up above and draws down below, etc., which is the content of the matter of "I will lift the cup of salvations."
This is the reason that the Rebbe Rayatz chose this name whose acronym is "*Kos*"—to indicate (in addition to the function of the thing) that this institution is *his cup* ("His—the Rebbe's—cup"), and consequently, it is the cup of *King David*,
– for our Rebbes, the Nesi'im, belong to King David, both from the aspect of the matter of *nesi'us* (leadership) in general which belongs to King David, and from the fact that the Alter Rebbe is of the seed of King David, and therefore our Rebbes have a spark of King David, and they are his successors—
And consequently, it will be in the manner of "*I will lift the cup of salvations*," through which blessing will be drawn down without limit both in spirituality and in physicality, meaning that even the drawing down into physicality is without limit, as explained in Chassidus regarding the interpretation of the saying of our Sages, "A vessel—a cup—that is empty holds; one that is full does not hold," that when it is an "empty vessel," it holds even physicality without limit, since physicality is like spirituality in that it does not occupy space.
From this it is also understood regarding the institution "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos," whose acronym is "*Es-Ha*" (You), as follows.

**H.** "*Es-Ha*" (You):
It is known that "You" (and similarly "This") refers to His *Atzmus* (Essence), because "You" (*Du*) in the second person can only be said of the *Atzmus*, for everything that is in the realm of revelation, like light, and everything that is outside the *Atzmus*—one cannot say "You" to it in the second person, since it is not present everywhere, and only of the *Atzmus* can one say "You" in the second person, since He is present everywhere, and in the well-known expression, "The Name of Heaven is common in everyone's mouth."
This is the connection of "You" to this association—that is why the Rebbe Rayatz called this association by a name whose acronym is "You"—because this association is a vessel for the drawing down and revelation of the *Atzmus* ("You") below, which is the goal of the founding of this association.

**I.** The matter of this:
In *Hilchos De'os*, the Rambam writes: "Just as the wise man is recognized by his wisdom and his opinions... so too he must be recognized by his deeds, his eating, and his drinking, etc. How so? A Torah scholar, etc."
Now, the name "wise man" (*chacham*) is on account of the *bitul* (nullification) of *Chochma* that is within him, for the essence of *Chochma* is *bitul*, in which it is recognized that "The *Ein Sof*, Blessed be He, is the one Truth, that He alone is, and there is nothing else," as the Alter Rebbe writes in *Tanya* in the name of his teacher, the Maggid, that "this is the level of *Chochma*." Therefore, one who possesses the *bitul* of *Chochma* (for which he is called a "wise man"), his conduct is in a manner where it is recognized in all his affairs that the Holy One, Blessed be He, "is He alone, and there is nothing else."
It turns out that the essence of the wise man is the drawing down and revelation of the *Atzmus* in the world, so that it will be recognized in all the affairs of the world that "He alone is, and there is nothing else." And this matter applies even to one who has not yet reached the level of the *bitul* of *Chochma*, but is only a "*talmid* chacham" (student of a wise man)—that his conduct, too, must be in a manner where it is recognized in all his affairs (as the Rambam writes), meaning that he, too, must effect the drawing down and revelation of the *Atzmus* in the world.
This is the goal of the founding of "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos"—whose acronym is "*Es-Ha*"—that the yeshiva students, who are *talmidei chachamim* whom the Rambam says must be recognized in all their affairs, should unite together in everything related to fulfilling their role to effect the drawing down and revelation of the *Atzmus* below.

**J.** And in greater detail:
The members of "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos" are required (in addition to their primary *avodah* as yeshiva students in studying Torah with diligence and persistence) both in matters regarding their own *avodah*,
– among them: (a) giving *tzedakah* every day before *davening*, through which clarity and lucidity ("*klorkeit*") is created in all matters, as the Rebbe (Rashab) explains in one of his *maamarim* the interpretation of "He gives a *pruta* to a pauper and then *matzli* (prays)", "*matzli*" from the word *tzalul* (clear), "*klar*"; (b) studying the daily *shiurim* that are for everyone in Chumash, Tehillim, and Tanya, which, in addition to the general obligation in this, there is an additional obligation on the members of the association specifically; (c) studying every day the *halachos* necessary for practice in *Shulchan Aruch, Orach Chaim*—
And both in matters regarding the activity with others, both regarding yeshiva students, and regarding strengthening the matters of Torah and *mitzvos* in general, even regarding simple matters.
Similar to what we find in the conduct of the Rebbe Rayatz (and our Rebbes in general) who demanded two types of matters that are opposites: on one hand, he demanded that they engage in the dissemination of Judaism even regarding simple matters, for example, in proper education: teaching the *Aleph-Beis* and the like. And on the other hand, he would say and publish even difficult Chassidic *maamarim* ("*shverre maamarim*") that are not understood by everyone, and he demanded that they study and understand even such *maamarim*, and furthermore he demanded that even those who until recently had to be dealt with regarding simple matters, and who had only just been brought close to the matters of Torah and *mitzvos*—they too should study and understand these *maamarim*.
And this matter, that they demand from the members of "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos" two types of matters, both regarding themselves and regarding others—relates to the acronym "*Es-Ha*" which refers to the *Atzmus*:
The reason that "You" refers to the *Atzmus* is, as mentioned, because the *Atzmus* is present everywhere, "The Name of Heaven is common in everyone's mouth." It turns out that "You" points to two extremes: on one hand—His *Atzmus*, which is completely above the realm of revelation and concealment, etc., and on the other hand—from this very aspect, "The Name of Heaven is common in everyone's mouth," that He is present everywhere.
And in accordance with these two extremes, they demand from the members of "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos"—acronym "*Es-Ha*," which is the vessel for drawing down the *Atzmus*—two extremes: matters regarding themselves—like the fact that the *Atzmus* is above all definition, etc., and matters regarding others—like the fact that the *Atzmus* is present everywhere.

**K.** And this is the place to urge that upon each and every member of "Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos" lies the obligation to bring an additional member to *Es-Ha* by the upcoming 19th of Kislev.
– This was also spoken about at *Simchas Beis HaShoeiva*, but without a time limit, and now a time limit has been added until the 19th of Kislev.
And it should be noted that before the 19th of Kislev there are three months of preparation: the first month—from the 18th of Elul until *Hoshana Rabbah*; the second month—until the 20th of Mar-Cheshvan; and the third month—until the 19th of Kislev.
In the present case: the *farbrengen* of *Simchas Beis HaShoeiva* (where this was spoken about) took place on the 19th of Tishrei, and from then until the 19th of Kislev there are two months.

**L.** (The Rebbe concluded:) May Hashem grant that the members of Igud Talmidei HaYeshivos fulfill the intent of the association—the drawing down of the *Atzmus* below, which is the intent of a dwelling place in the lower worlds, and may they do so with *simcha*.
The Rebbe instructed that the members of *Es-Ha* play a *nigun* (saying: "Let '*Es-Ha*' sing a *nigun*").

**M.** In *Torah Or*, Parshas Noach, it is explained that the Flood was not only a punishment, but also an act of purification (cleansing)—the Flood purified the world, for before the Flood it was not pure. Therefore, the Flood was forty days—corresponding to the forty *se'ah* of a *mikveh*. Therefore, the Flood is called by the name "waters of Noach"—*niyacha d'rucha* (a spirit of calm/satisfaction).
And so—as explained in *Torah Or* there in detail—it is also regarding the worries and burdens of livelihood, which are called by the name "many waters," which wash away and remove from man the undesirable matters, and thereby also effect a "*niyacha d'rucha*."

**N.** Explanation of the connection between the worries of livelihood, the Flood, and the *mikveh*:
The inner essence of a *mikveh* is "*bitul*," as the words of the Rambam say, "that he shall immerse in the waters of pure knowledge." Therefore, the *mikveh* must hold forty *se'ah*, which is the amount "in which his entire body ascends", meaning that they encompass the entire body of man, and "*tevilah*" (immersion) is the letters of "*ha-bitul*" (the nullification), meaning that the *bitul* that man exits from his "*Ani*" (ego) effects in him an ascent (elevation), that he becomes a vessel for holiness.
This is also the true intent in that man is given the worries of livelihood. Although temporarily it disturbs and confuses him, through this he is broken and exits from his private "*Ani*"—and thus he becomes a vessel for holiness.

**O.** And since the true intent is not to punish him, G-d forbid, but in order to wash and cleanse from him the undesirable matters—it is understood that the matter can be accomplished even in one moment—if within one moment he extracts the inner intent within this, and effects in himself what the matter is intended to effect—then he has already fulfilled his obligation, and the "*niyacha*" can already exist for him both in physicality and in spirituality.

**P.** (Towards the end of the *farbrengen*, the Rebbe said:)
It is known that the time of *Mincha* on Shabbos is an "*eis ratzon*" (time of favor) both above and below, which is why we say then, "And as for me, my prayer is to You, Hashem, at a time of favor, etc.".
And as we stand at this time of favor—may Hashem grant that in addition to the drawing down of all the matters that were effected through the *avodah* of each and every one according to his strength, there should also be drawn down the matters that could and should have been added and completed,
– "what was done, what was not done," this does not matter at an "*eis ratzon*"... –
And may they be drawn down below ten *tefachim*, with everything necessary for each and every one in children, life, and ample sustenance,
And may there also be drawn down a blessing of success in all matters of the *avodah* of studying Torah and keeping *mitzvos*, and primarily—in the dissemination of the springs outward,
And may the Rebbe lead us towards the complete Redemption through Mashiach Tzidkeinu, speedily in our days.
